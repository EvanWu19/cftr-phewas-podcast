How did this episode land for you?

One-paragraph blurb

Uden and colleagues from the Hannover and Berlin CF groups replicated the previously-reported European-CF-Twin-and-Sibling-Study finding that the microsatellite D12S889 in intron 1 of the tumour-necrosis-factor-receptor gene TNFRSF1A — TNFR1 — modifies CFTR function, extended the readout from disease severity to actual patient survival in two cohorts, and proposed a molecular intermediate: the alternative TNFR1 transcript TNFR1delEx2, which lacks exon 2 and is regulated by the epithelial splicing-regulator protein ESRP2. F508del homozygotes born between 1975 and 1985 with the severe D12S889-13/13 genotype had median survival of twenty-four years versus forty-two years for D12S889-10/10 homozygotes — log-rank P equals 0.002. The proposed direction is to add the Uden six-marker TNFR1 intron-1 haplotype (rs1860545, rs4149581, rs4149580, rs4149577, D12S889, rs4149576) to the locked CF-modifier-locus panel for a CFGP-WGS-anchored phenome-wide modifier scan, with survival, chronic-infection burden, transplant outcomes, and modulator response as primary endpoints. The open verification question is whether the D12S889 microsatellite is reliably callable from short-read whole-genome sequencing on the CFGP substrate.

Plain-English glossary

  • ALI (Air-liquid interface) culture: Tissue-culture system in which respiratory epithelial cells grow on a porous membrane with culture medium below and air above, mimicking the in-vivo airway architecture. Standard substrate for CF epithelial biology studies.
  • D12S889: Formal name of a specific microsatellite in intron 1 of TNFR1. Alleles named by number of repeats — D12S889-10 (mild allele) and D12S889-13 (severe allele) are the two relevant alleles here.
  • ENCODE: Encyclopedia of DNA Elements — public consortium project that annotates regulatory elements, transcription-factor binding, and chromatin marks across the human genome.
  • ESRP2 (Epithelial Splicing Regulator Protein 2): An RNA-binding protein that regulates alternative splicing in epithelial cells; known CF modifier through its effect on the ENaC β-subunit gene SCNN1B; in this paper, also regulates TNFR1 splicing.
  • EUCFTSib: European CF Twin and Sibling Study — multi-center European cohort of CF sibling and twin pairs; original substrate for the 2009-2010 TNFR1 discovery.
  • FAMHAP: Family-based haplotype-analysis software package (RRID:SCR_009070); used to reconstruct haplotypes and diplotypes from parent-child genotype data.
  • GTEx: Genotype-Tissue Expression project — public reference database of tissue-specific gene-expression eQTL effects.
  • KNoRMA: Knorm-Mayo lung-function-severity phenotype — the canonical age-adjusted FEV1 percentile-rank statistic used across CF modifier-gene papers.
  • Microsatellite: A short DNA sequence that's tandemly repeated; different individuals carry different numbers of repeats, and each repeat-count is an allele.
  • Modifier gene: A gene outside the disease-causing gene (CFTR for CF) that influences how severe the disease manifests in patients with the same primary mutation.
  • Nasal potential difference (nPD): In-vivo electrophysiology test measuring electrical potential across nasal epithelium; the chloride-free-plus-isoproterenol response is a direct CFTR-function readout.
  • NMD (Nonsense-mediated decay): A cellular surveillance mechanism that degrades messenger RNAs containing premature stop codons; predicted to apply to TNFR1delEx2.
  • OrkambiFACTS: A clinical study cohort of CF patients enrolled for monitoring on lumacaftor/ivacaftor (brand name Orkambi) modulator therapy.
  • PBMC: Peripheral blood mononuclear cells — lymphocytes and monocytes isolated from whole blood; a common substrate for genotype-transcript correlation studies.
  • PCR-RFLP: Polymerase-Chain-Reaction Restriction-Fragment-Length-Polymorphism — a classical SNP-genotyping method using a restriction enzyme whose cut site depends on the SNP allele.
  • SurvMHH: Hannover Medical School CF survival cohort — F508del homozygotes seen at the Hannover CF center with long-term follow-up.
  • TNF (Tumour Necrosis Factor): A pro-inflammatory cytokine secreted by immune cells and many other cell types during infection, injury, and inflammation.
  • TNFR1 / TNFRSF1A: TNF Receptor 1 — a cell-surface receptor for TNF, encoded by the gene TNFRSF1A on chromosome 12.
  • TNFR1delEx2: An alternative TNFR1 transcript isoform lacking exon 2; annotated in ENSEMBL as TNFRSF1A-205 (transcript ID ENST00000534885.5); predicted to undergo nonsense-mediated decay.
  • TRAPS: Tumour-necrosis-factor-Receptor-Associated Periodic Syndrome — a rare autoinflammatory disease caused by specific mutations in TNFRSF1A; provides baseline biology for what TNFR1 dysfunction looks like.

Methods to lift

  • Multi-cohort replication design: discovery cohort EUCFTSib was where TNFR1 was originally proposed; replication carried out in OrkambiFACTS (genotype-CFTR-function) and SurvMHH (genotype-survival). Independent cohort replication is the gold-standard structure for any modifier-gene claim and applies directly to any CFTR PheWAS follow-up.
  • Survival extension from disease-severity readout: previously the TNFR1 signal was a CFTR-function and severity statistic; this paper rotates the endpoint to actual Kaplan-Meier survival, which is a clinically more meaningful primary outcome and one CFFPR can support directly.
  • Year-of-birth-stratified survival analysis: overlapping subsamples of sixty patients each by birth year, plus non-overlapping subsamples for genotype-frequency-distribution analysis — handles the cohort-effect confounder where treatment standards and median survival changed over time across the analyzable period.
  • Six-marker haplotype reconstruction at rs1860545-rs4149581-rs4149580-rs4149577-D12S889-rs4149576 using FAMHAP, with both haplotype-level and diplotype-level case-vs-control comparison.
  • Genotype-transcript correlation in primary airway epithelia as a candidate-molecular-intermediate identification step — sixty-four ALI-cultured donor samples, RT-qPCR for the alternative transcript TNFR1delEx2 with primers from Rittore et al.
  • ESRP2 small-interfering-RNA knockdown as a candidate-splice-regulator experiment, leveraging cDNA banked from a prior unrelated Hannover-group study on the ENaC β-subunit SCNN1B.
  • Negative-tissue control: PBMCs failed to show the TNFR1-genotype-TNFR1delEx2 correlation that airway epithelia did — establishing the tissue specificity of the mechanism.
  • Pubic-data cross-reference for biological plausibility: ENCODE chromatin marks (H3K4me1 inside the 2,424-bp intron element, H3K4me3 and H3K27ac flanking it) and ENSEMBL transcript-annotation database used to put the regulatory architecture on a public-reference substrate.

What to verify first

  • Whether the D12S889 microsatellite is reliably callable from short-read whole-genome sequencing data as used in the CFGP cohort — microsatellites are notoriously hard to genotype accurately from short reads, and a long-read sequencing benchmark (e.g., Mahmoud 2024) may be needed before claiming microsatellite-level replication.
  • Whether the four flanking SNPs (rs1860545, rs4149581, rs4149580, rs4149577, rs4149576) are reliably called in CFGP at acceptable concordance with gnomAD allele-frequency expectations, and whether their LD structure on the CFGP substrate matches the European-derived haplotype block Uden reports.
  • Whether CFFPR has the depth and longitudinal follow-up to support a Kaplan-Meier survival analogue with sufficient events for the CFGP-cohort-size power calculation.
  • Whether the CFGP ancestry composition (~93–94% European in cohorts referenced in recent papers) is compatible with European-anchored replication or requires per-ancestry stratification; the Uden cohorts are entirely European, so generalizability to non-European CF patients is unestablished at this finding.
  • Whether the existing project literature folder (04_literature/per_paper/) already contains a Stanke-2009 or Stanke-2010 per-paper note that should be consolidated before any new TNFR1 analysis design.
  • Whether KNoRMA-adjusted lung-function trajectory data exists for the CFGP substrate at adequate density to support secondary-endpoint analyses.
  • Whether the Reay-et-al PheWAS hit linking rs4149581 to lifetime pneumonia diagnosis is on a cohort that overlaps the CFGP comparison cohort (UK Biobank or All-of-Us) and what that comparator's per-variant power profile looks like.

Gap-map: closes / leaves open

Closes:

  • Replication of TNFR1-intron-1-genotype-to-CFTR-function association in an independent European cohort beyond the EUCFTSib discovery substrate (now confirmed in OrkambiFACTS).
  • Extension of the TNFR1 modifier readout from disease severity to actual patient survival — the eighteen-year median-survival contrast between D12S889-13/13 and D12S889-10/10 F508del homozygotes is a hard-numbered clinically meaningful endpoint.
  • Identification of TNFR1delEx2 as a candidate functional intermediate downstream of the intron-1 genotype, supported by ALI-culture qPCR evidence in sixty-four donor airway epithelia.
  • Identification of ESRP2 as a candidate splice regulator of the TNFR1delEx2 isoform via knockdown experiment in 16HBE14o-cells.
  • Tissue-specificity of the mechanism — PBMC analysis failed to show genotype-transcript correlation, ruling out a generic circulating-cell-based explanation.

Leaves open:

  • Whether short-read WGS in CFGP can reliably genotype D12S889 — the canonical Uden assay is PCR with a biotinylated primer and denaturing polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis, not extracted from sequencing.
  • The mechanistic question of how a nonsense-mediated-decay-targeted transcript drives a downstream phenotype if it is rapidly degraded.
  • Modulator-era persistence — the survival contrast is observed in cohorts whose CF care substantially predates Trikafta; whether the TNFR1 effect on survival attenuates under elexacaftor/tezacaftor/ivacaftor therapy is unestablished.
  • Pleiotropy of the TNFR1 intron-1 haplotype across non-pulmonary CF complications (CFRD, hepatobiliary, mental health, growth, malnutrition, transplant rejection).
  • Rare-variant burden architecture in TNFR1 — the locus is described entirely in terms of common-variant and microsatellite-allele effects.
  • Generalizability beyond European-ancestry CF cohorts.
  • Gene-by-environment interaction with viral pneumonia and other inflammatory triggers — Reay's PheWAS hit on rs4149581 and lifetime pneumonia suggests this dimension is operationally relevant and entirely untested in CF.
  • Whether the chr11p13 EHF / APIP locus and the TNFR1 locus share mechanistic biology through the EHF-ESRP2-EMT axis the authors invoke in the discussion — both EHF and ESRP2 are CF modifier genes, both are mediators of epithelial-mesenchymal transition, and TNFR1delEx2 is regulated by ESRP2; whether this convergence is real or coincidental is a clean follow-on question.

ALEX Today's CFTR-WGS episode is Uden and colleagues 2025, published in EBioMedicine, titled "Genotype and transcript processing of the tumour necrosis factor receptor TNFRSF1A in epithelial cells: implications for survival in cystic fibrosis." Senior author Frauke Stanke at Hannover Medical School with the European CF Twin and Sibling Study consortium and the Mall lab at Charité Berlin. Published just last summer.

JORDAN Why this one today?

ALEX Because it's a rare combination — a replication study, a survival study, *and* a molecular-mechanism study on the same gene in the same paper. The Hannover group originally proposed TNFR1 — the tumour-necrosis-factor receptor — as a CF modifier gene back in 2009-2010 from the European CF Twin and Sibling Study. This 2025 paper revisits that finding fifteen years later with new cohorts, an actual survival endpoint instead of just disease severity, and an attempt to find the molecular intermediate that explains *how* a TNFR1 genotype affects CFTR function in epithelial cells.

JORDAN Slow me down on the players first. What's TNF?

ALEX TNF is tumour necrosis factor. It's a pro-inflammatory cytokine — a small signalling protein — secreted by immune cells and many other cell types during infection, injury, and inflammation. It's central to the innate immune response. TNF binds to two receptors on target cells: TNFR1, which is the one we're focused on today, and TNFR2.

JORDAN And TNFR1?

ALEX TNFR1 — also called TNFRSF1A in formal gene nomenclature, which stands for tumour necrosis factor receptor superfamily member 1A — is a cell-surface receptor that, when activated by TNF, triggers downstream signalling that can drive either inflammation, cell survival, or programmed cell death depending on context. The gene is on chromosome 12. There's also a known clinical disease called TRAPS — tumour necrosis factor receptor–associated periodic syndrome — caused by certain TNFR1 mutations, which gives us a baseline biology of what TNFR1 dysfunction looks like.

JORDAN What's a CF modifier gene, again?

ALEX A modifier gene is one *outside* CFTR that affects how severe a given CF patient's disease is. Two patients with the same CFTR mutation — say, two F508del homozygotes — can have very different lung function, age at first Pseudomonas infection, age at diagnosis of CF-related diabetes, and age at death. Some of that variability is environmental, some is stochastic, but a measurable chunk is genetic. The variants outside CFTR that contribute to that genetic variability are called modifier genes. TNFR1 has been proposed as a CF modifier gene since the late 2000s.

JORDAN Now what's a microsatellite, and what does D12S889 mean?

ALEX A microsatellite is a short DNA sequence that's tandemly repeated — for example, a "CA" dinucleotide repeated five, ten, twenty, or thirty times in a row. Different people have different numbers of repeats. Each repeat-count is an allele. D12S889 is the formal name of a specific microsatellite on chromosome 12, sitting in intron 1 of the TNFR1 gene. The two relevant alleles in this paper are D12S889-10 — meaning the variant with the ten-repeat count — and D12S889-13, the thirteen-repeat version. Through previous Hannover group work the ten-allele is associated with mild CF disease and good residual CFTR function; the thirteen-allele is associated with severe disease and absent residual CFTR function.

JORDAN And the cohort acronyms?

ALEX Three cohorts. SurvMHH is the Hannover survival cohort — patients seen at Hannover Medical School's CF center with at least thirty years of follow-up. EUCFTSib is the European CF Twin and Sibling Study — multi-center European cohort of CF sibling pairs and twin pairs, which the Hannover group used for the original 2009-2010 TNFR1 discovery. OrkambiFACTS is a separate cohort of patients enrolled in a study of the CFTR modulator lumacaftor-ivacaftor — Orkambi being the brand name — with paired pre-treatment nasal-potential-difference measurements.

JORDAN One more — what's nasal potential difference?

ALEX Nasal potential difference, abbreviated nPD, is an in-vivo electrophysiology test where electrodes are placed in a patient's nose to measure the electrical potential across the nasal epithelium. CFTR is a chloride channel; when CFTR function is impaired, the chloride-driven component of the potential is reduced. The chloride-free-solution-plus-isoproterenol response is the standard CFTR-specific readout. It's the closest thing to a direct functional CFTR measurement available in living patients.

JORDAN Background and prior work?

ALEX The Hannover group, led by Burkhard Tümmler and Frauke Stanke, published two papers in 2009-2010 — Stanke and colleagues — that identified an eight-kilobase region in intron 1 of TNFR1 as associated with CFTR function and CF disease severity within the European CF Twin and Sibling Study. The flanking microsatellite D12S889 was the practical genotyping marker. Carriers of D12S889-10 had milder disease and detectable residual chloride conductance; D12S889-13 homozygotes had severe disease and no chloride conductance. That association was statistically convincing within EUCFTSib but had never been replicated in an independent cohort, and the molecular mechanism was a black box — the variants were in an intron, not in a coding region, so they couldn't be explained by a protein-changing mutation.

JORDAN So fifteen years of "we don't know what it does" hung over the finding.

ALEX Exactly. The 2025 paper is the Hannover group setting out to (a) replicate the genotype-function association in an independent cohort, (b) extend the readout from disease severity to actual patient survival, and (c) find the molecular mechanism — what does the intron-1 variant do that affects CFTR function in airway epithelial cells.

JORDAN Methods.

ALEX Three cohorts, three different analyses. SurvMHH provided one-hundred-seventy-four F508del-CFTR homozygous patients born between 1959 and 1994, seen regularly at the Hannover CF center; DNA was banked between 1990 and 1994 for CFTR mutation analysis. The team genotyped these patients for D12S889 using PCR amplification with a biotinylated primer and denaturing polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis. They ran Kaplan-Meier survival analysis on overlapping subsamples of sixty patients each, stratified by D12S889 genotype, using log-rank tests for between-group comparison. Within the EUCFTSib cohort, twenty-seven affected sib pairs with complete genotyping and survival data were analyzed for the older sibling's survival as a function of how many D12S889-13 alleles the pair carried. Within OrkambiFACTS, forty-six patients with valid pre-Orkambi nasal-potential-difference measurements and forty parental DNA samples from twenty-three families were genotyped at the six-marker haplotype that defines the refined two-thousand-four-hundred-twenty-four-base-pair intron-1 element — the marker set is rs1860545, rs4149581, rs4149580, rs4149577, D12S889, and rs4149576. They used the FAMHAP software package — a family-based haplotype-analysis tool, RRID SCR_009070 — to reconstruct haplotypes and diplotypes from parent-child genotype data. The diplotype-versus-CFTR-function comparison was done with the "dipcc" evaluation in FAMHAP.

JORDAN And the molecular arm?

ALEX They cultured primary airway epithelia from sixty-four lung-transplant donors at an air-liquid interface — ALI is the standard culture system in which respiratory epithelial cells grow on a porous membrane with culture medium below and air above, mimicking the in-vivo airway architecture. RNA was extracted with the standard Qiagen RNeasy mini-kit, reverse-transcribed, and assayed for TNFR1 transcripts by quantitative PCR using primers that distinguish wild-type TNFR1 from an alternative transcript called TNFR1delEx2 — a previously-described splice variant that lacks exon 2 — which had originally been characterized by Rittore and colleagues in the context of TRAPS biology. They also pulled cDNA from a prior Hannover-group experiment in which the epithelial-splicing-regulatory-protein-2 — ESRP2 — had been knocked down by small-interfering RNA in 16HBE14o-cells, to test whether ESRP2 regulates the TNFR1-delEx2 splice choice. CFTR protein expression was assessed by Western blot using the standard four-antibody mix from the CF Foundation antibody distribution program.

JORDAN Push back on something. The molecular arm uses sixty-four lung-transplant donors who are by definition end-stage disease. Is that the right substrate for inferring epithelial biology in CF?

ALEX That's a real concern. The lung-transplant donors are a mix of CF and non-CF tissue sources — they don't specify in the abstract what fraction is which. End-stage CF lung is chronically inflamed, which could push transcript-isoform ratios that aren't representative of earlier-stage disease. The authors acknowledge this implicitly in the discussion by noting that the epithelium's response to inflammation reshapes its secretome. But for the central claim — that D12S889 genotype tracks with TNFR1-delEx2 presence in primary airway epithelia — sixty-four donors with mixed genotypes is sufficient if the comparison is between TNFR1-genotype-defined groups within the same source-tissue pool.

JORDAN Results.

ALEX Three headline numbers. First, survival: within SurvMHH, F508del homozygotes born between 1975 and 1985 with D12S889 thirteen-thirteen had median survival of twenty-four years; D12S889 ten-thirteen heterozygotes had median survival of thirty-six years; D12S889 ten-ten homozygotes had median survival of forty-two years. Log-rank P equals 0.002. That's a roughly eighteen-year median-survival span between the contrasting homozygous genotypes — a stunning effect size if it holds. Second, replication of genotype-CFTR-function: within OrkambiFACTS, a significant difference in TNFR1 diplotype distribution between patients with versus without residual CFTR-mediated chloride secretion under the stringent above-five-millivolt-response definition, marker combination D12S889-rs4149581, P-best equals 0.041 using the "dipcc" FAMHAP evaluation. Homozygotes for the thirteen-two allele were observed only in the no-chloride-secretion subgroup. Third, the molecular link: TNFR1-delEx2 was differentially present in primary airway epithelia by D12S889 genotype, P equals 0.04. Only two out of fifteen non-thirteen-allele-carrier-derived ALI cultures expressed TNFR1-delEx2, versus more frequent expression in cultures derived from D12S889-thirteen carriers. And in the ESRP2 knockdown experiment, TNFR1-delEx2 transcript abundance increased upon ESRP2 silencing, P less than 0.02, implicating ESRP2 as a regulator of the TNFR1 splice choice.

JORDAN Did they answer the question?

ALEX Partially. They replicated the genotype-CFTR-function association in OrkambiFACTS — that's a real replication outside the discovery EUCFTSib cohort. They extended to survival, which is new and clinically dramatic. They proposed a molecular intermediate — the TNFR1-delEx2 splice variant, regulated by ESRP2 — but TNFR1-delEx2 is annotated in ENSEMBL as a non-functional transcript that should undergo nonsense-mediated decay, so the mechanism by which an NMD-targeted transcript would actually drive a survival difference is not directly addressed. They also acknowledge that they found no TNFR1-genotype-to-TNFR1-transcript correlation in peripheral blood mononuclear cells, only in airway epithelia, which means the mechanism is tissue-specific.

JORDAN So what does this suggest for the CFTR PheWAS project?

ALEX A specific concrete direction. The lead variant rs4149581 in the six-marker haplotype — one of the standard SNPs typed in this paper — was also previously linked to lifetime pneumonia diagnosis in a separate PheWAS by Reay and colleagues. That suggests the TNFR1 intron-1 element has pleiotropic inflammatory effects beyond CF. If we extend the locked-locus list for a CFFPR-PheWAS to include the Uden six-marker TNFR1 intron-1 haplotype, with primary outcomes being not just lung-function trajectory but survival, chronic infection burden, post-transplant outcomes, and modulator response, we could test whether the TNFR1 modifier signal generalizes across the CF complication phenome.
