How did this episode land for you?

Blurb

DeBoever and colleagues cataloged 18,228 polymorphic predicted protein-truncating variants on the UK Biobank Affymetrix array across 8,750 genes in 337,205 unrelated participants and tested them against 135 medical phenotypes. They found 74 significant PTV-phenotype associations — 47 in or near the MHC and 27 in 17 outside-MHC genes — and ran focused phenome-wide and non-additive analyses on the implicated genes plus 8 from prior literature. Standout hits included a protective splice-disrupting PTV in IL33 for asthma, non-additive FUT2-homozygote effects across hypertension and seven other phenotypes, and a NOL3 PTV-tissue-injury association backed by in-vitro knockdown. Proposed direction (conditional): apply DeBoever's MAF-stratified Benjamini-Yekutieli pipeline to LOFTEE-filtered CFTR PTVs in UKB exome data, testing whether heterozygous carriers show altered risk on the standard phenotype scaffold. Open question: whether LOFTEE-high-confidence CFTR variants align with the CFTR2 pathogenic-variant list.

Plain-English glossary

  • Array (genotyping): A chip that measures a fixed catalog of genetic variants per sample, much cheaper than sequencing but limited to the variants it was designed to capture.
  • Benjamini-Yekutieli (BY): False-discovery-rate correction method that holds under arbitrary dependence between tests; stricter than Benjamini-Hochberg.
  • Compound heterozygous: Two different LOF variants in the same gene, one on each chromosome — functionally equivalent to homozygous knockout.
  • Firth-fallback: Numerical technique for logistic regression that handles separation by penalizing the likelihood; used in PLINK when standard logistic regression fails to converge.
  • FUT2 / non-secretor: A common PTV in FUT2 (rs601338) creates non-secretor status; homozygotes do not secrete blood-group antigens into bodily fluids and have altered gut-mucosal biology.
  • Frameshift: Insertion or deletion of a non-three-multiple of bases, shifting the reading frame and producing nonsense downstream until the next stop codon.
  • gnomAD: Genome Aggregation Database — canonical reference of variant frequencies in unselected human populations.
  • HRC (Haplotype Reference Consortium): The imputation reference panel used in UK Biobank.
  • Knockout (human): Informal: a person with both copies of a gene predicted-broken — homozygous or compound-heterozygous LOF.
  • LOF / Loss-of-function variant: A DNA variant predicted to disable the gene product.
  • LOFTEE: Loss Of Function Transcript Effect Estimator — VEP plugin that filters predicted-LOF annotations to flag high-confidence LOFs.
  • MAF (minor allele frequency): Frequency of the rarer allele.
  • MHC: Major histocompatibility complex on chromosome 6 — high-LD region containing the HLA genes, often handled separately in association analyses.
  • Nonsense variant: Single nucleotide change that creates a premature stop codon.
  • Pleiotropy: One gene or variant affecting multiple phenotypes.
  • PLINK: Open-source whole-genome association software; widely used for biobank-scale GWAS.
  • PTV (protein-truncating variant): Nonsense SNV, frameshift indel, or splice-disrupting SNV.
  • REGENIE: Modern alternative biobank association software; used in Sun 2022 from yesterday's episode. DeBoever used PLINK because the paper predates REGENIE.
  • Splice-site variant: DNA change that disrupts intron-exon boundaries, breaking pre-mRNA splicing.
  • UKB / UK Biobank: UK population study; ~488,377 participants in this paper's data freeze.
  • VEP: Variant Effect Predictor — Ensembl's annotation tool that maps variants to predicted functional consequences.

Methods to lift

  • VEP v87 plus LOFTEE — annotate PTVs from raw genotype data; LOFTEE filters out the unreliable predicted-LOFs.
  • gnomAD allele-frequency sanity-check — flag PTVs whose UKB allele frequency diverges suspiciously from gnomAD (Fisher's exact and log-OR thresholds, stratified by MAF bin).
  • Cluster-plot manual review — visual review of array genotype-cluster plots to remove unreliable variant calls.
  • PLINK v2.00 Firth-fallback logistic regression — handles rare-variant separation problems where standard logistic regression fails.
  • Covariate set: age, sex, array type, first four principal components — note array-type covariate matters when participants span multiple Axiom array versions; for variants specific to one array, drop the array covariate.
  • Benjamini-Yekutieli FDR correction within MAF bins — three bins, 0.01–0.1%, 0.1–1%, >1% — corrected separately to handle the fact that test power differs dramatically across the frequency spectrum.
  • HLA-conditional analysis — for the ~half of hits in/near MHC, conditional analysis on 344 polymorphic HLA alleles to disentangle PTV signal from HLA-tag signal.
  • Imputed-PTV GWAS — supplementary analysis on 546 imputed PTVs from the HRC panel with imputation-score and missingness filtering, demonstrating that imputation captures only a subset of array-genotyped PTV signals.
  • Targeted PheWAS on implicated genes — 17 GWAS-implicated genes plus 8 prior-literature protective-effect genes, tested against the broader 206-phenotype scaffold.
  • Non-additive logistic regression with AIC comparison — for each gene-phenotype pair, compare additive versus genotypic-dominance / recessive models, retain non-additive model when ΔAIC ≤ −1.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage: apply the DeBoever 2018 pipeline — VEP plus LOFTEE-high-confidence filtering, MAF-stratified Benjamini-Yekutieli correction, Firth-fallback logistic regression — to UK Biobank exome data for the CFTR-pathogenic-PTV variant set, testing whether heterozygous carriers show altered risk across the standard 135 phenotype categories. The output is a complementary general-population-carrier arm to the in-CFGP CFTR PheWAS, focused specifically on whether one broken CFTR copy is detectable as a population-level phenotype risk-shifter — distinct from the in-CFGP question of phenotype variation within affected CF patients.

What to verify first

  • Whether LOFTEE-high-confidence annotation of CFTR exome variants in UKB matches the CFTR2 / ClinVar pathogenic-PTV list (LOFTEE-excluded but CFTR2-pathogenic variants, and vice versa, are a known reconciliation problem).
  • Whether the 135 DeBoever phenotype categories include CF-relevant comorbidities at sufficient case counts — chronic pancreatitis, bronchiectasis, chronic sinusitis, CBAVD, male infertility are rare enough that several may fall below DeBoever's 2000-cases threshold.
  • Whether the four-principal-component ancestry covariate is adequate for CFTR allele-frequency stratification — F508del is concentrated in Northern European ancestries and several other pathogenic alleles are population-specific.
  • Whether the unrelated-individuals filter — the DeBoever cohort was 337k unrelated of 488k total — needs re-derivation when restricting to the exome-sequenced subset.
  • Whether REGENIE (Sun 2022 standard) is preferred over PLINK Firth-fallback (DeBoever standard) for the modern exome substrate — REGENIE handles relatedness without subsetting, which would recover the ~150k related individuals DeBoever had to discard.
  • Whether non-additive testing (DeBoever's FUT2-style framing) is even meaningful for CFTR PTVs in heterozygotes-only, since homozygote PTV-carriers in UKB by definition have unmanaged CF and are unlikely to be enrolled.

ALEX Episode two. The paper is "Medical relevance of protein-truncating variants across 337,205 individuals in the UK Biobank study," published in Nature Communications in 2018. First author Christopher DeBoever, senior author Manuel Rivas, at Stanford at the time. PMID 29691392, PMC5915386. Open access.

JORDAN Why this one today?

ALEX Because protein-truncating variants — PTVs — are the conceptual category that anchors the entire loss-of-function-driven side of biobank genetics. If we want to ever say "what does it mean to have one broken copy of CFTR in a UK Biobank participant," we need to know how the field defines, filters, and tests for broken copies in general. DeBoever 2018 is the early UKB-scale paper that lays out that pipeline for genotyping arrays — what you do, what you find, what the gotchas are. It's also a paper that uses the phrase "human knockouts" deliberately and provocatively, which sets up half the rare-variant discussion that followed it.

JORDAN All right, hold on, before we go in. Plain English. PTV.

ALEX Protein-truncating variant. A DNA change that causes the protein the gene encodes to be cut short, or not made at all. Three main flavors. First, nonsense single-nucleotide variants — a single base change that creates a premature stop codon, telling the ribosome to halt translation before the protein is finished. Second, frameshift insertions or deletions — adding or removing some number of bases that's not a multiple of three, which shifts the reading frame and produces protein gibberish downstream until the next stop codon. Third, splice-site-disrupting variants — DNA changes that break the signals telling the cellular splicing machinery where to cut introns out of pre-mRNA, which usually produces a non-functional transcript.

JORDAN Loss-of-function, knockout — same thing?

ALEX Roughly. The term "loss-of-function variant" — LOF — and the term "protein-truncating variant" largely overlap; PTV is the more specific structural definition, LOF is the more functional one. Knockout is the genetics-of-mice term — a mouse with both copies of a gene deliberately broken — and human geneticists started using it informally to mean any person carrying two LOF copies of a gene. Hence "human knockouts" in the paper's framing.

JORDAN Homozygous, heterozygous, compound heterozygous.

ALEX Homozygous for a variant means both copies of the gene carry that same variant. Heterozygous means one copy. Compound heterozygous means two different LOF variants — one on each copy of the gene. From the gene's perspective, a compound heterozygote and a homozygote both have zero working copies, even though they're sequence-distinct.

JORDAN GWAS, pheWAS — we covered those yesterday. MAF — minor allele frequency, frequency of the rarer allele.

ALEX Right. And a couple of new ones for today. gnomAD — Genome Aggregation Database — the canonical reference catalog of allele frequencies in human populations, built from exomes and genomes of about 140,000 unselected individuals at the time of this paper. LOFTEE — Loss Of Function Transcript Effect Estimator — a software plugin for VEP that filters predicted-LOF annotations to remove the unreliable calls. VEP is Ensembl's Variant Effect Predictor, the tool that takes a DNA-position-and-allele list and tells you what each variant does to the proteins overlapping it. Then HRC, the Haplotype Reference Consortium panel — the reference dataset used to impute un-genotyped variants in UK Biobank samples. And finally Benjamini-Yekutieli — a multiple-testing correction method, a stricter cousin of Benjamini-Hochberg that controls the false discovery rate under arbitrary dependence between tests.

JORDAN I'm with you.

ALEX Background and purpose. The premise of the paper is built on two observations the field had collected through 2017. First, large sequencing studies — ExAC, gnomAD's predecessor — had shown that the average human carries roughly a hundred predicted loss-of-function variants. Most of them are tolerated. Some of them, when carried in two copies, cause Mendelian disease. The interesting subset is the variants where one or two copies measurably alter risk for *common* conditions — heart disease, asthma, hypothyroidism — because those variants point to genes that could be targeted pharmacologically.

JORDAN PCSK9 is the classic example.

ALEX Right. PCSK9 loss-of-function variants in humans lowered LDL cholesterol and lowered cardiovascular events — and from that observation grew an entire drug class, the PCSK9 inhibitors. So the implicit argument of DeBoever 2018 is: do PCSK9 at scale. Catalog every PTV in every gene that you can call from the UKB genotyping array, test it against every phenotype, find the next pharmacologically attractive target.

JORDAN And the second observation?

ALEX That GWAS by 2017 had collected tens of thousands of common-variant disease associations, but very few led to convincing drug targets — partly because common variants tend to have small effect sizes and ambiguous mechanism. PTVs by contrast are usually unambiguous: zero protein. So even a single hit lands you in a mechanistic place that's much closer to drug-target validation.

ALEX The unknown before this paper was empirical. How many PTVs in the UK Biobank actually associate with anything when you look across a broad phenotype set? How many of those replicate known biology, and how many are new? And critically, when you look at the homozygotes — the "human knockouts" of a gene — what phenotypes are different in those people?

ALEX Methods. They start from the Affymetrix UK Biobank Axiom array, annotated with VEP version 87 plus LOFTEE. After filtering — they removed PTVs with too much genotype-missingness, untrustworthy cluster plots, or allele frequencies that diverged suspiciously from gnomAD — they end up with 18,228 polymorphic predicted PTVs across 8,750 genes, on 337,205 unrelated individuals.

JORDAN That's a smaller number than the full 500k UKB cohort.

ALEX Right. They restricted to unrelated White-British-ancestry individuals — the standard pre-cleaning at the time — which leaves about two-thirds of the participants. Each participant had on average 95 predicted PTVs with MAF below one percent, consistent with the ~100 LOFs-per-individual baseline from 1000 Genomes.

JORDAN Phenotypes?

ALEX They tested 135 medical phenotypes — defined from self-reported conditions, ICD-10 codes, and clinical questionnaires — with at least one signal, plus 206 broader categorical phenotypes for some secondary analyses. For each PTV-phenotype pair they ran logistic regression in PLINK 2.00 with the Firth-fallback correction. Firth-fallback is a numerical trick: when the standard logistic regression fails because a cell of the case-control allele-count table is empty or near-empty — which happens a lot with rare variants — the algorithm falls back to a penalized-likelihood method called the logistf function that handles separated data without blowing up.

JORDAN Covariates?

ALEX Age, sex, array type — UKBB Axiom versus UKBiLEVE Axiom, the two genotyping platforms — and the first four principal components for ancestry. Then they corrected p-values with Benjamini-Yekutieli inside three separate MAF bins — 0.01 to 0.1 percent, 0.1 to 1 percent, and above 1 percent — to control false discovery rate at 5 percent within each frequency band.

ALEX One design choice I want to push back on. They run their phenome-wide-association sub-analysis only on the 17 genes that came up in the main GWAS, plus 8 from prior literature. Twenty-five genes. That's a hypothesis-generating approach and they're explicit about it, but it means we can't tell from the paper what a full all-PTVs-by-all-phenotypes scan would have shown. The argument they make — that the genotyping array is too sparse to power that — is reasonable, but it's also the cleanest example of why exome sequencing eventually had to replace array-based PTV scans.

ALEX Results. They find 74 significant PTV-phenotype associations total. 47 of them sit inside or near the MHC region on chromosome 6, where linkage disequilibrium with HLA alleles is overwhelming, so they set those aside. Outside the MHC, 27 associations involve PTVs in 17 genes against 20 different phenotypes.

JORDAN Specific hits worth remembering?

ALEX A few. The splice-disrupting PTV rs146597587 in IL33 — IL33 is an inflammatory cytokine — protects against asthma, MAF 0.48 percent, odds ratio 0.64, P=7.6×10⁻¹³. That replicated a prior finding. rs601338 in FUT2 — a common variant, MAF 49 percent, encoding a non-secretor status — associates non-additively with hypertension and other phenotypes, with heterozygote odds ratios near 1.0 and homozygote odds ratios ranging from 1.05 to 1.51 across eight phenotypes. The PTV rs11078928 in GSDMB protects against asthma, P=6.3×10⁻⁵⁰, OR 0.90. A frameshift in BRCA2 increases family history of lung cancer, OR 1.19. A rare PTV in NOL3 increases muscle injury, OR 3.43, and they back it up with cell-culture knockdown experiments showing increased apoptosis markers.

JORDAN Pleiotropy?

ALEX Lots. FLG — filaggrin — PTVs associate with eczema as expected, and also with asthma and elevated skin-cancer risk. CHEK2 PTVs associate with multiple cancers. TSHR PTVs associate with hypothyroidism. The pattern that recurs across the paper is that PTVs in any single gene rarely affect just one condition.

ALEX Did they answer the question? Partly. They demonstrated that a PTV-PheWAS on UK Biobank array data produces a meaningful, partially-replicating catalog of gene-disease associations — and that even at the array-only scale, you find some pharmacologically attractive targets like IL33. But they also demonstrated the upper bound of what arrays can do: at MAF below 0.1 percent they had almost no findings, and they admit explicitly that exome sequencing was needed for that band. So they answered the methodological question — "can it work" — and they answered the biological question only for the gene set the array could cover.

ALEX Beat six. What does this paper specifically suggest for Runyu's CFTR work? One direction is the following. CFTR has a long-known catalog of pathogenic and likely-pathogenic PTVs — nonsense, frameshift, splice-site — collected in the CFTR2 database and curated by the CF Foundation's variant-curation working group. In a UK Biobank exome-PTV-PheWAS framing, those CFTR PTVs would be a defined variant set, and the question would be whether *heterozygous* carriers — one functional CFTR copy, one PTV — show altered risk for any of the 135 standardized phenotype categories.

JORDAN That sounds very similar to what we proposed yesterday from the Sun paper.

ALEX It is, with two methodological wrinkles specific to DeBoever's framing. First, the PTV variant set has to be defined the way DeBoever defines it — with LOFTEE filtering, with array-cluster QC if you're using array data, with gnomAD allele-frequency sanity-checking — rather than just using the CFTR2 list as-is. Some pathogenic CFTR variants are missense rather than truncating, and would not enter a strict PTV pipeline. Second, the analysis is multi-MAF-stratified: you run separate Benjamini-Yekutieli corrections on rare versus common bins, which matters for CFTR because the carrier frequency for any single pathogenic variant is well below one percent, but the aggregate CFTR-carrier frequency in Europeans is around four percent.

JORDAN Formal version of the question?

ALEX "Do heterozygous carriers of LOFTEE-high-confidence CFTR protein-truncating variants in UK Biobank European-ancestry individuals show altered risk for any of the standard 135 medical-phenotype categories — including a pre-specified subset of CF-relevant comorbidities such as chronic pancreatitis, chronic sinusitis, bronchiectasis, male infertility, and CBAVD — using DeBoever-style MAF-stratified Benjamini-Yekutieli correction?"

ALEX Beat seven. Gap, conditional on that direction. What does DeBoever close, what does it leave open, what would Runyu need to check first?

JORDAN Closes?

ALEX The pipeline is reproducible. VEP-plus-LOFTEE annotation is a stable, well-documented standard. Firth-fallback logistic regression in PLINK is a documented method. Benjamini-Yekutieli inside MAF bins is a documented method. The 135-phenotype scaffold is published in the supplementary tables. None of those pieces have to be reinvented.

JORDAN Leaves open?

ALEX Whether the array-era results actually replicate at exome-sequencing depth — which is now a standard concern, because the larger exome PTV catalogs from Wang 2021 and Sun 2022 superseded DeBoever's findings for most genes. For CFTR specifically, whether the array even genotypes the relevant CFTR truncating variants depends on which version of the UKB Axiom array the participant was assayed on. The exome-sequenced subset of UKB is the cleaner substrate. Also, whether the FUT2-style non-additive associations would generalize to CFTR — DeBoever found that for FUT2, heterozygotes look like wild-type but homozygotes diverge, which is a recessive-trait pattern. For CFTR, heterozygotes are by definition *not* affected by CF, so the carrier-PheWAS question is precisely whether any non-recessive heterozygote-detectable phenotype exists at all. The DeBoever framing doesn't presuppose the answer.

JORDAN Verify first?

ALEX We'd need to check several things. Whether LOFTEE-high-confidence annotation of CFTR variants from UKB exome data matches the CFTR2 / ClinVar pathogenic-PTV list — there will be edge cases where LOFTEE excludes a variant that CFTR2 lists as pathogenic, or vice versa. Whether the 135 DeBoever phenotype categories include the CF-relevant comorbidities at all — chronic pancreatitis, bronchiectasis, and CBAVD are rare enough that they may not survive case-count thresholds. Whether the four-principal-component ancestry covariate is adequate for CFTR allele-frequency stratification, since some pathogenic CFTR variants are population-specific. Whether the unrelated-individual filter would have to be re-derived for any cohort change. None of these are knowable without running the pipeline. They're "what we'd need to check," not "what we know."
