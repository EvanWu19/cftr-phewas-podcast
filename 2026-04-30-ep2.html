Methods to lift

  • GraphTyper joint-genotyping as the primary short-variant caller, with GATK HaplotypeCaller as a parallel comparator. Use trio transmission, imputation accuracy, and concordance with curated truth sets (Genome in a Bottle) for head-to-head evaluation. Confirm what caller the CFF WGS pipeline used and document the mismatch.
  • AAscore quality filter at >=0.5, or an analogous logistic-regression QC score from whatever caller is in use, applied uniformly before all downstream analysis. Report retained-fraction explicitly (Halldorsson kept 98.14%).
  • Per-ancestry sub-cohort definition via genetic clustering of array genotypes plus self-described ethnicity plus supervised ancestry inference, with per-cohort imputation panels and per-cohort depletion-rank scoring rather than a single pooled analysis.
  • Haplotype reference panel built from variants in >=3 sequenced individuals, then imputed back into all UKB samples. Halldorsson achieved reliable imputation for 65.8% of 0.001-0.002% frequency variants in XBI (3-5 carriers).
  • Depletion rank (DR) score in 500-bp windows, controlling for nucleotide composition and heptamer mutation rate, as an intra-population conservation metric. Lift the published track as a non-coding annotation prior, or compute a CF-cohort-specific DR for 7q31.2 modifier loci.
  • SV calling pipeline: short-read discovery with Manta, joint genotyping with GraphTyper2, frequency stratification matching SNP/indel cohorts. Validates against gnomAD-SV and long-read truth sets.
  • Microsatellite calling with popSTR for tandem repeats 1-6 bp, separate from the SNP/indel pipeline.
  • ACMG secondary-finding scan comparing v3.0 (73 genes) to v2.0 (59 genes) on the same cohort, and WGS-vs-WES uplift on the same individuals.

Gap-map note

The WGS-only artifacts that justify a fresh CFTR PheWAS on the CFF cohort are the large CFTR exonic deletions (e.g. the Slavic CFTRdele2,3(21kb) and other multi-exon deletions, normally caught only by clinical MLPA) and the intron 9 TG-repeat plus poly-T microsatellite that modulates exon 10 splicing. Both classes are systematically invisible to the exome and SNP-array studies that dominate the prior CFF-cohort literature, so even against a 60+ publication backdrop, an SV-aware and microsatellite-aware joint-called WGS analysis of CFTR has a defensible novelty story - provided the upstream CFF pipeline actually ran SV and STR callers (open methods question for week one).
