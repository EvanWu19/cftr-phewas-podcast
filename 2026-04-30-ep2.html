Halldorsson et al. 2022 — The sequences of 150,119 genomes in the UK Biobank

Blurb

The UK Biobank's first whole-genome sequencing release on 150,119 participants. Built using the GraphTyper graph-based joint variant caller from deCODE Genetics, filtered with the AAscore quality threshold (kept >=0.5, retaining 98.14% = 643,747,446 variants), and stratified into three ancestry sub-cohorts (XBI British/Irish n=131,958; XAF African n=2,963; XSA South Asian n=3,035). The catalog: 585 million SNPs (about 7.0% of all theoretically possible human SNPs), 58.7 million short indels, 895,055 structural variants, and 2.5 million microsatellites. Per person, whole-genome sequencing yielded roughly 40-times more variants than whole-exome sequencing on the same individuals, and the rate of carriers of clinically actionable genotypes rose from 2.0% (whole-exome) to 2.5% (whole-genome, matched 59-gene list). This is the closest published comparator to a CFF whole-genome project: same data type (joint-called short-read whole-genome sequencing at ~30x), at population scale, with explicit choices about caller, quality filter, and ancestry stratification.

Plain-English glossary

  • AAscore — a per-variant quality score from a logistic regression model; the probability that a variant call is real rather than a sequencing error. The paper kept variants with AAscore >= 0.5.
  • ACMG actionable genes — a list curated by the American College of Medical Genetics of genes where finding a pathogenic variant changes clinical management (v2.0 had 59 genes; v3.0 has 73).
  • Coverage (30x) — average number of times each base in the genome was independently read by the sequencer. Higher coverage means more accurate calls. Halldorsson averaged 32.5x with a per-person floor of 23.5x.
  • CFTR — the gene whose loss-of-function causes cystic fibrosis. Has clinically important large deletions (caught by MLPA in clinic) and a TG/poly-T microsatellite in intron 9 that modulates splicing.
  • GraphTyper — graph-based joint variant caller built at deCODE Genetics in Iceland; used as the primary caller in this paper, with GATK HaplotypeCaller as a parallel comparator.
  • Haplotype — a stretch of DNA letters inherited together from one parent; a combination of nearby variants on the same chromosome.
  • Imputation — statistical inference of variants you did not directly measure, using a reference panel of haplotypes from people who were sequenced.
  • Indel — a small insertion or deletion of one to a few dozen DNA letters.
  • Joint-calling / genotype caller — the genotype caller is the software that decides each person's genotype at each position from raw reads; joint-calling means running it on all individuals at once so information is shared across samples.
  • Manta — structural-variant discovery tool used upstream of GraphTyper2 for the SV pipeline.
  • Microsatellite / STR (short tandem repeat) — a short DNA pattern (1-6 base pairs) repeated a variable number of times in tandem; called in this paper with popSTR.
  • MLPA — multiplex ligation-dependent probe amplification; a clinical assay used in CF clinics to detect large CFTR deletions that exome sequencing misses.
  • popSTR — microsatellite genotyping tool used to call the 2.5 million microsatellites in this dataset.
  • Reference panel — a curated catalog of haplotypes built from sequenced individuals, used as the source for imputation. Halldorsson built one per ancestry sub-cohort, requiring variants to appear in at least 3 individuals.
  • SNP (single-nucleotide polymorphism) — a one-letter difference at a single genomic position between people.
  • Structural variant (SV) — a large rearrangement (>=50 base pairs) such as a deletion, duplication, insertion, or inversion.
  • Variant call file — the file format (typically VCF) listing every variant called, the individuals who carry it, and quality metadata.
  • Variant quality score — a numeric confidence measure that a variant call is real, used to filter the raw callset.
  • WES (whole-exome sequencing) — sequencing limited to the protein-coding ~2% of the genome.
  • WGS (whole-genome sequencing) — sequencing the full ~3 billion-base human genome, including non-coding regions.
  • XBI / XAF / XSA — UK Biobank ancestry sub-cohorts: British/Irish, African, and South Asian respectively. Defined via genetic clustering plus self-reported ethnicity.

Methods to lift

  • Joint-call with a graph-based caller when feasible. GraphTyper had more true positives in trios and 9.4% more reliably-imputing variants than GATK HaplotypeCaller on the same data, despite producing fewer raw calls.
  • Set an explicit AAscore-style probabilistic quality filter. The 0.5 threshold retained 98.14% of variants — permissive, but defensible because rare variants are rescued by the haplotype reference panel.
  • Stratify by ancestry sub-cohort, build the haplotype reference panel per cohort. Halldorsson did XBI, XAF, XSA separately, with a minimum-three-carrier rule for panel inclusion.
  • Run dedicated structural-variant and microsatellite callers. Manta + GraphTyper2 for SVs, popSTR for STRs. These are the variant categories that whole-exome and chip arrays cannot recover and that include CFTR-relevant signals (large deletions, intron 9 TG/poly-T tract).
  • Document per-individual yields and saturation curves. Halldorsson reports ~3.4M variants per haploid genome, ~40x more variants per person than whole-exome on the same samples, and ~7% saturation of all theoretically possible human SNPs at n=150K.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on actually working with CFF whole-genome data, the first methodological task is to inspect the CFF variant call file format and document the presence or absence of structural-variant and short-tandem-repeat calls before any association analysis. The CFTR gene has clinically important large deletions (such as CFTRdele2,3, common in certain Eastern European populations, routinely detected by MLPA in the CF clinic) and the intron 9 TG/poly-T microsatellite that affects splicing of exon 10. These are exactly the WGS-only categories that justify using whole-genome data over a chip array. If they are not present in the CFF variant call file, the differentiation argument for whole-genome sequencing collapses. None of this presumes the project is going forward — it is a precondition check.

What to verify first

  • Variant call file format. Inspect the actual file format delivered by the CFF whole-genome pipeline (likely VCF; possibly multiple VCFs split by variant class).
  • Variant-class coverage. Confirm whether the call set includes structural variants and short tandem repeats, or only SNPs and short indels. Note which tools were used.
  • Joint-calling pipeline. Identify which caller and version was used (most likely DeepVariant or GATK rather than GraphTyper). This affects rare-variant precision in repetitive regions, including parts of CFTR.
  • Ancestry composition. Document the genetic-ancestry composition of the CFF cohort, including the size of any non-European subgroup, to inform pooled-versus-stratified analysis decisions.
