Blurb

Taliun et al. 2021 describes the joint analysis of 53,831 deep (~30x) whole genomes from 33 NHLBI-funded studies in the TOPMed program, with deliberately diverse ancestral composition (African American, Hispanic/Latino, European American, East Asian, Amish, Samoan). Centralized BWA-MEM alignment to hs38DH and joint variant calling with the GotCloud pipeline produced a catalogue of 410,323,831 SNVs and indels — 97% rare (MAF < 1%), 46% singletons — and seeded the public TOPMed imputation reference panel (now 97,256 individuals, 308M variants) that lifts rare-variant imputation r^2 in African-ancestry samples from ~0.17 to ~0.96. The single CFTR PheWAS implication: the TOPMed panel makes rare-variant imputation tractable in array-genotyped diverse cohorts, but pathogenic CFTR alleles are unlikely to be well-represented in a healthy-population reference, so any imputation-based extension of the CFF WGS analysis needs an explicit panel-coverage check at CF-relevant loci.

Methods to lift (into the methods primer)

  • BWA-MEM alignment to hs38DH (1000 Genomes GRCh38 build with decoy sequences) — name the reference build explicitly in the methods.
  • GotCloud joint-calling pipeline as the multi-sample variant discovery + genotyping reference; cite the reported lower Mendelian inconsistency vs GATK 4.1.3.
  • SVM-based site filter trained on array + 1000 Genomes positives and Mendelian-inconsistent negatives — alternative to GATK VQSR for the QC writeup.
  • Eagle 2.4 for phasing of variants with depth >10x, after MAC ≥ 2 filter.
  • PC-AiR for selecting an unrelated subset at third-degree threshold (TOPMed went 53,831 → 40,722 unrelated).
  • Two-layer ancestry framework: self-reported "population groups" for descriptive analyses; hard genetic-ancestry threshold (>90% inferred ancestry, seven-way pipeline) for analytical work; RFMix for local ancestry where needed.
  • Duplicate-sample concordance as a primary QC metric (TOPMed reports 0.9995 SNV / 0.9930 indel concordance) — borrow this for the CFF WGS QC table.
  • Batch-effect quantification via genome-wide association of variants against batch (TOPMed: 0.004% of variants reach genome-wide significance) — directly portable QC test.
  • Bespoke caller for hard regions (Stargazer for CYP2D6) — establishes the precedent that off-the-shelf joint calling will not resolve every clinically relevant locus, including potentially CFTR-adjacent complex regions.

Gap-map note

TOPMed sits at the methods-foundation end of our gap map: it defines what large diverse-cohort WGS QC and imputation look like in practice, but its analytical utility for the CFTR PheWAS is bounded by (a) the healthy-population ascertainment of the reference panel — pathogenic CF alleles will be rare or absent — and (b) the analytical N for any single non-European ancestry group still being modest (~2,000 broadly African, ~1,355 East Asian) even at a 53k-genome cohort scale, which usefully reframes our 5,000-CFF-WGS power conversation as "comparable to TOPMed's African analytical arm" rather than "small."
