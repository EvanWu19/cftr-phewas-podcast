Blurb (one paragraph)

Stanke, van Barneveld, Hedtfeld, Wölfl, Becker, and Tümmler 2014 in European Journal of Human Genetics is the European mechanistic replication of the original Wright 2010 11p13 CF-modifier GWAS hit. The Hannover group genotyped two intragenic microsatellites in EHF — the epithelial-specific ETS transcription factor at the locus — across European CF Twin and Sibling Study F508del homozygotes, then linked the EHF haplotype to two CF basic-defect endophenotypes (NPD amiloride response and ICM CFTR-mediated residual chloride secretion). Rare EHF haplotypes track with the atypical residual-function phenotype; the common 10-14 haplotype tracks with the classic severe CF basic defect. Rectal-biopsy transcriptome analysis (GEO GSE15568) shows 1166 differentially expressed probe sets between rare-EHF and common-EHF carriers, with DAVID enrichment placing the rare-EHF-upregulated 420-gene set in protein glycosylation and Golgi-trafficking pathways. The 1166-gene list contains 12 named CFTR-chaperome members (SNX4, CALU, KIF5B, PSMB1, CSTB, ubiquitin C, RANBP10, SEPT6, ATP2A2, FAM120A, AIFM1, CD59) plus syntaxin 16 and HSPA4. Proposed direction (conditional on CFGP WGS variant calls and CFFPR phenotype linkage): SKAT-O rare-variant burden test on the Stanke 98-gene EHF-regulated trafficking/glycosylation/chaperome set, restricted to F508del homozygotes in CFGP, against pre-specified residual-function-buffered endpoints. Gap-map closes: EHF as plausible causal gene at 11p13; rare-vs-common-allele direction-of-effect on CF basic-defect endophenotypes; epithelial trafficking/glycosylation as the relevant pathway. Leaves open: EHF-vs-APIP causal-gene question, independent transcriptome replication, modulator-era persistence.

Plain-English glossary

  • CFTR — Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane conductance Regulator gene; epithelial chloride/bicarbonate channel.
  • F508del / p.Phe508del-CFTR — the most common CF-causing mutation (three-letter deletion at codon 508), produces a misfolded protein.
  • Residual CFTR function — the small fraction of misfolded CFTR that escapes ER quality-control and reaches the cell surface.
  • EHF — ETS Homologous Factor, an epithelial-specific transcription factor at chr11p13, the candidate causal gene of the Wright 2010 GWAS hit.
  • APIP — co-located 11p13 candidate gene (methionine salvage pathway); not addressed in Stanke 2014.
  • ETS — family of transcription factors, named after an avian-leukemia-virus gene.
  • ER (Endoplasmic Reticulum) — cell organelle where membrane proteins are synthesized and quality-checked.
  • Golgi apparatus — stack of membrane compartments downstream of the ER where proteins are further processed (glycosylated).
  • Glycosylation — attachment of sugar chains to a protein, required for CFTR to traffic correctly.
  • Trafficking — the ER → Golgi → apical membrane shipping route.
  • Chaperome — the network of chaperone and co-chaperone proteins that fold and quality-control CFTR.
  • HDAC7 — histone deacetylase 7; HDAC inhibitors are known to restore p.Phe508del-CFTR function.
  • NPD — nasal potential difference; in vivo ion-channel function assay using a nasal electrode.
  • ICM — intestinal current measurement; ex vivo ion-channel function assay on rectal biopsy in an Ussing chamber.
  • Amiloride — drug that blocks ENaC, the partner epithelial sodium channel of CFTR.
  • ENaC — Epithelial Sodium Channel, hyperactive in CF when CFTR fails.
  • DIDS — non-CFTR chloride-channel blocker, used to confirm CFTR-mediated chloride secretion by exclusion.
  • Endophenotype — measurable intermediate biological trait between genotype and clinical disease.
  • Microsatellite — short tandem-repeat DNA marker (e.g. TG-TG-TG-TG); used as a genetic variant marker.
  • European CF Twin and Sibling Study — Hannover/Tümmler cohort of CF twin and sibling pairs; family-based design.
  • FAMHAP — family-based statistical association software (Becker lab, Bonn).
  • P-raw / P-corr — uncorrected p-value / within-locus-multiple-testing-corrected p-value.
  • GenePattern — Broad Institute genomic-analysis web platform.
  • DAVID — Database for Annotation, Visualization, and Integrated Discovery; gene-ontology enrichment tool.
  • GEO — Gene Expression Omnibus, public microarray/RNA-seq archive.
  • U133 Plus 2.0 — Affymetrix microarray platform with 22,283 probe sets used here.
  • Wright 2010 — original North American CF Genetic Modifier Study GWAS that identified 11p13.
  • ESRP2, MUC4/MUC20, CHP2 — recent additions to the CF lung-modifier catalog beyond Wright 2010.

ALEX Today's third paper is Stanke, van Barneveld, Hedtfeld, Wölfl, Becker, and Tümmler, 2014, in the *European Journal of Human Genetics*: "The CF-modifying gene EHF promotes p.Phe508del-CFTR residual function by altering protein glycosylation and trafficking in epithelial cells." Frauke Stanke is first author; the senior is Burkhard Tümmler at Hannover Medical School. We're reading it because it is the European-cohort mechanistic follow-up to the original 2010 Wright paper that identified 11p13 — the EHF/APIP locus — as a CF lung-disease modifier in a North American GWAS. We've already covered the original Wright paper. Today's job is to understand what the Hannover group did differently and why their replication-plus-mechanism story matters for any future CFTR PheWAS at CFFPR scale.

JORDAN Stop. Spell out every term in that cold open as if I have never heard it. EHF, F508del-CFTR, residual function, glycosylation, trafficking, all of it.

ALEX Good. CFTR you know — Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane conductance Regulator, the chloride/bicarbonate channel on the apical surface of epithelial cells. F508del is the most common CF-causing mutation, a three-letter deletion at position 1521 to 1523 of the CFTR coding sequence. The protein the deletion produces — p.Phe508del-CFTR — folds incorrectly in the endoplasmic reticulum, the ER, which is the cell organelle where new membrane proteins are made and quality-checked. Most p.Phe508del-CFTR protein gets caught by the quality-control machinery and sent for degradation by the proteasome — the cell's protein-shredder — before it ever reaches the cell surface. A small amount escapes and reaches the apical membrane. That escaped fraction is called residual CFTR function: the patient still makes some real chloride channels, just not many. Residual chloride secretion is what you can measure in those patients — a small ion-channel response that some F508del homozygotes display and others don't, depending on their genetic background. Glycosylation is the cellular process of attaching sugar chains to a protein after it is made; CFTR gets glycosylated as it passes from the ER through a stack of compartments called the Golgi apparatus on its way to the cell surface. Trafficking is the general term for that whole shipping route — ER, Golgi, then apical membrane. EHF stands for ETS Homologous Factor, a transcription factor — a protein that switches other genes on or off — that is specifically expressed in epithelial cells. It sits on chromosome 11p13. ETS is a family of transcription factors named after the original gene found in an avian leukemia virus. Endophenotype is a measurable, intermediate biological trait that sits between a genotype and a clinical disease — for CF, an endophenotype is something like residual chloride secretion in the rectum, which you can measure objectively, rather than "severity of lung disease," which is harder to quantify. NPD is nasal potential difference: an electrode in the nose measures the voltage across the airway epithelium while you spray different chemicals onto it; the response tells you which ion channels are working. ICM is intestinal current measurement: you take a small biopsy from the rectum, mount it in a chamber, and measure ion currents across it when you apply chemicals. Amiloride is a drug that blocks ENaC, the epithelial sodium channel — a partner-of-CFTR ion channel; in CF without working CFTR, ENaC is hyperactive, so you get a strong voltage drop when you block it, and that's the "high response to amiloride" you see in classic CF. DIDS is a chloride-channel blocker that does not block CFTR — it blocks the other chloride channels, so DIDS-insensitive chloride secretion is by elimination CFTR-mediated. Microsatellite means a short tandem-repeat DNA sequence, like TG-TG-TG-TG-TG; the number of repeats varies between people and you can use that variation as a marker. Twin and Sibling Study: a cohort design where you enroll twin pairs and sibling pairs with the same disease, and you compare genetic and phenotypic differences within pairs to discover modifier loci with much higher statistical power than comparing unrelated cases. FAMHAP is the family-based statistical association software they used. P-raw means uncorrected p-value; P-corr means p-value corrected for testing multiple markers within a locus. GenePattern is a Broad Institute genomic-analysis web platform. DAVID — Database for Annotation, Visualization, and Integrated Discovery — is the standard tool for asking whether a list of genes is enriched for biological processes or cellular components. GEO is the Gene Expression Omnibus, the public archive where microarray and RNA-seq data are deposited. Wright 2010 — we covered it back on May 7 — is the original North American CF Genetic Modifier Study GWAS that first reported the 11p13 association.

JORDAN Now what's the question they're trying to answer?

ALEX Wright et al. in 2010 had published a GWAS in over a thousand CF patients showing a genome-wide-significant association of a 11p13 intergenic region with CF lung-disease severity. Two questions immediately followed. First: is the association real in an independent, ancestrally and clinically distinct cohort — specifically, the European CF Twin and Sibling Study, which was assembled separately from the North American consortium, used twin/sib pair design, and characterized basic-defect endophenotypes rather than clinical lung function. Replication in an independent cohort is the standard step that converts a GWAS hit from a candidate to a confirmed locus. Second: assuming it replicates, what is the mechanism — what gene at the 11p13 locus mediates the effect, and through what cellular pathway. The intergenic GWAS signal itself doesn't tell you. Stanke's group selected EHF, the epithelial-specific transcription factor at the locus, as the candidate. The rationale: epithelial-specific transcription factors that regulate CFTR-pathway genes are obvious candidates to modify residual CFTR function. They aimed to replicate the locus association and then use rectal-biopsy transcriptome data to identify the EHF-regulated downstream genes that could mechanistically explain the modifier effect.

ALEX Methods. The European CF Twin and Sibling Study had genotyping data from 101 CF families, 85 of which came from a panel of 466 twin and sibling pairs. From those, 16 unrelated F508del homozygotes had their basic-defect phenotyping done. The case-reference design used endophenotypes, not lung function. Endophenotype one: response to amiloride in NPD. Patients with a small voltage change of 27 millivolts or less on amiloride application were cases — 13 unrelated patients with low response, which is the atypical phenotype indicating residual CFTR function. References were 17 patients with the high typical CF response of 28 mV or more. Endophenotype two: CFTR-mediated residual chloride secretion in ICM, defined as chloride current present after stimulation with carbachol and after stimulation with histamine but with DIDS pre-treatment to rule out non-CFTR chloride channels. Nine unrelated cases with residual CFTR-mediated chloride secretion versus 14 references without. They genotyped two intragenic microsatellites in EHF — EHFSat1 in intron 1, a TG-repeat, and EHFSat2 in intron 2, also a TG-repeat. Statistical testing was done in FAMHAP with 10,000 Monte Carlo permutations, with multiple-testing correction reported as P-corr. Then transcriptome analysis on the same 16 F508del homozygotes — rectal suction biopsies hybridized to Affymetrix U133 Plus 2.0 chips, 22,283 probe sets, deposited as GEO accession GSE15568. They split the 16 patients into seven who carried two rare EHF haplotypes versus nine who carried at least one common EHF haplotype, ran GenePattern differential expression, then DAVID gene-ontology enrichment on the differentially expressed gene lists.

JORDAN Pushback on-air. The sample sizes are tiny by 2026 standards. Thirteen cases against seventeen references for the NPD endophenotype, nine against fourteen for the ICM endophenotype, seven against nine for the transcriptome split. Even with the family-based design giving them better-than-naive power, the variance on these effect-size estimates is enormous, and there is no formal replication of the transcriptome result in a second cohort. Their multiple-testing correction is within-locus, not genome-wide — which is fine, since this is a candidate-replication study and not a GWAS, but it means the conclusions hinge on accepting the 2010 Wright GWAS hit at face value as the prior. If Wright's locus had been a false-positive, the multiple-testing math here doesn't catch it. The strength of the paper is mechanism, not the replication-power claim.

Headline biology: rare EHF alleles shift the epithelial-cell transcriptome toward more efficient protein glycosylation and trafficking, which lets more p.Phe508del-CFTR escape ER quality-control and reach the apical membrane as a functional residual channel.
