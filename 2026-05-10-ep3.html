One-paragraph blurb

Corvol and the International CF GWAS Consortium pool 6,365 pancreatic-insufficient CF patients from the U.S. Gene Modifier Study (GMS), Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study (CGS), and French CF Modifier Consortium (FrGMS) onto the same KNoRMA lung-severity phenotype, perform random-effects meta-analysis across 13 genotype-platform-by-cohort subgroups using ~8.5 million imputed SNPs, and identify five genome-wide-significant CF lung-disease modifier loci: chr3q29 (MUC4/MUC20, P=3.3×10⁻¹¹), chr5p15.3 (SLC9A3, P=6.8×10⁻¹²), chr6p21.3 (HLA Class II, P=1.2×10⁻⁸), chr11p12-p13 (EHF/APIP, P=1.9×10⁻¹⁰), and chrXq22-q23 (AGTR2/SLC6A14, P=1.8×10⁻⁹). Conditional analysis reveals independent secondary signal at SLC9A3 and EHF/APIP. Effect sizes are clinically interpretable: 110–190 mL FEV1 differences, ~2.5–4% predicted. The episode proposes a conditional CFTR PheWAS direction: lock the Corvol-five lead SNPs as a fixed positive-control panel for any modifier-locus PheWAS, with re-detection of the lung-severity association at each locus as the pipeline rejection criterion.

Methods to lift

  • Cross-consortium phenotype harmonization — pool extreme-of-phenotype-recruited (GMS) and population-based (CGS, FrGMS) cohorts onto a single KNoRMA score so meta-analysis is well-defined; eligibility restricted to PI patients or two-severe-CFTR-mutation patients.
  • Random-effects meta-analysis across 13 genotype-platform-by-cohort subgroups — handles cross-platform/cross-cohort effect-size heterogeneity that fixed-effects would assume away.
  • Imputation up to ~8.5M SNPs — per-cohort imputation against 1000 Genomes / HRC reference panels with platform-appropriate quality filters; imputation-quality r² >0.005 / >MAF threshold for inclusion.
  • HLA-region direct genotyping confirmation — five top imputed SNPs at HLA-DRA directly genotyped in a subset of patients to validate imputation accuracy (98–100% concordance).
  • Eigensoft principal-component ancestry adjustment with relative-set unrelated PCA + projection — random member from each family used to compute PCs that are then projected onto the full sample to handle related individuals.
  • Conditional analysis at each genome-wide-significant locus — re-fit holding the lead SNP constant; persistent signal indicates allelic heterogeneity at SLC9A3 and EHF/APIP.
  • eQTL annotation at lead SNPs — cross-tissue eQTL look-ups identify HLA-DRB1/DRB5 (chr6) and APIP (chr11) as plausible effector genes.
  • Effect-size translation to FEV1 units — beta-coefficients converted to mL of FEV1 at 18-year-old female 50th-percentile reference for clinical interpretability.
  • Candidate-gene-era literature audit — every previously published CF-modifier candidate-gene SNP claim re-tested in the consortium meta-analysis; only SLC9A3 candidate-gene SNPs survive multiple-testing correction.

ALEX Background and purpose. By 2014, when Corvol and colleagues began assembling this meta-analysis, the CF modifier-genetics field had three problems. First, the only well-replicated common-variant modifier locus was chromosome eleven-p-thirteen near EHF and APIP, established by Wright 2011 in F508del homozygotes. Second, candidate-gene studies had reported associations at probably twenty other loci over the previous fifteen years, but most of those had small sample sizes, inconsistent phenotype definitions, and no formal replication; the field had no principled way to separate the real ones from the false positives. Third, each national consortium had its own genotyping platform and its own version of the lung-severity phenotype, making cross-cohort comparison ad hoc rather than systematic. The animating question of Corvol 2015 was: if we pool the three major international CF GWAS cohorts onto the same lung-severity phenotype and do a single meta-analysis with imputation up to the eight-million-variant scale, how many genome-wide-significant modifier loci can we confirm, and what does the catalog look like?

JORDAN Why is "pooling the three cohorts" non-trivial?

ALEX Because each cohort is a different design. The U.S. Gene Modifier Study, GMS, recruited mostly through extreme-of-phenotype sampling — investigators deliberately enrolled patients at the mild and severe ends of the lung-severity distribution, because that maximizes statistical power for any given sample size. The Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study, CGS, recruited a population-based sample. The French CF Modifier Consortium, FrGMS, recruited yet differently. Different genotyping platforms — the Illumina 610, 660, Omni, etc. — were used across cohorts and even within cohorts. Imputation reference panels varied by year. So the operational achievement of Corvol 2015 was harmonizing all of that to the point where a single meta-analysis could be defended.

ALEX Methods. The consortium pooled six thousand three hundred sixty-five CF patients across thirteen genotype-platform-by-cohort subgroups: U.S. GMS, Canadian CGS, French FrGMS, plus several smaller sites. Inclusion required documented pancreatic insufficiency or two severe CFTR mutations. Lung-severity phenotype was KNoRMA, the same Kulich-derived consortium standard Wright 2011 used and Zhou 2023 used — age-specific CF percentile of FEV1 averaged over three years, then mortality-adjusted. Each cohort genotyped on its native platform — Illumina 610, 660, OmniExpress, or similar — and imputed using one-thousand-genomes or HRC reference panels appropriate to its data, yielding eight million five hundred twenty thousand four hundred fifty-eight SNPs after quality control. Per-cohort GWAS used linear regression with principal components for ancestry adjustment, computed by Eigensoft on a maximal set of unrelated individuals, with one randomly chosen relative per family used to compute the PCs that were then projected onto the full sample. Genome-wide significance threshold was set at p less than five times ten to the minus eight, the standard. Random-effects meta-analysis pooled the thirteen subgroups to handle effect-size heterogeneity. The HLA locus on chromosome six required a quality-control extra step — five imputed SNPs there were directly genotyped in a subset of patients to confirm the imputation accuracy, with concordance ranging from ninety-eight to one hundred percent. Approximately five percent of the cohort was non-European-ancestry by principal-component analysis; sensitivity analyses restricted to the European-ancestry six thousand seventy-nine confirmed all five loci held.

JORDAN Push back: extreme-phenotype recruitment in GMS distorts effect-size estimation. Doesn't that bias the meta-analysis?

ALEX It biases effect-size estimates upward in the GMS subgroup specifically, yes, but the random-effects meta-analysis is designed to cope with exactly that — heterogeneity across subgroups is built into the model rather than assumed away. The authors check this with Cochran's Q test for heterogeneity; only the chromosome-three-q-twenty-nine MUC4/MUC20 locus shows significant heterogeneity at p equals 0.04, which they attribute to small sample size in some subgroups, not to a genuine biological difference.

ALEX Results, did they answer the question. Five loci reach genome-wide significance. Chromosome three-q-twenty-nine, encompassing the mucin genes MUC4 and MUC20, p equals 3.3 times ten to the minus eleven. Chromosome five-p-fifteen-point-three, near SLC9A3, the cation-proton antiporter that handles intestinal pH and ion transport, p equals 6.8 times ten to the minus twelve — the most significant signal in the paper. Chromosome six-p-twenty-one-point-three, the HLA Class II region, p equals 1.2 times ten to the minus eight. Chromosome eleven-p-twelve-to-thirteen, the EHF/APIP locus, p equals 1.9 times ten to the minus ten — confirming and improving on Wright 2011's original signal. And chromosome X-q-twenty-two-to-twenty-three, encompassing AGTR2 and SLC6A14, p equals 1.8 times ten to the minus nine. Effect sizes for individual SNPs are clinically interpretable when expressed in FEV1 units: the SLC9A3 lead variant rs57221529 corresponds to about a one-hundred-ninety-millilitre FEV1 difference, or about four percent predicted, in eighteen-year-old female patients at the fiftieth height percentile. The AGTR2/SLC6A14 lead variant rs5952223 corresponds to about one hundred ten millilitres or two-and-a-half percent predicted. Conditional analysis — re-running each locus while statistically holding the lead SNP constant — eliminated remaining signal at chromosomes three, six, and X, but left independent secondary signal at chromosomes five and eleven, suggesting allelic heterogeneity at SLC9A3 and EHF/APIP. eQTL look-ups at each locus showed that the chromosome-six lead SNPs regulate HLA-DRB1 and HLA-DRB5 expression, and the chromosome-eleven lead SNPs regulate APIP expression. The paper also surveyed every previously-published candidate-gene CF-modifier claim: only SLC9A3 candidate-gene SNPs replicated at genome-wide significance after multiplicity correction. Every other candidate-gene-era claim either failed to replicate or could not be evaluated because the SNP wasn't on the platform. So the question they posed was answered cleanly — five loci, replicated, with biologically plausible candidate genes underneath each one.
