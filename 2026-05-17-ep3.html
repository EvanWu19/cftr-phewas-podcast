Blurb

Boëlle, Debray, Guillot, Corvol and colleagues 2019 (Hepatology) analyzed 3,328 pancreatic-insufficient CF patients born after 1985 from the French CF Modifier Gene Study (NCT00037778) to estimate cumulative CF-related liver disease (CFLD) incidence with age and to evaluate UDCA treatment effect under instrumental-variable assumptions. CFLD cumulative incidence reached 32.2% (95% CI 29.7–35.2) by age 25, severe CFLD reached 10.2% (8.5–12.9) by age 30. Three independent risk factors in multivariable Cox regression: meconium-ileus history (HR 1.66, 1.36–2.01), F508del homozygosity (HR 1.17, 1.00–1.37), male sex (HR 1.15, 0.99–1.37). The highest-risk subgroup — male F508del-homozygous with meconium-ileus history (~4% of the cohort) — reached 33% CFLD by age 20 versus 17% in the lowest-risk subgroup. UDCA treatment showed no detectable effect on severe CFLD onset age under any of three instrumental-variable specifications (RMAD 0–2 years, all p > 0.5). The episode suggests one possible direction: a CFFPR-anchored CFLD analysis that first replicates the Boëlle clinical-risk-factor model in the US cohort, then layers in CFGP-WGS rare-variant burden tests for established hepatobiliary modifier genes, then extends to a CFLD-endpoint phenome-wide rare-variant scan.

ALEX Welcome to episode three. Today's CFF-cohort paper is Boëlle, Debray, Guillot, Corvol and colleagues, 2019, in Hepatology — "Cystic Fibrosis Liver Disease: Outcomes and Risk Factors in a Large Cohort of French Patients." First author Pierre-Yves Boëlle, biostatistician at Sorbonne Université in Paris; senior author Harriet Corvol, a CF clinician-scientist also in Paris. The study is built on the French CF Modifier Gene Study, a national CF cohort with 3,328 pancreatic-insufficient patients born after 1985. We're reading it because it is the largest and methodologically cleanest population-scale estimate of CF-related liver disease incidence, and because it precisely defines the meconium-ileus and F508del-homozygous risk-factor reference numbers that any CFF-cohort modifier-gene analysis of hepatobiliary endpoints will have to position against.

JORDAN All right. Stop right there. Define every term in that intro as if I have heard none of them. CFLD, UDCA, meconium ileus, all of it.

ALEX Yes. CF is cystic fibrosis. CFLD, CF-related liver disease, is the cluster of hepatobiliary problems that develop in some CF patients. The relevant pathology is focal biliary cirrhosis, which over time can extend into multilobular biliary cirrhosis, and then into portal hypertension — abnormally high pressure in the vein that drains the gut into the liver. Portal hypertension causes esophageal varices, which are dilated veins in the lower esophagus that can bleed catastrophically. Severe CFLD in this paper is defined as having cirrhosis, portal hypertension, or esophageal varices. UDCA is ursodeoxycholic acid — a bile acid drug that is widely prescribed to CF patients with any sign of liver involvement, on the rationale that it might slow progression to severe disease. The evidence base for it is weak; this paper is one of the larger evaluations. Meconium ileus, abbreviated MI, is intestinal blockage by meconium — the thick, sticky first stool — in CF newborns. It is itself a partial-CF-phenotype: about 15 percent of CF newborns have it, and a meconium ileus history at birth is one of the strongest predictors of which CF patients will go on to develop modifier-driven complications later in life. PI is pancreatic insufficiency, which means the pancreas isn't producing enough digestive enzymes; patients need enzyme replacement and are at higher risk for nutritional and metabolic complications. PS is pancreatic sufficient — the milder CF genotype. F508del homozygous means both copies of CFTR carry the F508del variant — the most severe and most common CF genotype. FEV1 is forced expiratory volume in one second; the most-used spirometry-based lung-function measure in CF. BMI is body mass index. The hazard ratio, HR, is the survival-analysis analog of an odds ratio: ratio of the instantaneous risk of an event in one group versus another, over time. CI is confidence interval. The Cox model is a regression for survival data where the outcome is time-to-event. Interval-censored data means you don't know the exact day the event happened, only that it happened between two observed visits. RMAD is restricted mean age difference — a measure used in this paper for the effect of a treatment on the age at onset of an event, restricted to a maximum age. Instrumental variable analysis is an econometric trick for estimating causal effects when treatment assignment is confounded — you find a third variable that affects treatment but doesn't affect the outcome through any other path, and you use it as a proxy. The CFF Patient Registry, abbreviated CFFPR, is the United States analog of the French CF Modifier Gene Study; it is the registry Runyu works with. ICD codes are International Classification of Diseases codes. WGS is whole-genome sequencing. CFGP is the CF Genome Project, the planned CFF-anchored WGS resource.

JORDAN Why is the field asking these questions about liver disease now?

ALEX Because CFLD is the third-leading cause of death in CF after respiratory failure and post-transplant complications, accounting for about 2.5 percent of CF mortality. Yet basic epidemiology has been a mess for decades. Reported CFLD prevalence in the literature ranges from 2 percent to 68 percent — a 35-fold spread — depending on how you define it, how old the cohort is, and how aggressively you ultrasound your patients. There is no consensus on how often CFLD progresses to severe disease, no consensus on whether UDCA prevents progression, and no consensus on what the modifier risk factors are. The clinically actionable open questions are: who do you screen, how often, with what tools, and do you treat with UDCA pre-emptively? The previous best evidence was a 177-patient Colombo cohort, an Italian study with a much narrower age range that suggested a plateau in late childhood, contradicted by adult-onset CFLD case series that suggested liver disease keeps appearing in adulthood. The Boëlle paper was built to settle the cumulative-incidence-versus-age curve at population scale and to evaluate UDCA without the protopathic bias that contaminates simple observational comparison. Protopathic bias here is the situation where a drug appears harmful only because it tends to be prescribed to people who are already on a worse disease trajectory — the apparent harm is the indication, not the drug.

JORDAN How did they do it?

ALEX They pulled all 3,328 PI patients born after 1985 from the French CF Modifier Gene Study (NCT00037778), with retrospective record review before 2004 and prospective follow-up after. CFLD was defined using the European best-practice criteria of Debray and colleagues: at least two of clinical (hepatomegaly or splenomegaly), biochemical (elevated transaminases or gamma-GT), and ultrasonographic (heterogeneous echogenicity, portal hypertension, biliary abnormalities) signs. Severe CFLD required at least one of cirrhosis, portal hypertension, or esophageal varices. Patients with no CFLD information in their record were considered unaffected, a conservative choice that biases the cumulative incidence slightly downward. For statistical analysis: cumulative incidence of CFLD with age was estimated nonparametrically via the maximum-likelihood estimator for interval-censored data — interval-censored because the event date was only known to lie between two visits. Cox regression adapted to interval-censored data was used to test risk factors. Confidence intervals were bootstrapped. For UDCA they couldn't do a randomized trial — almost everyone with any liver hint is treated — so they used instrumental variable analysis: birth cohort (before vs after 1995) and CF-center prescribing pattern (early vs late) as the instruments, on the grounds that the year you were born and which CF center you happened to live near affect your UDCA exposure but should not directly affect your CFLD trajectory. The treatment effect was summarized as a restricted-mean-age-difference at severe CFLD onset. Lung function and nutrition were compared across CFLD strata with Kruskal-Wallis. ALEX pushes back on one design choice: defining the no-record patients as unaffected is acceptable but it does mean the absolute incidence numbers are conservative; the *relative* risk factors are likely unbiased but the headline 32.2 percent should be read as a lower bound for that cohort.

JORDAN And the numbers?

ALEX Cumulative CFLD incidence reached 32.2 percent by age 25 with a 95 percent confidence interval of 29.7 to 35.2 percent. Severe CFLD was rare before age 5, then rose at roughly 0.4 percent per year, reaching 10.2 percent by age 30 with a 95 percent CI of 8.5 to 12.9 percent. Three risk factors independently predicted CFLD in multivariable Cox regression: male sex with hazard ratio 1.15 (95 percent CI 0.99 to 1.37, borderline), F508del homozygosity with HR 1.17 (1.00 to 1.37, borderline), and meconium-ileus history with HR 1.66 (1.36 to 2.01, clearly significant). For severe CFLD, male sex was a stronger predictor at HR 1.48 (1.10 to 2.08); F508del homozygosity and meconium ileus were weaker for severe CFLD than for CFLD overall. The highest-risk subgroup — male F508del-homozygous patients with a meconium-ileus history, about 4 percent of the cohort — had a 33 percent cumulative CFLD incidence by age 20, against 17 percent in the lowest-risk subgroup. For UDCA: the restricted-mean-age-difference at severe CFLD onset was essentially zero under all three instruments — 2 plus-or-minus 3.5 years using birth cohort (p = 0.56), 0 plus-or-minus 1.0 years using CF center (p = 0.98), 0 plus-or-minus 0.8 using both combined (p = 0.95). Translation: no detectable causal effect of UDCA on severe CFLD onset age. CFLD patients had worse FEV1 and lower BMI than non-CFLD patients; severe CFLD patients worse still — FEV1-percent-predicted 75 / 71 / 63 across no-CFLD, CFLD, severe-CFLD strata; BMI Z-score −0.48 / −0.57 / −0.92. Did they answer their questions? On the cumulative-incidence-with-age curve, fully. On the risk-factor structure, fully. On UDCA, the answer is a strong null: no evidence of benefit on severe CFLD onset under any instrument. They acknowledge this raises an ethical question about prescribing UDCA to young children given its known adverse-effect profile.

JORDAN One possible direction this episode suggests?

ALEX A CFFPR-anchored CFLD phenotype-PheWAS analysis. Plain English: take the CFF Patient Registry, define a CFLD endpoint using the same European Debray-style criteria Boëlle used so the numbers are directly comparable, and run a CFTR-modifier-PheWAS using the CFF Genome Project WGS — but with three layers: layer one, replicate the Boëlle clinical-risk-factor model (male sex, F508del-homozygosity, meconium-ileus history) in CFFPR to confirm the US cohort behaves like the French; layer two, add modifier-gene burden tests for previously reported CFLD candidates such as SERPINA1, GSTP1, and TGFB1; layer three, run a CFLD-as-outcome PheWAS sweep across all WGS-callable variants. Formal one-sentence version: "Using CFF Patient Registry CFLD phenotypes defined per Debray 2011 European best practice, replicate the Boëlle 2019 clinical-risk-factor multivariable model in the US cohort, then layer in a CFTR Genome Project WGS rare-variant burden test for established hepatobiliary modifier genes, then extend to a CFLD-endpoint phenome-wide rare-variant scan."
