How did this episode land for you?

Blurb

Blackman, Commander, Watson, and the full Cystic Fibrosis Gene Modifier Consortium roster — Hopkins, UNC Chapel Hill, Toronto Sick Kids, Case Western Reserve, with senior author Garry Cutting — published in Diabetes in October 2013 the discovery paper for SLC26A9 as a genome-wide-significant modifier of CF-related diabetes onset. The genome-wide association study pooled 3,059 CF individuals (644 with CFRD) across the Twin and Sibling Study, the Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study, and the Genetic Modifier Study lung-and-liver subsamples, with a separate non-overlapping replication sample of 694 CF individuals (124 with CFRD). The headline result: two SNPs on chromosome 1 (rs4077468 and rs4077469, in complete LD) within and 5'-to SLC26A9 — hazard ratio 1.38 per copy of the A allele in discovery (P=3.6×10⁻⁸), HR 1.47 in replication (P=0.007), combined HR 1.39 across 3,753 individuals (P=9.8×10⁻¹⁰). The candidate-T2D-locus arm replicated TCF7L2 (combined P=3.8×10⁻⁶) and identified sub-significant CFRD signal at CDKN2A/B (P=5.1×10⁻⁵), CDKAL1 (P=1.6×10⁻³ and P=1.9×10⁻⁴), and IGF2BP2 (P=4.2×10⁻³) — all in the same direction as the established T2D risk allele. The five loci together account for 8.3 percent of phenotypic variance in CFRD onset and a population-attributable risk of 68 percent. The SLC26A9 effect is independent of meconium ileus, liver disease, and female sex, and the SLC26A9 locus shows no T2D signal in the DIAGRAM consortium summary statistics — meaning SLC26A9 is a CF-specific modifier, not a T2D risk locus. The proposed direction this episode suggests, conditional on later inspection of CFGP WGS variant calls and CFFPR phecode catalog linkage, is a pre-registered phenome-wide pleiotropy scan of the locked CFRD-modifier-locus list (SLC26A9, TCF7L2, CDKN2A/B, CDKAL1, IGF2BP2, plus PTMA from Aksit 2020) against the rest of the CF complication phenome — lung function trajectory, infection burden, mental health, transplant outcomes, modulator response, and mortality — none of which has been systematically tested against this locus list using modern CFGP WGS data, leaving Aksit 2022's MI-dimension pleiotropy work as the most recent partial closure of one slice. The open question is whether the CFRD-modifier-locus pleiotropy signal extends into the modulator era, where Trikafta-treated CF patients have substantially restored CFTR function and the modifier-effect baseline may be quite different from the pre-modulator CFGP data Blackman 2013 was built on.

Plain-English glossary

  • Bonferroni correction — a conservative multiple-testing correction that divides the desired significance threshold (typically 0.05) by the number of independent tests performed; the genome-wide-significant Bonferroni threshold here is 0.05 / 549,869 SNPs = 9.1×10⁻⁸.
  • CFRD (CF-related diabetes) — a form of diabetes that develops in CF patients, featuring beta-cell dysfunction with generally preserved insulin sensitivity; clinically distinct from both type 1 and type 2 diabetes.
  • CFTR — the gene that, when both copies are damaged, causes cystic fibrosis; encodes a chloride and bicarbonate channel in epithelial cells throughout the airway, pancreas, sweat glands, intestine, and reproductive tract.
  • Cystic Fibrosis Gene Modifier Consortium — a multi-institution collaboration spanning Hopkins, UNC Chapel Hill, Toronto Sick Kids, and Case Western Reserve, formed to pool CF patient cohorts at scale sufficient for genome-wide modifier-gene discovery.
  • Cox proportional hazards model — a statistical regression model used in time-to-event analysis; estimates how each covariate (here, an SNP genotype) multiplicatively scales the per-unit-time hazard of the event (CFRD diagnosis).
  • DIAGRAM consortium — the DIAbetes Genetics Replication And Meta-analysis consortium, a large international collaboration that pooled type-2-diabetes GWAS results across many cohorts; its published summary statistics serve as a reference for whether a putative T2D locus has been seen in the general population.
  • F508del / p.Phe508del — the most common CFTR pathogenic variant; a three-base-pair deletion removing one phenylalanine residue at amino acid position 508. Roughly 70 percent of CF chromosomes worldwide.
  • GWAS (genome-wide association study) — a scan of all common variants across the genome for statistical association with a trait.
  • Hazard ratio (HR) — the standard measure of effect size in time-to-event analysis; an HR of 1.4 means each copy of the risk allele increases the per-unit-time hazard of the event by 40 percent.
  • Imputation — statistical inference of untyped genetic variants from a reference haplotype panel; used here in a one-megabase window around SLC26A9 to assess fine-scale association beyond the directly-genotyped SNPs.
  • Linkage disequilibrium (LD) — the statistical tendency of two nearby genetic variants to be inherited together; rs4077468 and rs4077469 are in complete LD in the discovery sample, meaning their genotypes are perfectly correlated and statistically indistinguishable.
  • MACH / Minimac — a pair of widely-used statistical genotype-imputation software packages; MACH does the haplotype phasing, Minimac does the actual imputation against a reference panel.
  • Meconium ileus (MI) — perinatal-onset CF complication in which the infant's first stool (meconium) is too thick to pass and obstructs the intestine.
  • Meta-analysis Z-statistic — a method for combining association results across multiple cohorts by taking a weighted sum of per-cohort Z-scores, with weights inversely proportional to per-cohort standard errors.
  • Modifier gene — a gene other than the CF disease-causing gene CFTR that influences the severity or pattern of CF complications among CF patients.
  • PLINK — a widely-used open-source software package for GWAS data handling and standard statistical analyses.
  • Population-attributable risk — the fraction of cases of a disease in a population that would not have occurred if all the risk alleles being studied were absent; the five-locus combined population-attributable risk for CFRD here is 68 percent.
  • Population stratification — the confounding effect that arises when ancestry differences between cases and controls produce false-positive association signals; controlled here by including ancestry principal components as covariates.
  • Principal components (PCs) — statistical summary axes that capture the dominant patterns of genetic variation in a cohort; the top few PCs typically reflect ancestry and are included as covariates in any GWAS.
  • Proportional hazards assumption — the modeling assumption that the effect of a covariate on the hazard is constant over time; tested here using time-dependence of Schoenfeld residuals.
  • SLC26A9 — Solute Carrier family 26 member 9; an epithelial chloride and bicarbonate channel that physically interacts with CFTR; the headline CFRD modifier locus discovered here.
  • SNP (single-nucleotide polymorphism) — a single-letter variant present at meaningful frequency in the population.
  • TaqMan Assays-on-Demand — a commercial single-SNP genotyping platform from Applied Biosystems, used here for the replication sample where only specific candidate SNPs needed to be typed.
  • TCF7L2 — Transcription Factor 7 Like 2; a Wnt-signaling transcription factor with strong roles in pancreatic beta-cell function; the strongest single-gene T2D risk locus in the general population, and a replicated CFRD modifier here.
  • T2D (type 2 diabetes) — the common form of diabetes in the general population, characterized by insulin resistance plus eventual beta-cell dysfunction.
  • Twin and Sibling Study (TSS) / Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study (CGS) / Genetic Modifier Study (GMS) — the three constituent CF cohorts pooled to form the discovery sample.

Methods to lift

  • Cystic Fibrosis Gene Modifier Consortium pooled-cohort design — pooling Twin and Sibling Study (Hopkins) + Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study (Toronto) + Genetic Modifier Study lung-and-liver subsamples (UNC + Case Western) into a single discovery sample, with study-specific Z-scores combined via inverse-variance-weighted meta-analysis to handle inter-cohort effect heterogeneity.
  • Cox proportional hazards model with CFRD age of onset as time-to-event endpoint — captures the age-dependent nature of CFRD better than binary-CFRD logistic regression; uses censoring at age of most recent diabetes screening test for non-cases.
  • Two-tier covariate-adjustment design — unadjusted analysis with three to eight ancestry principal components selected by Scree plot, plus an "adjusted" analysis additionally including female sex and liver disease as covariates (both established CFRD risk factors).
  • Proportional hazards assumption verification via Schoenfeld residuals — the Stata estat phtest command; reportable as a per-SNP QC step for any survival-GWAS publication.
  • Heterogeneity assessment via I² and Weibull-with-shared-frailty fallback — when between-cohort heterogeneity exceeds I² ≥ 25 percent, re-fit using a Weibull model with shared frailty to allow study-specific effect heterogeneity rather than assuming pooled effect homogeneity.
  • Conditional analysis at the lead SNP — re-fitting the regional model with the lead SNP as a covariate to test for residual independent association signals at the locus; rules out locus heterogeneity. Here, conditioning on rs4077468 reduced all other SLC26A9-region SNP associations to P > 0.05.
  • Imputation in a one-megabase locus window — used MACH and Minimac with the August 2010 release of the 1000 Genomes Project as the reference panel; produced 1,567 high-quality imputed SNPs (R² > 0.3) for the SLC26A9 region.
  • Conservative significance thresholds — Bonferroni-corrected genome-wide threshold of P < 9.1×10⁻⁸ for primary discovery (P = 0.05 / 549,869 SNPs); P < 0.004 for the candidate-T2D-locus arm (P = 0.05 / 12 SNPs tested).
  • Cross-trait look-up against published external GWAS summary statistics — querying the DIAGRAM consortium T2D summary statistics for the SLC26A9 locus to test CF-specificity of the modifier signal; the no-T2D-signal-at-SLC26A9 finding is what licenses the "CF-specific modifier" claim.
  • Genetic risk-score construction across multiple loci — summing the number of high-risk alleles across the five detected modifier loci into a per-individual integer score, then stratifying CFRD prevalence by score; ranged from 11 percent CFRD in patients with 0–1 risk alleles to 40 percent CFRD in patients with 8–9 risk alleles.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFGP WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage:

A pre-registered phenome-wide pleiotropy scan of the locked CFRD-modifier-locus list (SLC26A9, TCF7L2, CDKN2A/B, CDKAL1, IGF2BP2, plus PTMA from Aksit 2020) in the CFGP WGS cohort, against the locked CFFPR phecode catalog excluding the already-tested CFRD and MI endpoints (i.e., lung function trajectory, Pseudomonas aeruginosa and other infection-burden phenotypes, mental health, transplant outcomes, CFTR-modulator response, and mortality), with the locus list, the lead-variant identifiers, the phecode list, the SAIGE-GENE+ or equivalent test framework, the multiple-testing-corrected per-locus-per-phecode threshold, and the per-CFTR-genotype-class stratification (F508del homozygous vs. F508del compound heterozygous vs. rare non-F508del) all pre-registered in a publicly-deposited analysis plan before any test is run; per-locus and per-phecode effect sizes reported with confidence intervals and forest plots as the primary outcome.

What to verify first

  • Whether the SLC26A9 lead variants rs4077468 and rs4077469 have been re-genotyped in the CFGP WGS cohort and whether the same rs-identifiers map cleanly to the current GRCh38 reference build (the 2013 paper used NCBI build 36.3 coordinates).
  • Whether the locked CFRD-modifier-locus list as we have it is current — whether subsequent publications between Blackman 2013 and 2026 have added or refined CFRD modifier loci that are not on the list.
  • Whether the CFFPR phecode catalog has been pre-mapped from registry-encoded fields to standard phecodes for the lung-function, infection-burden, mental-health, transplant, modulator-response, and mortality endpoints; case counts per phecode to assess per-test power.
  • Whether a consortium has already attempted any subset of this pleiotropy scan — for example, a SLC26A9 pleiotropy scan beyond the MI dimension covered by Aksit 2022 — that the design here should build on rather than duplicate.
  • Whether the modulator-era stratification is feasible at adequate per-stratum sample size — the proportion of CFGP WGS participants on Trikafta or other highly-effective modulators by the relevant follow-up window may make a clean pre/post-modulator analysis under-powered.
  • Whether the per-CFTR-genotype-class stratification preserves adequate per-stratum sample size — F508del homozygotes are the largest stratum and the within-stratum analysis is best-powered, but rare non-F508del strata may be too small for confident per-locus modifier estimates.
  • Whether the non-European-ancestry generalizability of the SLC26A9 effect — Blackman 2013 cohort was 92 percent Caucasian — has been examined in any subsequent paper, and whether ancestry-stratified re-analysis is needed for the pleiotropy scan.

Gap-map: closes / leaves open

Closes:

  • The CFRD-onset GWAS discovery question for SLC26A9 (definitively, combined P=9.8×10⁻¹⁰ across 3,753 individuals).
  • The CFRD-onset replication of TCF7L2 from prior work in the consortium.
  • The CFRD/T2D shared-genetic-architecture question for TCF7L2, CDKAL1, CDKN2A/B, IGF2BP2 (yes, shared in direction at sub-genome-wide significance).
  • The CF-specificity question for SLC26A9 (no T2D signal in DIAGRAM at SLC26A9 — the modifier signal is CF-specific, not a T2D risk locus repackaged).
  • The SLC26A9-versus-MI-and-liver-disease independence question (independent of both, plus female sex).

Leaves open:

  • Pleiotropy of the locked CFRD-modifier-locus list across the rest of the CF complication phenome — only the MI dimension was added by Aksit 2022, leaving lung function, infection burden, mental health, transplant, modulator response, and mortality un-tested.
  • Modulator-era persistence of the SLC26A9 effect — Blackman 2013 entirely predates Trikafta and whether SLC26A9-driven CFRD risk persists under highly-effective CFTR modulator therapy is unaddressed.
  • Rare-variant burden architecture at the SLC26A9 locus — Blackman 2013 is common-variant GWAS on Illumina array data plus imputation; the rare-variant trait-specificity layer that Aksit 2022 added on the WGS substrate is part of the more recent story.
  • Non-European-ancestry generalizability — 92 percent Caucasian cohort, no per-ancestry-stratum analysis; the question of whether the SLC26A9 effect transfers into the more-admixed contemporary CFGP cohort documented by Li 2011 is open.
  • Functional mechanism of SLC26A9 in pancreatic ductal cells — proposed but not functionally validated in the 2013 paper; the mechanism for how SLC26A9 variants modulate CFRD onset rather than CFRD presence remains an open biological question.

The paper appeared in Diabetes in October 2013, titled "Genetic Modifiers of Cystic Fibrosis-Related Diabetes." We are reading it because it is the discovery paper for SLC26A9 as a genome-wide-significant modifier of CF-related diabetes onset — a result that has been showing up repeatedly in our prior episodes as the primary citation, and one that the modern CFGP WGS work by Aksit and others built on directly.

JORDAN Stop. I want a vocabulary pass before we go in. There is a lot of CF-specific language here.

ALEX Yes. Let me lay them all out plainly. CFTR — the Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Conductance Regulator — is the gene that, when both copies are damaged, causes cystic fibrosis. The protein it encodes is a chloride and bicarbonate channel that sits on the surface of epithelial cells throughout the airway, pancreas, sweat glands, intestine, and reproductive tract. Cystic fibrosis-related diabetes, abbreviated CFRD, is a distinct form of diabetes that develops in CF patients — clinically it looks somewhat like type 1 diabetes because it features a subacute decline in pancreatic beta-cell function, but it differs from type 1 in that it is not autoimmune, and it differs from type 2 diabetes in that insulin sensitivity is generally preserved. CFRD affects roughly 19 percent of CF adolescents and 40 to 50 percent of CF adults. Type 2 diabetes, abbreviated T2D, is the common form of diabetes in the general population, characterized by insulin resistance plus eventual beta-cell dysfunction. F508del — also written p.Phe508del — is the most common CFTR pathogenic variant; a three-base-pair deletion that removes one phenylalanine residue at amino acid position 508 of the CFTR protein, accounting for roughly 70 percent of CF chromosomes worldwide. A modifier gene is a gene other than the CF disease-causing gene CFTR that influences the severity or pattern of CF complications among CF patients. A genome-wide association study, abbreviated GWAS, scans all common variants across the genome for statistical association with a trait. A single-nucleotide polymorphism, abbreviated SNP, is a single-letter variant present at meaningful frequency in the population. A hazard ratio, abbreviated HR, is the standard measure of effect size in time-to-event analysis — a hazard ratio of 1.4 means each copy of the risk allele increases the per-unit-time risk of the event happening by 40 percent. Linkage disequilibrium, abbreviated LD, is the statistical tendency of two nearby genetic variants to be inherited together as a unit, which is why a single GWAS hit usually marks a region of co-inherited variants rather than the single causal letter. Imputation refers to statistical inference of untyped genetic variants from a reference haplotype panel. The CF Gene Modifier Study, abbreviated GMS, the Twin and Sibling Study, abbreviated TSS, and the Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study, abbreviated CGS, are the three constituent CF cohorts that pooled their data to form the discovery sample. Meconium ileus, abbreviated MI, is a perinatal-onset complication of CF in which the infant's first stool — meconium — is too thick to pass and obstructs the intestine. Population-attributable risk is the fraction of cases of a disease that would not have occurred in the population if all the risk alleles being studied were absent. With those terms in hand, we can talk about the paper.

JORDAN What was the problem they were solving — what was unknown before this paper?

ALEX Two specific gaps. First: prior work had established that CFRD risk has a substantial genetic-modifier component. A 2009 twin and sibling study from the same Hopkins group — Blackman 2009, which we have not yet read in this podcast but is in our CFTR-WGS reading list — had shown that family history of type 2 diabetes in non-CF family members approximately triples the risk of CFRD in the CF patient, suggesting shared genetic architecture between CFRD and T2D. The same group had shown that the T2D risk allele in TCF7L2 — Wnt-signaling transcription factor with strong roles in pancreatic beta-cell function — modified CFRD risk in 1,745 CF individuals. But no genome-wide unbiased scan for CFRD modifier loci had been published. Second: the broader question of how much of the genetic architecture of CFRD overlaps with the genetic architecture of T2D in the general population was unresolved. Was CFRD essentially a CF-specific accelerator of T2D-like beta-cell biology, in which case T2D modifier loci should transfer? Or was CFRD a fundamentally different beta-cell disease driven by CF-specific mechanisms, in which case T2D modifier loci should not transfer? Before this paper, neither possibility had been ruled out at GWAS scale.

JORDAN How did they actually do it? Mechanically.

ALEX They assembled a discovery sample of 3,059 CF individuals — 644 of whom had CFRD — pooled across the three Cystic Fibrosis Gene Modifier Consortium constituent studies: the Twin and Sibling Study, the Canadian CF Gene Modifier Study, and the Genetic Modifier Study lung-and-liver subsamples. They added a separate non-overlapping replication sample of 694 CF individuals — 124 with CFRD — from the same parent studies. CFRD was defined as clinician-diagnosed CFRD plus insulin treatment for at least one year, with age of CFRD onset extracted from clinic records. SNP genotyping in the discovery sample used the Illumina 610-Quad platform; quality control filtered down to 549,869 SNPs across chromosomes 1 through 22 and the X chromosome. Replication-sample SNPs were genotyped one at a time using TaqMan Assays-on-Demand. They imputed additional SNPs in a one-megabase window around the SLC26A9 locus using MACH and Minimac with the August 2010 release of the 1000 Genomes Project as the reference panel. Statistical analysis used a Cox proportional hazards model with CFRD age of onset as the time-to-event endpoint, with three to eight ancestry principal components as covariates in the unadjusted model and additional adjustment for female sex and liver disease in the adjusted model. The three discovery cohort results were combined using a meta-analysis Z-statistic with weights inversely proportional to standard error. Significance thresholds: a Bonferroni-corrected genome-wide threshold of P below 9.1×10⁻⁸ for primary discovery, and P below 0.004 for the candidate-gene tests of seven established type-2-diabetes loci. ALEX should push back on a design choice here. The candidate-T2D-locus arm tested seven loci against CFRD with a Bonferroni correction across twelve SNPs, using the eight earliest replicated T2D loci minus FTO — which they explicitly excluded because the FTO T2D effect appears mediated by BMI, and CFRD patients are typically not obese. That FTO exclusion is principled but introduces a degree of investigator choice into the candidate panel; a more rigorously pre-registered modern design would either include FTO and report the null or list the exclusion criterion in advance.

JORDAN What did they actually find — what is the headline?

ALEX The headline is two clean genome-wide-significant CFRD modifier signals, plus replication of T2D candidate loci. The genome-wide signal: two SNPs on chromosome 1, rs4077468 and rs4077469 — in complete linkage disequilibrium in the discovery sample — within and 5-prime to SLC26A9, with a hazard ratio of 1.38 per copy of the A allele in the discovery sample, a 95 percent confidence interval of 1.23 to 1.54, and a P-value of 3.6×10⁻⁸. Replication in the 694-person separate cohort: hazard ratio 1.47, confidence interval 1.11 to 1.94, P-value 0.007 — same direction, similar magnitude. Combined meta-analysis across all 3,753 individuals: hazard ratio 1.39, confidence interval 1.25 to 1.54, P-value 9.8×10⁻¹⁰. The candidate-T2D-locus arm: TCF7L2 rs7903146 replicated as a CFRD modifier with combined hazard ratio 1.31 and P-value 3.8×10⁻⁶. Three additional T2D loci reached the candidate-arm significance threshold: CDKN2A/B at rs1412829 with P-value 5.1×10⁻⁵; CDKAL1 at rs7754840 with P-value 1.6×10⁻³ and rs7756992 with P-value 1.9×10⁻⁴; and IGF2BP2 at rs1470579 with P-value 4.2×10⁻³. Direction of effect was the same as in T2D for every candidate locus. Combined contribution: the five detected modifier loci each affected CFRD hazard ratio by 1.2- to 1.4-fold, together accounting for 8.3 percent of the phenotypic variance in CFRD onset and a population-attributable risk of 68 percent. They also showed that the SLC26A9 effect was independent of meconium ileus, liver disease, and female sex — adjustment for any of those covariates did not attenuate the SLC26A9 hazard ratio — and crucially they queried published DIAGRAM consortium T2D GWAS summary statistics for the SLC26A9 locus and found no T2D signal there. SLC26A9 is a CF-specific modifier, not a T2D risk locus. Did they answer the question they posed? Yes, fully on the discovery side — SLC26A9 is the first genome-wide-significant CFRD modifier — and yes on the architecture-overlap side — CFRD shares genetic risk with T2D at TCF7L2, CDKN2A/B, CDKAL1, IGF2BP2 (the T2D-via-beta-cell loci) but has CF-specific modifier biology at SLC26A9 (the CFTR-pathway-adjacent locus).
