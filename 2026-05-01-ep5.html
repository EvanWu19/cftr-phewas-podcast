Blurb

Wang and colleagues at AstraZeneca's Centre for Genomics Research ran a phenome-wide rare-variant scan on 281,104 UK Biobank exomes (269,171 European-ancestry analysis cohort), testing 18,762 genes against 18,780 phenotypes (17,361 binary, 1,419 quantitative) using gene-level collapsing analysis across twelve qualifying-variant masks. They derived a study-wide significance threshold of p less than or equal to 2 times 10 to the minus 9 from a synonymous-collapsing empirical null and an n-of-1 permutation null, and surfaced 1,703 significant gene-phenotype associations (936 binary, 767 quantitative) with median binary OR of 12.4. The headline methodological result: 83 percent of significant binary associations were undetectable by single-variant ExWAS at the same threshold, demonstrating the power of collapsing in the high-allelic-heterogeneity regime. Summary statistics are released as a free public PheWAS portal at http://azphewas.com/ — a natural external replication tier for any rare-variant analysis.

Methods to lift

  • Gene-level collapsing analysis as the primary inferential frame for rare variants — one test per gene per mask, comparing carrier proportions in cases vs controls (Fisher's exact at the gene level).
  • Twelve qualifying-variant masks: 10 dominant non-synonymous models, 1 recessive model, 1 synonymous-only model as empirical negative control. Masks vary on three axes — MAF cap (private to 5 percent), predicted consequence (PTV-only vs PTV+damaging missense), and missense pathogenicity filters (REVEL + MTR thresholds). For our scaled-down CFF WGS run: synonymous null + PTV-only + PTV+damaging-missense at MAF<0.1 percent + ultra-rare cut.
  • PTV definition from SnpEff: exon-loss, frameshift, start-lost, stop-gained, stop-lost, splice-acceptor, splice-donor, gene fusion, bidirectional gene fusion, rare amino-acid variant, transcript ablation.
  • Study-wide significance threshold via two converging methods: empirical null from synonymous-collapsing model, and n-of-1 phenotype permutation. Avoids naive Bonferroni; both gave p <= 2e-9.
  • Lambda genomic inflation factor reporting per phenotype, excluding exome-wide significant genes (P < 2.6e-6) before lambda calculation.
  • Phenotype scaffolding via PEACOK (PHESANT fork) — handles ICD-10 tree, union-of-cases across related codes, sex-specific case-control rebalancing.
  • Quality-control and ancestry-pruning hygiene as a precondition for using Fisher's exact (no native covariate adjustment): peddy v0.4.2 ancestry calls with Pr(European) > 0.99, +/-4 SD trim on top 4 PCs.
  • Benchmarking framework: cross-validate Fisher against SAIGE-SPA and REGENIE 2.0.2 on a single ICD chapter to demonstrate calibration before scaling to all phenotypes.
  • Drug-target enrichment as a downstream sanity check (DrugBank-derived list + Informa Pharmaprojects Approved/Phase I-III).
  • AZ-PheWAS portal (http://azphewas.com/) — public summary-statistics interface, queryable per gene or per phenotype.

Replication strategy note

AZ-PheWAS is a free, public PheWAS resource on 281K UKBB exomes that already contains roughly 11,000 CFTR carriers (carrier frequency ~1/25 in northern Europeans). For any CFTR-anchored hit or pleiotropic signal we surface from the CFF WGS analysis, we will query the AZ-PheWAS portal as the first-tier external replication and pleiotropy lookup before pursuing more expensive replication routes.

The two approaches converged, which is the paper's quiet flex.

JORDAN Stop me. The thing I want my fellows to actually understand is collapsing analysis versus single-variant ExWAS. Walk me through the difference, with the Wang masks.

ALEX Sure. Single-variant ExWAS is what it sounds like — Fisher's exact or regression at every variant, one at a time. It's well-powered for common variants, useless at MAF below maybe one in a thousand because you have nobody in the rare cell. Collapsing flips it: for each gene, you define a qualifying variant rule — say, "any protein-truncating variant at MAF below 0.1 percent" — and then you ask whether the count of carriers of any qualifying variant is enriched in cases versus controls. One test per gene per mask.

JORDAN And the masks are the policy choice.

ALEX The masks are the entire policy choice. Wang ran twelve. Ten dominant non-synonymous models, one recessive model, and one synonymous-only model that serves as the empirical negative control. The dominant models vary along three axes: allele-frequency cap (private up to a max of 5 percent), predicted consequence (PTV-only versus PTV plus damaging missense), and missense pathogenicity filters using REVEL and MTR scores. The recessive model requires two qualifying alleles, homozygous or compound-het, and qualifies hemizygous X-chromosome calls.

JORDAN And PTV is defined how?

ALEX From SnpEff annotations: exon-loss, frameshift, start-lost, stop-gained, stop-lost, splice-acceptor, splice-donor, gene fusion, bidirectional gene fusion, rare amino-acid variant, and transcript ablation. Standard PTV grab-bag, nothing exotic. Damaging missense gets layered on top with REVEL plus MTR thresholds depending on the model. The full spec is Extended Data Table 1.

JORDAN OK. Now the part that matters for whether collapsing is even worth doing.

ALEX Yes. The 1,703 statistically significant gene-phenotype associations they call out have a median odds ratio of 12.4 for binary traits and a median absolute beta of 0.35 for quantitative traits. And critically, 83 percent of those binary associations were undetectable by single-variant ExWAS at the same threshold. That's the killer chart. When you have allelic heterogeneity — many different rare hits in the same gene, each individually too rare to call — collapsing aggregates the signal and single-variant analysis cannot.

JORDAN So why pick collapsing-Fisher over LMM?

ALEX Their argument is twofold. One: at very low MAF, exact tests are robust where regression can be unstable. Two: Fisher was the most computationally efficient of the three. The trade-off is that Fisher has no native covariate adjustment, so you have to handle confounding by aggressive ancestry pruning and case-control harmonisation up-front. They are explicit that if you can't do that hygiene, you should use a regression-based method.
