Blurb

The prequel to Aksit 2020. Blackman and colleagues at the CF Modifier Consortium ran the original CFRD genome-wide association study in 3,059 CF individuals (644 CFRD cases) with replication in 694 (124 cases). They discovered SLC26A9 as a CFRD modifier locus (HR 1.38, combined P = 9.8e-10), replicated TCF7L2, and showed that three additional general-population type 2 diabetes risk loci (CDKAL1, CDKN2A/B, IGF2BP2) also influence CFRD risk. Five loci together account for 8.3% of phenotypic variance and 68% population-attributable risk. This is the paper that put SLC26A9 on the CFRD map and established the T2D-CFRD common-variant overlap that Aksit 2020 then expanded into a polygenic-risk-score architecture and a 5,740-individual cohort with the addition of PTMA.

Gap-map: closes

  • SLC26A9 as a CFRD modifier locus — first genome-wide-significant evidence, with mechanistic plausibility through CFTR-SLC26A9 apical-membrane coupling.
  • T2D-CFRD common-variant overlap established — TCF7L2 replicated, plus CDKAL1, CDKN2A/B, IGF2BP2 nominated as shared CFRD/T2D risk loci.
  • Sample-size proof-of-principle — ~3K CF individuals with deeply ascertained CFRD phenotype is sufficient for genome-wide significance under a time-to-event design. Quantifies the combined common-variant contribution at 8.3% phenotypic variance / 68% population-attributable risk.
  • Hazard-ratio framing for CFRD — formalizes age-at-onset Cox-style outcome as the appropriate analytic frame for an age-dependent CF complication.

Gap-map: leaves open

  • PheWAS scans of SLC26A9 across non-diabetes complications — the locus has been re-discovered three times for one outcome at a time (CFRD here, CFRD again in Aksit 2020, meconium ileus in Sun 2012). Nobody has done a clean apples-to-apples scan across the full CF phenome in one cohort.
  • Rare-variant burden in T2D-pathway genes — Blackman tested only common SNPs at known T2D loci. Rare coding burden in TCF7L2, KCNJ11, HNF1A, etc., in CF WGS data is unexplored territory.
  • Modulator-era effect modification — Blackman's cohort is fully pre-modulator; Aksit's is largely so. Whether SLC26A9 and T2D-overlap variants still predict CFRD onset in patients on ivacaftor/Trikafta from early disease is open and is exactly the question current CFF registry follow-up could answer.
  • Beta-cell vs. insulin-sensitivity decomposition — flagged here at the single-locus level, not formally resolved. Aksit 2020 closes this with PRS decomposition.
  • PTMA and other CF-population-specific loci — discovery sample was underpowered to find them; Aksit 2020 closes this gap.
  • Cross-ancestry portability — discovery and replication cohorts are predominantly European-ancestry CF populations.

For the concept note (prior modifier landscape paragraph)

Blackman 2013 established SLC26A9 and the type 2 diabetes common-variant overlap as the foundation of CFRD modifier genetics; the remaining white space is rare-variant burden in the same pathway, modulator-era effect modification, and pleiotropy of SLC26A9 across non-diabetes CF complications.
