Blurb

Ling, Raraigh, Pugh, Aksit, Zhang and CF Genome Project investigators (Am J Hum Genet, 2024) ran a 4,393-person CFGP WGS-based GWAS for AvgBMIz (average per-quarter BMI Z-scores) using 9.8M SNPs at MAF >0.005. Two genome-wide significant loci: rs28567725 in FTO (chromosome 16, MAF 0.41, β 0.106, p = 1.21e-08, n = 4,393) — the same locus driving general-population BMI; and rs162500 in ADAMTS5 (chromosome 21, MAF 0.005, β = −0.768, p = 2.11e-10, n = 4,085 PI-only) — a CF-specific signal sitting on a nine-SNP haplotype carried by ~18% of African-ancestry individuals but only ~0.6% of European-ancestry individuals. Inclusion of 90 self-reported Black CF participants made the ADAMTS5 locus detectable. A 924-SNP general-population BMI PRS predicted CF AvgBMIz (p = 2.2e-16, r² = 0.03). The two loci jointly explain 1.4–1.8% of AvgBMIz variance. The prior 1p36 / 5q14 linkage peaks from the same group's earlier family-based work did not replicate as common-variant signals. Proposed direction (conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage): ADAMTS5-haplotype phenome-wide association scan in CFGP-WGS with CFFPR phenotype linkage, restricted to PI individuals, testing whether the BMI-decreasing haplotype also modulates lung function trajectory, CFRD onset, CFLD onset, exacerbation frequency, or modulator response. Open question: whether the haplotype-carrier subset (~22 White + ~21 Black PI-CF subjects in this cohort) is powered for phenome-wide stratification beyond BMI.

ALEX Welcome back. Our CFTR-WGS pick today is "Genetic modifiers of body mass index in individuals with cystic fibrosis" by Hua Ling, Karen S. Raraigh, Elizabeth W. Pugh, Melis A. Aksit, Peng Zhang and the CF Genome Project investigators including the Cutting and Knowles groups, published in the American Journal of Human Genetics in 2024. The paper has only been cited once so far, but it sits squarely on top of the CFGP whole-genome sequencing substrate that Runyu's project would also draw on.

JORDAN Why does BMI matter in cystic fibrosis?

ALEX Because nutritional status is one of the strongest predictors of survival in cystic fibrosis. Pancreatic insufficiency — which we'll define in a moment — means most CF patients can't absorb fat and protein normally, and the result is chronic undernutrition. Higher BMI in CF correlates with better lung function trajectory and longer survival, and the CF clinical-care field has spent decades pushing BMI upward through enzyme replacement, high-calorie diets, and feeding tubes. So the question "what genetic variants modify BMI in CF" is not academic — it potentially points to interventional biology, and the CFFPR uses BMI percentile as one of the key registry-level quality benchmarks for CF care centers.

JORDAN Before we go into the paper, give me a vocabulary primer.

ALEX WGS is whole-genome sequencing — reading the entire 3-billion-base-pair genome. MAF is minor allele frequency — the frequency of the less-common version of a variant in the population; a SNP with MAF of 0.41 is common, MAF of 0.005 is rare. β, or beta, is the effect size — how much the trait changes per copy of the risk allele. The Z score is a standardized score: BMI Z-score means how many standard deviations a person's BMI is above or below the age- and sex-matched expected value, so you can compare a 6-year-old and a 26-year-old on the same scale. PI versus PS — pancreatic insufficiency versus pancreatic sufficiency — is a CF-specific phenotype split: PI patients have lost essentially all pancreatic exocrine function and depend on enzyme replacement; PS patients retain some residual function and have milder nutritional disease. F508del — phenylalanine-508 deletion — is the most common CF-causing CFTR variant, accounting for around 70 percent of CF chromosomes worldwide. A G-tube is a gastrostomy tube — a feeding tube placed surgically into the stomach to deliver high-calorie nutrition when oral intake is insufficient. A haplotype is a string of co-inherited variants traveling together as a block on the same chromosome.

JORDAN And GWAS, PRS, LD, PC?

ALEX GWAS — genome-wide association study — tests every common variant in the genome for association with a trait. PRS — polygenic risk score — sums the small effects of many trait-associated variants into a single composite predictor. The general-population BMI literature has a well-developed PRS built from massive GWAS in non-CF populations; Ling et al. test whether that PRS also predicts BMI variation within CF, which is a clever way to ask whether CF BMI shares architecture with general-population BMI or is genetically distinct. LD is linkage disequilibrium — variants nearby on the chromosome are correlated because they tend to be inherited together. PC is principal component — a numerical summary of ancestry derived from genome-wide variant data, used as a covariate to prevent ancestry differences from generating spurious genetic associations.

JORDAN What was the problem?

ALEX Two earlier CF BMI studies — including a family-based linkage scan by the same group — had pointed to two chromosomal regions, 1p36 and 5q14, as likely modifier loci. Those were linkage peaks, not specific genes, and they had not been independently validated. The field needed a properly powered, individual-variant, whole-genome-resolution GWAS in CF to either fine-map those peaks or replace them with new candidates. Beyond that, the CF community has long suspected that some BMI-modifying biology in CF is different from general-population biology — because CF patients face an unusual physiological challenge of chronic fat malabsorption on a high-fat dietary intake — but had no genome-scale evidence to confirm or refute that.

JORDAN What did they do?

ALEX They assembled 4,393 people with CF from five recruitment streams across three coordinating centers: the University of Washington Pseudomonas-early-life cohort, Johns Hopkins, and the University of North Carolina extremes-of-lung-function cohort. Phenotype was AvgBMIz — the average across quarterly BMI Z-score measurements after age 2, derived from the longitudinal weight-and-height data captured in the U.S. CF Foundation Patient Registry. Genotype was 9.8 million common SNPs at MAF greater than 0.005, extracted from whole-genome sequencing of each subject. They controlled for site of recruitment, birth cohort, PI versus PS status, and four principal components of ancestry. They then ran a linear mixed model GWAS — first on all 4,393 individuals, then restricted to the 4,085 pancreatic-insufficient subset to reduce phenotypic heterogeneity, then restricted further to the 2,625 F508del homozygotes as a CFTR-genotype-homogeneity check. They also tested the general-population BMI PRS in the CF cohort and ran population-specific principal-component analyses for two ancestry-defined subgroups identified by PCA.

JORDAN Push back on a design choice.

ALEX The G-tube decision is the most defensible-but-debatable choice. G-tube placement is itself a consequence of poor nutritional status — so if you include G-tube use as a covariate, you partially adjust away the very phenotype you're trying to find genetic determinants of. They handled this honestly: they ran the analysis both with and without G-tube as a covariate and showed only slight differences. But the choice of "without G-tube" as the primary analysis is a judgment call. The other pushback: their cohort is 96 percent self-reported White, and the inclusion of just 90 self-reported Black participants ended up being the linchpin for one of the headline findings — which is both a strength of the paper and an unsettling reminder of how thin the diversity is in the canonical CF research substrate.

JORDAN What did they find?

ALEX Two genome-wide significant loci. First, on chromosome 16 within the FTO gene, the lead SNP rs28567725 reached p value 1.21e-08 in the full 4,393-person analysis, with MAF of 0.41 and β of 0.106. This is the same FTO locus that drives BMI in the general population — the BMI signal there is so robust that 286 of 760 published FTO associations are for BMI-related traits as of late 2023. In CF, each copy of the minor allele increases AvgBMIz by 0.10, roughly 0.2 to 0.5 kg per meter squared, in the same range as the general-population effect. When they conditioned on the canonical general-population BMI lead at FTO, rs1558902, the CF signal collapsed, meaning they are seeing the same signal. Second, on chromosome 21, the lead SNP rs162500 within the ADAMTS5 gene reached p value 2.11e-10 in the 4,085 PI-only subset, with MAF of 0.005 in this cohort and β of minus 0.768. Per copy of the minor allele, AvgBMIz decreased by 0.77 — a large effect, much larger than FTO. ADAMTS5 is "a disintegrin and metalloproteinase with thrombospondin motifs 5" — a secreted enzyme that breaks down extracellular-matrix proteins. The associated rs162500 variant sits in a nine-SNP haplotype carried by about 18 percent of African-ancestry individuals but only 0.6 percent of European-ancestry individuals; inclusion of the 90 Black CF participants made the locus detectable. The CF-cohort haplotype frequencies match TOPMed/BRAVO general-population frequencies for those ancestries. A 924-SNP general-population BMI polygenic risk score also predicted AvgBMIz in CF at p value 2.2e-16, explaining 3 percent of variance, meaning some general-population BMI architecture transfers into CF beyond FTO. The two GWAS-significant loci jointly explain about 1.4 to 1.8 percent of AvgBMIz variance, and notably, no association reached genome-wide or even suggestive significance under the 1p36 and 5q14 linkage peaks reported in the prior family-based work — those linkage peaks are not replicated as common-variant signals here.

JORDAN One concrete research direction this episode suggests?

ALEX Here is one possible direction. The ADAMTS5 signal is striking because it is essentially CF-specific and PI-specific — it does not appear in any general-population BMI GWAS in the GWAS catalog, including the very large All of Us cohort. One interpretation Ling et al. offer is that ADAMTS5 only matters under the high-fat-diet, fat-malabsorption physiological condition that PI CF patients live in. If that is true, ADAMTS5 is the first cleanly conditional-on-environment CF BMI modifier — a kind of pharmacogenomic logic, except the "drug" is the prescribed CF high-fat diet plus enzyme replacement therapy. One possible direction: a CFTR-PheWAS extension that uses ADAMTS5 rs162500 carrier status as the exposure and asks, across the full CFFPR phenotype catalog, what other CF complications track with this haplotype. Formally: an ADAMTS5-haplotype phenome-wide association scan in the CFGP-WGS sample with CFFPR phenotype linkage, restricted to PI individuals, testing whether the haplotype that decreases BMI also modulates lung function trajectory, CFRD onset, CFLD onset, exacerbation frequency, or modulator response, with population-specific principal-component adjustment to handle the ancestry-correlated frequency of the haplotype.

JORDAN What does the paper close and what is left open under that direction?

ALEX What it closes: there is now a defensible primary CF BMI GWAS in the post-WGS era. FTO is confirmed as a transferred general-population locus. ADAMTS5 is a new CF-specific signal at genome-wide significance, with a clear ancestry-driven discovery story and a plausible pancreatic-insufficient physiological interaction hypothesis. The previously claimed 1p36 and 5q14 linkage peaks did not replicate as common-variant signals in this WGS dataset, which is a useful negative result. What is left open: ADAMTS5 mechanism — the paper does not show that ADAMTS5 expression, protein level, or any specific functional SNP within the haplotype is the causal driver; they document a haplotype that includes nine LD-correlated SNPs and reference published functional SNPs altering plasma ADAMTS5 levels. Whether ADAMTS5 carrier status predicts any non-BMI CF complication is untested. Replication in a population-genetics framework with larger African-ancestry CF representation has not been done — they had 90 Black participants total and the haplotype-carrier subset is small. Things we would need to check before committing to the ADAMTS5-PheWAS direction: whether the CFGP variant calls include rs162500 with reliable genotype quality at MAF 0.005; whether the CFFPR phenotype catalog has the longitudinal lung-function, CFRD, and CFLD measurements at adequate density to power haplotype-stratified outcome regressions; whether the ancestry-correlated allele frequency would require population-specific PC adjustment and what software supports that without dropping the related individuals; whether the haplotype is well-tagged in non-WGS imputed datasets in case external replication needs to happen on imputed-array data; whether the prior CFRD-modifier work on the same CFGP substrate already captured rs162500 as a candidate that simply did not reach significance for CFRD-specific endpoints; and whether the modulator era has shifted CF BMI distributions enough that pre-modulator and post-modulator subjects need to be stratified separately.

One-line takeaway: the CF BMI architecture is partly shared with general-population BMI through FTO and the broader PRS, and partly CF-specific through ADAMTS5 — and the CF-specific piece was only detectable because 90 Black CF participants were included, which is the clearest argument for ancestry-diverse CF cohort building that any single CFTR-modifier paper has yet produced.
