One-paragraph blurb

This Nature Genetics paper, anchored to the Million Veteran Program with UK Biobank, ICBP, and BioVU replication, is the canonical demonstration of the full modern multi-ancestry GWAS + TWAS + PheWAS + drug-gene pipeline applied to a single trait family — blood pressure. With 459,777 discovery participants and over 750,000 total, the authors found 201 novel common BP loci, 10 rare missense variants in 7 genes (PDE3A, SLC9A3R2, RRAS, PHC3, DBH, COL21A1, NOX4) at genome-wide significance, 4,043 gene-tissue TWAS associations across 45 GTEx tissues with aorta as the top tissue, and a PheWAS of BP genetic risk scores that surfaced 88 phenotypes ranging from circulatory disease to diabetes sequelae. One possible direction the episode suggests for the CFTR PheWAS work: build a CF-lung-modifier weighted genetic risk score from published CF GWAS hits and run a PheWAS of that score against the CFFPR phenome, ancestry-stratified. Open: whether sample size in a roughly 5,000-WGS CF cohort can support the down-stream components of the pipeline.

Plain-English glossary

  • DEPICT: gene-set, tissue, and pathway enrichment tool built on top of GWAS summary statistics.
  • GRS / w-GRS (genetic risk score / weighted genetic risk score): single per-person number summarizing disease-allele burden across many SNPs, optionally weighted by per-SNP effect sizes.
  • GTEx (Genotype-Tissue Expression project): reference dataset of paired genotype and gene-expression panels across human tissues; the substrate for building predicted-expression models used in TWAS.
  • GWAS (genome-wide association study): systematic test of millions of common SNPs against a phenotype, with a stringent significance threshold (typically P < 5×10⁻⁸).
  • IPA (Ingenuity Pathway Analysis): commercial pathway-enrichment software.
  • LD (linkage disequilibrium): non-random co-inheritance of nearby SNPs; lets one SNP tag many neighbors but obscures which SNP is causal.
  • MAF (minor allele frequency): frequency of the less common allele at a SNP; conventionally common ≥1%, rare <1%.
  • MVP (Million Veteran Program): US Department of Veterans Affairs biobank, EHR-linked, started 2011.
  • PheWAS (phenome-wide association study): systematic test of one genetic predictor against many phenotypes.
  • Pulse pressure: systolic minus diastolic blood pressure; reflects large-artery stiffness.
  • S-PrediXcan: TWAS software that uses GWAS summary statistics plus GTEx-derived expression-prediction models to test genetically-predicted gene expression against a trait.
  • SBP / DBP: systolic and diastolic blood pressure.
  • SNP (single-nucleotide polymorphism): a single-letter DNA position where the population carries multiple common variants.
  • TWAS (transcriptome-wide association study): test of genetically predicted gene expression — not raw SNPs — against a phenotype; gives gene-level rather than SNP-level signals.

Methods to lift

  • Multi-cohort discovery-plus-replication design anchored to MVP + UKB discovery, ICBP + BioVU + BP-ICE replication — a template for any biobank-scale trait analysis.
  • Joint scan + conditional analysis for surfacing additional independent signals at known loci.
  • Rare-variant analysis in two parallel substrates — exome-chip and genome-wide — with separate replication paths.
  • S-PrediXcan TWAS across all GTEx v6 tissues plus a kidney-specific reference panel (Ko et al.) — example of layering a custom expression panel onto the standard GTEx setup.
  • DEPICT for tissue and gene-set enrichment from GWAS summary statistics.
  • w-GRS PheWAS in EHR-derived phenotype catalog, run separately by ancestry stratum, regressed onto each w-GRS adjusted for top 10 PCs.
  • Drug-gene cross-reference against known antihypertensive drug targets and against non-antihypertensive drugs with hypotension/hypertension on their adverse-event labels.
  • Murine kidney single-cell RNA-seq as a tissue-deconvolution step layered on top of human TWAS — relevant template for any cell-type localization of a CF lung-modifier signal.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage: build a CF-lung-modifier weighted genetic risk score from previously-published genome-wide-significant CF modifier SNPs (chr11p13 EHF/APIP, chr20q13.2, SLC6A14 X-linked, SLC9A3 chr5p13, and others), then run a PheWAS of that score across the CFFPR phenome, ancestry-stratified, asking whether the modifier-score signal extends to non-pulmonary CF endpoints and whether pleiotropy patterns differ between European-ancestry and African-ancestry CF subgroups.

What to verify first

  • Whether the published CF-modifier sentinel SNPs are reliably genotyped at adequate quality in the CFF whole-genome sequencing variant call set.
  • Whether the CFFPR phenotype catalog has been or can be mapped to a phecode-style coding scheme suitable for PheWAS.
  • Whether ancestry-stratum sample sizes in the CFF cohort are large enough to detect anything beyond the dominant pulmonary signal — power on the African-ancestry stratum is likely the binding constraint.
  • Whether TWAS via S-PrediXcan is feasible given that GTEx and the Ko kidney panels are general-population substrates, not CF airway substrates; airway-tissue prediction models built on healthy donors may not represent CF airway expression patterns.
  • Whether sex-stratified analysis is even meaningful given that the CFF cohort is roughly 50/50 male/female — a far better demographic substrate than MVP's 91.5% male — and whether the published CF modifier hits replicate sex-pooled in our cohort.

ALEX Episode two of today. Today's paper is Giri and colleagues, 2019, Nature Genetics, "Trans-ethnic association study of blood pressure determinants in over 750,000 individuals." First authors out of the Million Veteran Program team at Vanderbilt with major contributions from UK Biobank, the International Consortium for Blood Pressure, and the BioVU biobank. The reason we're reading a blood-pressure paper on a CF podcast: this is one of the canonical demonstrations of the full modern toolkit — multi-ancestry genome-wide association, rare-variant analysis, transcriptome-wide association, phenome-wide association, and drug-gene mapping — all glued together in one pipeline. If we're going to think about doing any of this for CFTR, we should know what the gold-standard recipe looks like.

JORDAN Stop right there. I need a primer on most of those terms.

ALEX Fair. Let me set the table. GWAS is genome-wide association study — you test millions of common variants across the genome, one at a time, against a trait, and look for the ones that exceed a stringent significance threshold, conventionally five times ten to the minus eight. SNP is single-nucleotide polymorphism, just a one-letter DNA change at a position where the population carries more than one common variant. MAF is minor allele frequency — the frequency of the less common allele at a SNP; a MAF above one percent is conventionally called "common," below one percent is "rare." LD is linkage disequilibrium — when two SNPs sit close together on the chromosome, they tend to be inherited together; LD lets one SNP serve as a tag for many neighbors but also makes it hard to pin down which SNP is doing the work.

JORDAN And the rest of the alphabet?

ALEX PheWAS is phenome-wide association study — instead of one trait against many variants, you test one genetic predictor against many traits, looking for unexpected cross-trait associations. TWAS is transcriptome-wide association study — instead of testing variants against a trait, you test genetically predicted gene expression against a trait, which gives you a gene-level rather than SNP-level answer. S-PrediXcan is the specific software tool that does this from summary statistics rather than individual genotype data. GTEx is the Genotype-Tissue Expression project, the reference dataset of gene-expression-versus-genotype panels across human tissues that lets you build those predicted-expression models in the first place. GRS is genetic risk score, a single number per person summarizing their disease-allele burden across many SNPs. DEPICT is a gene-set enrichment tool. IPA is Ingenuity Pathway Analysis, a commercial pathway-enrichment tool. MVP, ICBP, UKB, BioVU are all big biobanks — Million Veteran Program, International Consortium for Blood Pressure, UK Biobank, and Vanderbilt's BioVU.

JORDAN What's the substantive question here? Blood pressure has been GWAS'd to death.

ALEX Blood pressure has been GWAS'd extensively — the field had over 250 known loci going into this study — but four things were still unsatisfying. First, most prior work was European-ancestry-heavy, so trans-ancestry generalizability was an open question. Second, most BP SNPs sit in noncoding regions and just say "something around here matters" without telling you which gene. Third, the field didn't have a clean view of which tissues the BP genes act in. Fourth, drug discovery would benefit from being able to triangulate genetic hits against known drug targets. The MVP, which started recruiting in 2011, is the largest US veteran-affiliated biobank and is unusually well-positioned to address all of these: it has roughly equal representation of African American participants compared to the country average, real-world clinical phenotypes from VA electronic health records, and a substantial sample size — eventually north of 800,000 enrolled.

ALEX So the study sets out to do five things on one substrate: a common-variant GWAS, a rare-variant analysis, a transcriptome-wide analysis across forty-five tissues, a PheWAS using BP genetic risk scores, and a drug-gene relationship analysis. All anchored to the Million Veteran Program and UK Biobank as the discovery substrate.

ALEX Methods. Discovery: 318,891 MVP participants — 91.5 percent male, 69 percent non-Hispanic white, 18.8 percent non-Hispanic black, 6.7 percent Hispanic, smaller Native American and Asian groups — plus 140,886 from UK Biobank, for a discovery sample of 459,777. Replication: 316,301 from the International Consortium for Blood Pressure for common variants; 445,360 from UK Biobank for genome-wide rare variants; 420,704 from the BP-ICE exome-chip consortium for exonic rare variants. They tested three blood pressure traits separately: systolic, diastolic, and pulse pressure. Pulse pressure, by the way, is just systolic minus diastolic, and it captures large-artery stiffness in a way the other two don't.

ALEX Then S-PrediXcan across forty-four GTEx tissues plus a kidney reference panel built by Ko and colleagues. PheWAS using nine flavors of weighted genetic risk score — three traits times three ancestry strata — regressed against 1,813 phenotypes derived from VA EHR data in MVP. Pathway analysis via DEPICT for tissue and gene-set enrichment, and Ingenuity Pathway Analysis for biology. Drug-gene mapping cross-references the significant TWAS genes against known antihypertensive drug targets and against non-antihypertensive drugs whose adverse event labels mention hypertension or hypotension.

JORDAN ALEX, push back on a design choice for me.

ALEX The big one is the demographic make-up of MVP. Ninety-one and a half percent male. That's not the US population. For BP traits where sex-specific physiology, sex-by-medication interactions, and sex-specific outcomes all matter, building the largest BP GWAS to date on a 90-percent-male substrate is a real limitation that the paper acknowledges but does not solve.

ALEX Now to results. Common variants: 505 independent loci associated with one or more BP traits, of which 201 were novel — meaning they replicated, exceeded genome-wide significance in the joint discovery-plus-replication meta-analysis, and were more than 500 kilobases from any known sentinel SNP and not in LD with one. Effect sizes for the novel loci were systematically smaller than for the known loci, which is expected as you push deeper into the polygenic tail. All sentinel SNPs together explained roughly 3.6 percent of systolic, 1.1 percent of diastolic, and 3.7 percent of pulse pressure variance.

ALEX Rare variants. Eighteen exome-chip variants made the final meta-analysis cut, of which ten missense variants across seven genes were genome-wide significant: PDE3A, SLC9A3R2, RRAS, PHC3, DBH, three different variants in COL21A1, and one in NOX4. Effect sizes for rare missense were larger than common — 1.5 millimeters of mercury per allele for systolic on average — and the COL21A1 and NOX4 hits were pulse-pressure-specific with opposite-direction effects on systolic versus diastolic, consistent with a vascular-stiffness mechanism rather than a volume-handling mechanism. A separate genome-wide rare-variant scan in MVP plus UKB added 48 more significant rare variants, most of them pulse-pressure-specific, with much larger per-allele effects — nearly 14 millimeters per allele for pulse pressure on average.

ALEX Transcriptome-wide. S-PrediXcan turned up 4,043 significant gene-tissue pairs covering 840 genes across the 45 tissues. Aorta was the most enriched tissue across all three traits — strongest for pulse pressure at P equals 1.2 times ten to the minus nine — followed by other arterial tissues. DEPICT independently agreed: 36 tissues across seven systems enriched for pulse pressure, with eleven of the 36 cardiovascular. Crucially, 409 of the TWAS genes were genes the SNPs would not have been assigned to under a nearest-gene heuristic — that's the methodological argument for doing TWAS in the first place. Murine single-cell RNA-seq on kidney mapped the implicated kidney-side TWAS genes to specific tubule cell types — proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal convoluted tubule, collecting duct principal and intercalated cells.

ALEX PheWAS. The blood pressure weighted genetic risk scores were tested against 1,813 phenotypes in MVP whites, blacks, and Hispanics. Eighty-eight phenotypes survived Bonferroni correction at 2.76 times ten to the minus five. Hypertension and essential hypertension were the top hits, predictably, with P-values smaller than ten to the minus three hundred and five. Beyond hypertension, the cross-trait spillover concentrated in circulatory phenotypes — 52 of the 88 hits — followed by endocrine and metabolic, 28 hits. Notable specificity: pulse-pressure GRS hit five diabetes sequelae — diabetic retinopathy, neurological manifestations, ophthalmic manifestations, polyneuropathy, and abnormal glucose — that the systolic and diastolic GRS did not capture as strongly. Aortic and other aneurysms uniquely associated with diastolic GRS.

ALEX Drug-gene. Cross-referencing significant TWAS genes against drug-target databases turned up 617 unique drug-gene interactions, 175 of them with antagonistic directionality. The interesting cases are non-antihypertensive drugs whose targets came up — theophylline targeting PDE3A, carfilzomib targeting PSMB9, pazopanib targeting SH2B3 — all of which have hypo- or hypertension listed as adverse events on their labels and which show the predicted direction of effect from the TWAS.

ALEX So did they answer their questions? Yes for "how do we run the full pipeline" — completely. Yes for "are there novel BP signals to find" — 201 novel loci. Partially for "is the genetic architecture trans-ancestry-portable" — common-variant effects were directionally consistent but quantitatively weaker correlations than prior work suggested, and the PheWAS GRS effects were consistent across the three ancestries in MVP. No for "did they identify causal genes at any locus" — TWAS narrows the candidate-gene list dramatically but doesn't formally establish causality, which still needs experimental work.

One possible direction the paper suggests, not the direction we'll take.

JORDAN And the gap, given that direction?

ALEX This paper closes the methodological question — the recipe for combining multi-ancestry GWAS plus TWAS plus PheWAS plus drug-gene cross-reference is now thoroughly demonstrated. It also closes a specific concern about whether GRS effects transfer across ancestries for common-variant traits: in this paper they did, at least directionally. What it leaves open for our purposes is whether any of this transfers down to a CF cohort of around five thousand whole-genome-sequenced individuals. MVP and UK Biobank together gave them 459,777 discovery participants. We have at most around 5,000. Power for any of these analyses at that sample size is the central question.
