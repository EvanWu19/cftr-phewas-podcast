Aksit et al. 2020 — CFRD genetic modifiers and overlap with T2D

Blurb

Aksit and colleagues conducted a genome-wide association study (GWAS) for cystic fibrosis-related diabetes (CFRD) onset in 5,740 individuals with CF, drawn from the North American CF Modifier Consortium (Cutting / Knowles / Drumm) and the French CF Modifier Study (Corvol), with statistical leadership from Strug at Toronto. They modeled age at CFRD diagnosis as a survival-style outcome and identified three genome-wide significant loci — PTMA (novel), TCF7L2 (a canonical type 2 diabetes locus), and SLC26A9 (a known CF complication modifier). Polygenic risk scores (PRS) imported from general-population GWAS showed CFRD overlaps T2D strongly along the beta-cell function axis (insulin secretion, postchallenge glucose, fasting plasma glucose), weakly with T1D, inconsistently with insulin sensitivity, and not at all with islet autoimmunity. An 18-variant CFRD PRS, built by combining the GWAS hits with FDR<0.1 variants from the cross-trait scans, replicated in an independent CF population. The discovery cohort is the same CFF-anchored modifier-consortium resource a CFTR PheWAS would draw on, which makes this paper a load-bearing reference for both the saturation analysis and the gap-map of any concept note.

Plain-English glossary

  • Beta-cell dysfunction — Reduced insulin secretion from the pancreatic islet beta cells; the dominant mechanism in CFRD and a contributing one in T2D.
  • Case-control study — Design where disease status (yes/no) is the outcome, snapshot in time. Less powerful than survival analysis when the disease is age-dependent.
  • CFRD — Cystic fibrosis-related diabetes. A distinct form of diabetes that develops in CF patients, characterized by beta-cell dysfunction with relatively preserved insulin sensitivity. Not T1D and not T2D.
  • Cox regression — Statistical model for time-to-event data; outputs hazard ratios.
  • Endophenotype — A measurable intermediate trait (e.g., fasting glucose, insulin secretion) that lies between genotype and disease.
  • FDR (false discovery rate) — Looser significance threshold than genome-wide significance. FDR<0.1 means at most 10% of called hits are expected to be false positives.
  • Genome-wide significance threshold — Conventionally p < 5x10^-8, accounting for the millions of independent tests in a GWAS.
  • GWAS — Genome-wide association study. Tests millions of common SNPs across the genome for association with a trait.
  • Locus — A region of the genome, typically a SNP and the neighboring DNA that travels with it.
  • Modifier gene — A gene outside the disease-causing gene (here, outside CFTR) that influences how severe a complication becomes.
  • PRS (polygenic risk score) — A weighted sum across many genetic variants, summarizing aggregate genetic risk for a trait.
  • Replication cohort — An independent second sample used to confirm a finding from a discovery cohort.
  • SNP — Single nucleotide polymorphism, a single-base genetic variant.
  • Survival / age-at-onset analysis — Models when an event occurs, not just whether it has occurred at a snapshot. Preserves signal for age-dependent conditions like CFRD.
  • T1D — Type 1 diabetes. Autoimmune destruction of beta cells.
  • T2D — Type 2 diabetes. Insulin resistance plus gradual beta-cell decline.
  • Trikafta — The elexacaftor-tezacaftor-ivacaftor combination CFTR modulator approved October 2019; rescues CFTR function in most CF patients and is changing the natural history of CF complications.

  • Adjustment for CFTR genotype, sex, and population structure as standard covariates in the modifier-locus regression.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on data access and locus-level WGS quality, the natural extension of Aksit is an inverted-direction phenome-wide association scan: take the established CFRD-modifier loci — particularly the CF-specific PTMA and SLC26A9 — and scan them across the full set of registry-linked CF phenotypes (exocrine pancreatic disease severity, lung function trajectory, BMI trajectory, infection burden, liver disease, sinus disease, exacerbation rate) with appropriate multiple-testing correction. The hypothesis is that loci modifying CFRD risk also modify other CF complications via shared epithelial or developmental biology, and the registry's longitudinal depth provides power that a CFRD-onset-only design cannot access. This is conditional, not committed; it presumes verification of the items below.

Gap-map: closes

  • CFRD GWAS in the CFF-anchored modifier consortium at n~5,740 — definitively done.
  • Whether CFRD shares genetic architecture with T2D — yes, along the beta-cell function axis.
  • Whether CFRD is autoimmune-like (T1D-like) — essentially no; islet autoimmunity PRS unassociated.
  • Whether CFRD is insulin-sensitivity-driven — no; insulin sensitivity PRS inconsistent.
  • Existence of a validated, replicated CFRD polygenic risk score — done (18-variant score, replicated independently).
  • PTMA as a novel CF modifier locus — established.

Gap-map: leaves open

  • Phenome-wide scans of the established CF-modifier loci (PTMA, SLC26A9, TCF7L2) across non-diabetes CF complications in the same cohort — not done; this is the inverted direction Aksit did not take.
  • Modulator-era effects: Aksit's CFRD events accrued largely before Trikafta (approved October 2019); the genetic architecture of CFRD and other complications under widespread elexacaftor-tezacaftor-ivacaftor exposure is unaddressed.
  • Longitudinal registry phenotypes (repeated lung function, weight, infection, exacerbation measurements) — Aksit used a single age-at-onset endpoint and did not exploit the trajectory data the CFFPR contains.
  • Rare-variant burden tests on the established loci using whole-genome sequencing rather than imputed common variants — out of scope for Aksit's design.
  • Modern PRS construction (LDpred2, PRS-CS) — Aksit used FDR<0.1 variant selection, which is permissive and a candidate for methodological refinement.

  • SLC26A9 and PTMA genotype quality in the CFF WGS variant call file — coverage, call rate, and concordance with prior genotyping at these specific loci.
  • Replication cohort size and composition in Aksit's PRS validation step (the abstract says "an independent population"; the exact n should be pulled from the full text before citation in the concept note).
  • Overlap between Aksit's discovery cohort and the CFF WGS sub-cohort — to know whether any planned CFTR PheWAS work is statistically independent of Aksit or shares samples.

That makes CFRD different from T1D, which is type 1 diabetes, an autoimmune destruction of beta cells, and different from T2D, type 2 diabetes, where insulin resistance is the dominant defect alongside a slower beta-cell decline.

JORDAN Okay. And GWAS?

ALEX Genome-wide association study. You take a few million common genetic variants, called single nucleotide polymorphisms or SNPs, and you test each one for association with a trait. Because you are running millions of tests, the field uses a stringent significance threshold of about 5 times 10 to the negative 8 — that is the genome-wide significance threshold. Anything below that p-value is considered a real hit. A locus is just a region of the genome — usually a SNP and the neighboring DNA that travels with it.

JORDAN PRS?

ALEX Polygenic risk score. The idea is that a trait like T2D is influenced by thousands of variants, each with a tiny effect. A PRS sums those up — for each variant you carry, you add its effect size — and the total is a single number summarizing your genetic risk for that trait. You can build a PRS for T2D and apply it to a person who has never had a glucose test, and it will still give you a meaningful risk gradient.

JORDAN FDR?

ALEX False discovery rate. Looser cousin of the genome-wide significance threshold. Instead of "this hit has less than a 5 in 100 million chance of being noise," FDR less than 0.1 means "of the hits I am calling real, I expect at most 10% of them to be false." You use FDR when you want to scoop up suggestive variants that are not individually genome-wide significant.

JORDAN And modifier gene, survival analysis, replication cohort?

ALEX A modifier gene is a gene outside CFTR itself that influences how severe a CF complication becomes. CFTR causes CF; modifiers determine whether your CF lungs decline at age 25 or age 55. Survival or age-at-onset analysis means you model when an event happens, not just whether it happens — so for CFRD you ask "by what age did this person develop diabetes," rather than "do they have it, yes or no" at a snapshot. A replication cohort is a second, independent group of patients you re-test your finding in, to make sure it was not a fluke of the first dataset. And a case-control study is the simpler alternative — yes-or-no disease status compared between groups.

JORDAN Good. Now, what was the question?

ALEX The setup is this. CFRD is the most common non-pulmonary complication in adults with CF. By the time someone with CF reaches their 30s or 40s, the cumulative incidence is north of 50%. Clinically it has been a puzzle, because it looks like T2D in some respects — beta-cell amyloid deposits, gradual onset, no autoantibodies — but it occurs in young, often lean patients who have normal insulin sensitivity, which is not T2D-like. So the field has argued for years over whether CFRD's underlying genetics overlap with T2D, with T1D, with neither, or with some private CF-specific architecture.

JORDAN And why does that matter clinically?

ALEX Two reasons. First, mechanism informs therapy. If CFRD turns out to share machinery with T2D along the insulin-secretion axis, drugs that improve beta-cell function or incretin biology become more attractive than insulin sensitizers. Second, risk stratification. CFRD onset is variable. If you could tell at age 10 which CF patients are headed for early CFRD, you could intensify glucose monitoring and perhaps intervene earlier.

ALEX Discovery GWAS first.
