Blurb

Backman and colleagues at the Regeneron Genetics Center released the exome catalogue for 454,787 UK Biobank participants and ran a 2.3-billion-test rare-variant association sweep across 3,994 health traits using REGENIE, identifying 564 genes with associations at P less than or equal to 2.18 x 10^-11. Ninety-one percent of the rare-variant signals were independent of nearby GWAS common-variant signals, and 81 percent of the well-powered associations replicated in the 133,370-person DiscovEHR cohort. For the CFTR-PheWAS proposal, this is the methods paper: REGENIE step-1 plus step-2 with leave-one-chromosome-out is the rare-variant gene-burden engine we will adopt, but Backman's protective-disease yield even at n equals 430K is the proof that at our n equals 5K we must restrict scope to high-effect rare-variant gene-burden tests on biologically prioritized CFTR-modifier candidates rather than running a full population-scale scan.

Citation

Backman JD, Li AH, Marcketta A, et al. Exome sequencing and analysis of 454,787 UK Biobank participants. Nature. 2021;599(7886):628-634. PMID: 34662886 — PMC: PMC8596853 — DOI: 10.1038/s41586-021-04103-z

Methods to lift

  • REGENIE two-step pipeline: step-1 stacked ridge regression on a sparse genome-wide variant set to estimate polygenic background, relatedness, and population structure; step-2 per-variant and per-gene burden tests conditioning on the step-1 predictor.
  • Leave-one-chromosome-out (LOCO) construction of the step-1 predictor to prevent proximal contamination of test statistics.
  • Approximate Firth logistic regression for binary outcomes to keep test statistics calibrated for low case counts and ultra-rare carriers.
  • Two burden masks per gene: pLOF only, and pLOF plus deleterious missense (5-algorithm consensus); evaluated at multiple MAF cutoffs (1 percent, 0.1 percent, singletons).
  • Bonferroni significance threshold of P less than or equal to 0.05 / total tests, which yielded 2.18 x 10^-11 for their 2.3 billion tests; recompute for our test grid.
  • Conditional analysis: re-fit each rare-variant burden conditioning on the nearby GWAS sentinel common variant to demonstrate independence (their 91 percent figure).
  • Replication in an independent cohort restricted to gene-trait associations with at least 80 percent power for replication.
  • Ancestry-stratified follow-up: discover in the largest ancestry, then test directional concordance in smaller ancestry strata as a sensitivity check rather than a discovery mode.
  • Carrier-count floor for reported burden hits; relax the Bonferroni threshold to 10^-7 only for hypothesis-prioritized exploratory tables, never for primary discovery.

Power note for n=5K CFF

Backman explicitly notes that even at n equals 430,998 Europeans, power to detect protective rare-variant disease associations at exome-wide P less than or equal to 2.18 x 10^-11 was low — only 5 such genes were identified at the strict threshold. At our discovery scale of approximately 5,000 CFF WGS samples, we are roughly 90-fold underpowered relative to Backman for population-scale common-effect rare-variant scans, so we should pre-specify a hypothesis-restricted gene-burden PheWAS over CFTR-modifier candidates against a curated CF-phenotype panel — the regime where high-effect rare-variant gene-burden signals (analogous to Backman's TTN-cardiomyopathy and BRCA1/2-cancer positive controls) remain detectable.
