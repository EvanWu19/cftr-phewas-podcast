Blurb

The CF Gene Modifier Consortium's flagship WGS modifier paper. Zhou and colleagues ran whole-genome sequencing on 4,248 pancreatic-insufficient pwCF, combined it with imputed genotypes from another 3,592 patients (n=7,840 total), and threw the modern toolkit — common single-variant, rare single-variant, TWAS, gene-burden, and pathway analysis — at the KNoRMA premodulator lung-function severity phenotype. The headline finding is a credibly powered null on novel single-variant discovery, with the two known modifier loci (SLC9A3/CEP72 at chr5p13 and EHF/APIP at chr11p13) confirmed and fine-mapped, and pathway-level signal pointing at organ-development and lung-repair biology. For anyone proposing a CFTR PheWAS on overlapping samples, this is the paper your reviewers will cite first.

Gap-map: what this paper closes

  • Common single-variant modifier discovery for premodulator KNoRMA lung function on the CF GMC WGS cohort (~15.9M SNPs tested, n=7,840) — credibly powered null beyond the two confirmed loci.
  • Rare single-variant single-marker discovery for the same phenotype on the same cohort — also a credibly powered null.
  • Gene-burden testing on lung-function severity for this cohort, with pathway-level extension already published (organ development / dysanapsis / lung-repair hypothesis).
  • Confirms and fine-maps SLC9A3/CEP72 (chr5p13) and EHF/APIP (chr11p13) as multilocus modifier signals — these are now consensus loci.
  • TWAS on predicted expression for premodulator FEV1 severity on this cohort.

Gap-map: what this paper leaves open

  • Phenome-wide scans across non-pulmonary CF outcomes on these same WGS samples — CFRD, CF liver disease, nutritional status, infection age-of-acquisition, bone, GI phenotypes. Wide-open white space and the natural home of Runyu's PheWAS.
  • Postmodulator-era phenotypes — explicitly excluded by design (premodulator FEV1 only).
  • Modulator-stratified or modulator-response phenotypes.
  • Longitudinal trajectories — KNoRMA collapses time. Rate-of-decline, age-at-event survival, exacerbation frequency as quantitative traits all untouched.
  • Structural variants, copy-number variants, repeat expansions on the WGS substrate — testing was SNP-focused.
  • Non-European-ancestry analyses — GMC cohort is overwhelmingly European; the registry now has more diverse representation.
  • Formal gene-by-gene epistasis and gene-by-environment interactions (infection, modulator era, birth cohort).
  • Targeted interrogation of the established modifier loci (SLC9A3/CEP72, EHF/APIP) against non-pulmonary phenotypes — a clean low-risk preregisterable hypothesis that builds on, rather than competes with, this paper.

One sentence for the concept note

Zhou and colleagues (2023) established that no novel common or rare single-variant modifier of premodulator KNoRMA lung function remains discoverable in the CF GMC WGS cohort at n=7,840; our PheWAS extends the same WGS substrate to the registry-wide phenome, including non-pulmonary, postmodulator-era, and longitudinal outcomes that were explicitly outside the scope of that work.

JORDAN Let's do that. Set the scene. Who, what, how big.

ALEX The CF GMC — the Gene Modifier Consortium — pulls from three legacy collections. Knowles at UNC, Cutting at Hopkins, and the Canadian arm through Strug and Sick Kids, with the French CF cohort via Corvol layered in. The trial anchors people usually cite are NCT00037778 and NCT00676169 — the GMS and TSS recruitments — plus the Canadian and French registry-linked samples. For this paper, the headline n is seven thousand eight hundred and forty pancreatic-insufficient pwCF. Of those, four thousand two hundred forty-eight have actual whole-genome sequencing. The remaining three thousand five hundred ninety-two are imputed off prior genotype arrays. Premodulator lung function only.

JORDAN That is a critical detail. Premodulator. So no Trikafta-era physiology contaminating the phenotype.

ALEX Right. Which is both a strength — clean disease-severity signal not confounded by modulator response — and a limitation we will come back to, because it means the postmodulator question is explicitly left open. They flag that themselves in the conclusion.

JORDAN Phenotype. They use KNoRMA?

ALEX KNoRMA. Kulich Normal Residual Mortality-Adjusted FEV1. It is the consortium's standard quantitative lung-disease severity metric — adjusts FEV1 percent predicted for age, sex, height, and the survivor bias from CF mortality, then ranks within the cohort. It's been the workhorse since the 2011 Wright paper and the 2015 Corvol GWAS. So this is not a new phenotype, it is the field-standard one applied at WGS scale.

JORDAN How many variants are they testing?

ALEX Approximately fifteen point nine million SNPs after QC. That is the WGS payoff — you get into the rare and low-frequency space the arrays could not see. And then methodologically they did not just run single-variant. They ran four families of tests: common single-variant, rare single-variant, transcriptome-wide association — so TWAS via predicted expression — gene-level burden, and pathway analysis on top of all of it.

JORDAN A full kitchen-sink.

ALEX Full kitchen-sink, and that matters for our gap-map because they are essentially saying: we threw the standard modern toolkit at this phenotype on this cohort. If a common-variant signal existed at this sample size and it was not found, it probably is not there.

JORDAN Okay. The hits. What survived?

ALEX Two multilocus regions. Chromosome 5p13, the SLC9A3 / CEP72 locus, which has been a known modifier signal since the 2015 Corvol GWAS and the Li-Sun follow-ups. And chromosome 11p13, the EHF / APIP region, also previously reported. Those are confirmed and extended — better fine-mapping, refined credible sets, multilocus structure made explicit. Crucially, the abstract states it directly: "No novel primary associations with common single variants or rare variants were found."

JORDAN That is the sentence.

ALEX That is the sentence. For a peer reviewing your concept note, that is the sentence they will quote at you. Seven thousand eight hundred patients, fifteen million variants, four analytic families, and the primary single-variant and rare-variant common-modifier search returned no new hits. The novelty in this paper is in the pathway layer — they pull out organ-development pathways and propose dysanapsis and lung-repair biology as the mechanistic frame.

JORDAN Dysanapsis meaning the airway-to-lung-size mismatch idea.

ALEX Right, the developmental mismatch between airway caliber and parenchymal volume. It is a hypothesis they are seeding for follow-up mechanistic work, not a closed finding. They are honest about that.

JORDAN They also say something about consistent effect-size ordering across cohorts. What do you make of that?

ALEX That is one of the more interesting subtle points. Variant effect sizes at the associated loci are ordered the same way across the three contributing cohorts — UNC, Hopkins, Canada-France — but the magnitudes differ. They float that this could be age cohort or birth cohort effects. Translation: even a "fixed" genetic effect on FEV1 may look different depending on when the patient was born and what the standard of care was during their developmental window. For Runyu's purposes that is a flashing yellow light about how you handle birth year and treatment era as covariates in any registry-linked analysis.

ALEX Bigger than the closed side, which is the good news for us. Open. First, this is one phenotype. KNoRMA is lung-function severity in pancreatic-insufficient patients. Everything non-pulmonary is untouched in this paper — CF-related diabetes, liver disease, nutritional status, infection age-of-acquisition, pancreatic sufficiency itself, the bone phenotypes, the GI phenotypes. PheWAS-style breadth across the registry phenome on these same WGS samples is wide-open white space.

JORDAN That is exactly Runyu's framing.

ALEX It maps directly onto the concept note. Second, postmodulator phenotypes are explicitly excluded by design. They use premodulator FEV1 on purpose. So modulator-response phenotypes, modulator-stratified severity, and on-treatment trajectories are completely outside the scope. Third, structural variation, copy-number, repeat expansions — they had WGS data but the abstract describes SNP-based testing. There is room for a structural-variant pass on the same cohort if anyone has the appetite. Fourth, ancestry. The GMC cohort is overwhelmingly European-ancestry, which is acknowledged across the modifier literature, and the registry has more diverse representation now than it did when GMC recruitment closed. Fifth, longitudinal trajectories — KNoRMA collapses time. Rate-of-decline phenotypes, age-at-event survival modeling, exacerbation frequency as a quantitative trait, all of those are still open. And sixth, gene-by-gene and gene-by-environment interactions — they did pathway analysis but not formal epistasis or G-by-E with infection or modulator era.
