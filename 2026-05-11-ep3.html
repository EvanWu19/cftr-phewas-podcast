Blurb

Faino et al. is the first genome-wide-significant chronic Pseudomonas aeruginosa (Pa) modifier discovery in cystic fibrosis on a modern whole-genome substrate — the CF Genome Project (CFGP) cohort of 4,945 pancreatic-insufficient CF participants drawn from UNC GMS/CFLD, JHU TSS/CFRD, and UW EPIC, with longitudinal Pa-culture phenotype data from the 2017 CFFPR. A time-to-event Cox proportional-hazards GWAS with two endpoint definitions (Green two-of-three and three-of-four) identifies rs194810 on chromosome 16q12.2 — 35 kb downstream of CHP2 and 42 kb upstream of PRKCB — at P=2.21×10⁻⁸ with MAF 0.43. The minor A allele is protective: AA homozygotes acquire chronic Pa at median age 11 years vs 8 years for GG homozygotes (HR_GG_vs_AA=1.39, P=2.51×10⁻⁶). The signal is independent of lung function (KNoRMA-adjustment robust) and rs194810 is a lung-tissue eQTL for CHP2 (NES=0.35, P=1.5×10⁻¹⁰). TWAS implicates CHP2 most strongly (P=2.82×10⁻⁵). Among previously claimed Pa-modifier loci, TMC6 and MBL2 replicate nominally; CAV2 and DCTN4 do not. Proposed direction this paper suggests: a locked-locus phenome-wide pleiotropy scan of the chr16q12.2 CHP2/PRKCB haplotype across the registry-wide CFFPR phecode catalog, with rs194810 as the primary instrument. Open: causal-effector resolution (CHP2 vs PRKCB), modulator-era persistence, non-European generalizability, structural-variant contribution at the locus.

Plain-English glossary (alphabetical)

  • Chronic Pa infection (Green two-of-three / three-of-four) — operational definitions of persistent Pseudomonas in the CF airway based on positive cultures in 2 of 3 (or 3 of 4) consecutive years.
  • CFGP — Cystic Fibrosis Genome Project, the consortium WGS cohort (~4,945 participants) pooled from UNC, JHU, and UW contributing studies.
  • CFFPR — Cystic Fibrosis Foundation Patient Registry; longitudinal U.S. CF patient registry, source of the phenotype data used here.
  • CFTR — gene encoding the chloride/bicarbonate channel whose dysfunction causes CF.
  • CHP2 — calcineurin B homologous protein 2; binds NHE1 (sodium-hydrogen exchanger 1) regulating intracellular pH; also activates the calcineurin/NFAT immune-cell pathway.
  • Cox proportional hazards (Cox PH) — standard survival-regression model; estimates a hazard ratio per unit of covariate.
  • eQTL / NES — expression quantitative trait locus; normalized effect size of the variant on gene expression in a given GTEx tissue.
  • F508del (p.Phe508del) — most common CF-causing CFTR variant; ~85% of US CF chromosomes.
  • GENESIS / PC-AiR / PC-Relate — software pipeline for mixed-model GWAS with genetic-relatedness-matrix correction in related samples.
  • HEMT (highly effective modulator therapy) — small-molecule drugs partially restoring CFTR function; Trikafta (elexacaftor/tezacaftor/ivacaftor) is the current standard.
  • KNoRMA — Kulich Normalized Residual Mortality-Adjusted FEV1; canonical CF lung-function modifier phenotype (Taylor 2011).
  • Martingale residuals — Cox-regression residuals used as the outcome in score-test GWAS.
  • NHE1 — sodium-hydrogen exchanger isoform 1; CHP2 binding partner.
  • PI CF — pancreatic-insufficient cystic fibrosis; severe-genotype-class subset.
  • PRKCB — protein kinase C beta; the other gene flanking the chr16q12.2 locus (eQTL evidence in esophagus, tibial artery).
  • rs194810 — lead variant; chr16:23,793,566:G:A in GRCh38; MAF 0.43 in CFGP.
  • S-MultiXCan — multi-tissue TWAS method using GTEx eQTL reference data.
  • TWAS — transcriptome-wide association study; tests predicted gene expression against phenotype rather than individual variants.

This is a Cystic Fibrosis Genome Project paper, the exact cohort we eventually care about, on the exact phenotype we eventually care about. Senior authors are Elizabeth Blue, Ron Gibson, and Mike Bamshad at the University of Washington and Seattle Children's.

JORDAN That's a big deal — a paper on the CFGP doing a GWAS on chronic Pseudomonas in CF, the central infection endpoint.

ALEX Right. Let's pin down terms first.

JORDAN Chronic Pseudomonas aeruginosa airway infection.

ALEX Pseudomonas aeruginosa, abbreviated Pa, is a gram-negative bacterium that thrives in the abnormally thick CF airway mucus. Most people with CF acquire it by adolescence, and once it establishes a chronic foothold it accelerates lung function decline and shortens survival. "Chronic" means the bacterium has stopped being clearable by antibiotics and is reliably culturable over a defined time window. The two operational definitions used in this paper come from the work of Marianne Green and colleagues — "two of three" means a positive Pseudomonas culture in two of three consecutive years; "three of four" means at least one positive culture per year in three of four consecutive years. Two-of-three is more inclusive; three-of-four is stricter.

JORDAN Time-to-event GWAS. Cox proportional hazards. KNoRMA.

ALEX Time-to-event is just survival analysis applied to a phenotype that has an age of onset rather than a present-versus-absent status — in this case, age at first establishment of chronic Pseudomonas infection. Cox proportional hazards is the standard survival-analysis regression model, abbreviated Cox PH; it estimates a hazard ratio — the relative risk per unit of the covariate over time. KNoRMA stands for Kulich Normalized Residual Mortality-Adjusted, a CF-specific composite phenotype that adjusts forced expiratory volume in one second for age, sex, mortality attrition, and disease-progression effects — it's the canonical lung-function modifier-trait used across the CF Gene Modifier Consortium, established by Taylor 2011.

JORDAN PI CF, F508del, modulators, HEMT.

ALEX PI CF stands for pancreatic-insufficient cystic fibrosis — the severe CFTR-genotype subset where pancreatic function is essentially absent, defined by genotype class. F508del is the most common CF-causing CFTR variant, abbreviated p.Phe508del — a single-amino-acid deletion at position 508 of the CFTR protein; about 85 percent of CF chromosomes in the US registry carry it. Modulators are the small-molecule drugs that partially restore CFTR function — Trikafta, the combination of elexacaftor, tezacaftor, and ivacaftor, is the current standard of care for most CF patients. HEMT stands for highly effective modulator therapy, the umbrella term for this drug class.

JORDAN eQTL, TWAS.

ALEX eQTL is expression quantitative trait locus — a variant that doesn't change protein sequence but changes how much a nearby gene is transcribed. TWAS is transcriptome-wide association study — a post-GWAS approach where you use an eQTL reference dataset like GTEx to predict gene expression from genotype, then test predicted expression against the phenotype. It re-frames a variant-level GWAS as a gene-level test of imputed expression. S-MultiXCan is the multi-tissue version used in this paper.

JORDAN rs194810, CHP2, calcineurin B homologous protein 2.

ALEX rs194810 is the lead variant in this paper — a common single-nucleotide variant on chromosome 16, position 23,793,566 in GRCh38 coordinates, with minor allele frequency 43 percent in this CF cohort. It sits 35 kilobases downstream of the gene CHP2, which encodes calcineurin B homologous protein 2, and 42 kilobases upstream of PRKCB, encoding protein kinase C beta. CHP2 binds the sodium-hydrogen exchanger isoform 1 (NHE1), regulating intracellular pH, and also activates the calcineurin/NFAT immune-cell-activation pathway. PRKCB is involved in ventilator-induced lung injury signaling.

JORDAN Background.

ALEX Chronic Pseudomonas infection in CF is heritable — twin studies estimate roughly 76 percent heritability for chronic Pa colonization and 85 percent for age at chronic Pa onset, both from a single study by Stanke and colleagues in 2010. Earlier candidate-gene work and earlier GWAS identified six modifier loci for chronic Pa with mixed reproducibility: CHP2 (this paper), DCTN4 from Emond 2012, TMC6 and CAV2 from Emond 2015, MBL2 from candidate-gene work, and SFTPD from the same line. Before this paper there was no published large-cohort time-to-event GWAS on chronic Pa onset in CF on a modern WGS substrate. The CFGP — the Cystic Fibrosis Genome Project — assembled whole-genome sequencing on 4,945 PI CF participants pooled from four contributing studies: the UNC Gene Modifier Study and the CF Liver Disease study from Chapel Hill (n=1,784), the Twin and Sibling Study and CF-Related Diabetes study from Johns Hopkins (n=1,824), and the Early Pseudomonas Infection Control study (EPIC) from the University of Washington (n=1,337). The CFGP samples are sequenced at 30× coverage with GATK Best Practices QC. The question Faino et al. set up is: does a properly powered time-to-event GWAS on this whole-cohort WGS substrate identify new chronic-Pa modifier loci, and how do the previously claimed candidate-gene-era signals look in this larger, better-designed analysis.

JORDAN Methods.

ALEX Cox proportional hazards regression on age at chronic Pa onset, with two endpoint definitions in parallel — Green two-of-three and Green three-of-four. Sex, study site, and the first two principal components are model covariates. Birth cohort enters as a stratification factor because treatment eras differ. The genetic relatedness matrix enters as a random effect using the GENESIS pipeline, with PC-Relate and PC-AiR for principal component estimation. Two-stage approach — the null model is fit first with all covariates, residuals are inverse-normal transformed, and additive-effect score tests are run on high-quality SNVs and short indels with MAF above one percent and missingness below two percent. The headline analysis restricts to participants with greater-than-80-percent predicted European ancestry, with pancreatic-insufficient genotype, with bacterial culture data starting before age three and no gaps over two years, and not censored before age seven — that selection yields 2,358 individuals. Genome-wide significance is the standard 5×10⁻⁸ threshold. Candidate-variant analysis from previously published modifier loci is run with Bonferroni correction. TWAS is run via S-MultiXCan over all GTEx v8 tissues. The two-of-three and three-of-four definitions are run separately and concordance between them is reported.

JORDAN Design choice to push back on.

ALEX The same one as Vuckovic — European-ancestry-only is the primary analysis. The sensitivity analysis without ancestry filtering (n=2,587) shows the signal is largely preserved across populations, but the cohort composition is 93.9 percent European, 4.0 percent Hispanic, 1.8 percent African, 0.2 percent South Asian, 0.1 percent East Asian — there are not enough non-European participants for a per-stratum primary analysis even if the authors had wanted to.

JORDAN Results.

ALEX Headline finding. rs194810 on chromosome 16q12.2 reaches genome-wide significance for the two-of-three chronic Pa endpoint — P equals 2.21 times ten to the minus eight, beta minus-0.14 on the inverse-normal-transformed Martingale-residual scale. The minor A allele is protective. The variant is 35 kilobases downstream of CHP2 and 42 kilobases upstream of PRKCB. Effect size translation: PwCF homozygous for the A allele acquire chronic Pa at a median age of 11 years, heterozygotes at 10, GG homozygotes at 8. Hazard ratio for GG versus AA homozygotes is 1.39, P equals 2.51 times ten to the minus six. The signal does not attenuate when KNoRMA — lung function — is added as a covariate, so the effect is not just lung-function-mediated confounding. The signal is robust across two-of-three and three-of-four definitions (genome-wide Pearson beta correlation 0.72). TWAS shows CHP2 is the strongest imputed-expression hit at P equals 2.82 times ten to the minus five — not transcriptome-wide significant but the strongest signal in the analysis. Two further suggestive loci at chromosome 10p12.1 and chromosome 16p13.3 reach P below 5×10⁻⁶ but not genome-wide significance. On the candidate-variant replication arm: TMC6 rs34712518 replicates nominally (P=0.013), MBL2_B rs1800450 nominally (P=0.017), SFTPD rs3088308 nominally on the three-of-four definition (P=0.017); CAV2 and DCTN4 candidates do not replicate. Did they answer the framing question? Yes. They identified the first genome-wide-significant chronic Pa modifier locus on a modern WGS substrate (CHP2), they replicated MBL2 and TMC6 nominally, they failed to replicate CAV2 and DCTN4, and they functionally implicated CHP2-NHE1-calcineurin signaling.

JORDAN Proposed direction for us.

ALEX One concrete possible direction. The CHP2 finding sits in a chromosome-16q12.2 haplotype near both CHP2 and PRKCB; rs194810 is an eQTL for CHP2 in lung tissue (NES 0.35, P=1.5×10⁻¹⁰) and for PRKCB in esophagus and tibial artery — gene-level resolution is not closed. A possible direction is a focused fine-mapping plus phenome-wide pleiotropy scan of the chr16q12.2 CHP2/PRKCB locus across the registry-wide phenome — not just chronic Pa age-of-onset, but lung function trajectory, bronchiectasis severity, sinusitis, allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis, CFRD onset, growth, post-modulator-era spirometry preservation, and mortality — to test whether the CHP2/PRKCB protective haplotype is specific to Pa infection or pleiotropic across the CF complication phenome. In one formal sentence: a Faino-style locked-locus phenome-wide pleiotropy scan of the chr16q12.2 CHP2/PRKCB modifier haplotype on the CFGP WGS substrate against a pre-registered CFFPR phecode panel covering pulmonary, infectious, endocrine, gastrointestinal, growth, and mortality endpoints, with rs194810 as the primary instrument and PRKCB-vs-CHP2 effector-gene resolution as a secondary aim.
