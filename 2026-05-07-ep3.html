Blurb

Wright, Strug, Doshi, and colleagues anchored by Garry Cutting at Hopkins published the foundational paper of the modern CF modifier-genetics era in Nature Genetics in 2011. Combining GWAS in 1,978 F508del homozygotes (replicated in 557 family-based F508del homozygotes, joint meta P=1.49×10⁻⁹) with linkage analysis in 486 sibling pairs (LOD=5.03), they identified the chr11p13 EHF/APIP common-variant modifier locus and a chr20q13.2 linkage signal that has never resolved into a clean GWAS hit. The proposed direction this episode suggests, conditional on later inspection, is a phenome-wide scan of the chr11p13 lead variant — and the chr20q13.2 linkage region — across registry phenotypes, simultaneously a pipeline positive control and a novel-pleiotropy scan at non-lung outcomes. Open questions: causal gene at chr11p13 (EHF vs APIP vs regulatory), the never-resolved chr20q13.2 signal, modulator-era persistence of the EHF-as-CFTR-regulator mechanism.

Plain-English glossary

  • APIP — APAF1-interacting protein, also known as MtnB, an enzyme in the methionine salvage pathway.
  • CFTR — Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane conductance Regulator gene; loss of function causes cystic fibrosis.
  • EHF — ETS Homologous Factor, an epithelial-restricted transcription factor that regulates CFTR expression.
  • F508del homozygote / F508del/F508del — patient who inherited the F508del CFTR variant from both parents; restricting analysis to this group removes CFTR-locus variability.
  • F508del / p.Phe508del — three-DNA-letter deletion that removes phenylalanine at codon 508 from CFTR; the most common CF-causing variant (~70% of CF chromosomes worldwide).
  • FEV1 — forced expiratory volume in one second; standard pulmonary-function measurement, derived to lung-disease severity scores.
  • GWAS — genome-wide association study; tests every common variant individually for trait association.
  • HapMap reference panel — the 2010-era imputation reference; has been superseded by 1000 Genomes and TOPMed.
  • Imputation — statistical reconstruction of unmeasured SNPs from a reference panel.
  • Linkage analysis — family-based method that tests whether a chromosome region is shared more often than chance among affected siblings.
  • LOD score — log of odds; linkage-analysis statistic. LOD > 3 considered significant; > 3.6 considered genome-wide significant.
  • Modifier gene — a gene that affects severity in patients who already have the primary disease-causing mutation.
  • Penetrance — probability that a variant carrier develops disease; CFTR penetrance is essentially complete, modifier penetrance is partial.
  • QTL (quantitative trait locus) — chromosome region statistically associated with a continuous trait.
  • SNP / SNP array — single nucleotide polymorphism / array chip genotyping common SNPs cheaply.

Methods to lift

  • F508del/F508del subgroup restriction — cleanly removes CFTR-locus variability when testing for non-CFTR modifiers; trades modulator-era generalizability for signal isolation.
  • GWAS + linkage combined design — common-variant GWAS in unrelated cohort plus family-based linkage in sib-pairs; positive findings from both methods at a locus are independently strong; positive in one method only still informative.
  • Discovery + independent replication structure — 1,978-patient discovery + 557-patient family-based replication + joint meta-analysis is the canonical replication structure for a CF-modifier claim.
  • Affected-sib-pair variance-components linkage — 486 sibling pairs from the family-based study, LOD=5.03 at chr20q13.2.
  • Phenotype harmonization — adjusted lung-function score (precursor to KNoRMA per Taylor 2011 PMID 21462361) used as a continuous quantitative phenotype.
  • Genome-wide significance thresholds — p < 5×10⁻⁸ for GWAS; LOD > 3.6 for genome-wide-significant linkage.

ALEX Today's third episode is the foundational paper of modern cystic fibrosis modifier genetics: Wright, Strug, Doshi, and a long author list anchored by Garry Cutting at Hopkins, published in Nature Genetics in June 2011, titled "Genome-wide association and linkage identify modifier loci of lung disease severity in cystic fibrosis at 11p13 and 20q13.2." This is the paper that put the chr11p13 region — the EHF and APIP genes — on the map as a real, replicated, genome-wide-significant CF lung-disease modifier. Every subsequent CF modifier paper, including all the contemporary CFGP whole-genome analyses we have been reading, cites this paper as the starting point for the modern era. We are reading it because the chr11p13 locus has appeared in our catalog repeatedly, often in passing, and we owe it a careful pass at the original source.

JORDAN Glossary first. I want every term defined.

ALEX Cystic fibrosis is the autosomal recessive disease caused by mutations in the CFTR gene. F508del, written formally as p.Phe508del, is a deletion of three DNA letters in the CFTR coding sequence that removes a single amino acid — phenylalanine at position 508 — from the CFTR protein, and it is by far the most common disease-causing CFTR variant, accounting for roughly seventy percent of CF chromosomes worldwide. F508del/F508del means a person who inherited the F508del variant from both parents — sometimes called homozygous for F508del — and the convention of restricting genetic-modifier analyses to F508del homozygotes serves to remove variability from the CFTR locus itself, so that any signal seen elsewhere in the genome cannot be attributed to differences in the patient's CFTR variant. Lung disease severity, in this paper, is measured by an adjusted lung-function phenotype derived from forced expiratory volume in one second — abbreviated FEV1 — corrected for age, sex, height, and the survivor bias that plagues cross-sectional CF cohorts. Genome-wide association study, abbreviated GWAS, scans the entire genome by testing each common variant individually for statistical association with a trait. Linkage analysis is an older but complementary method that uses families — usually sibling pairs or larger pedigrees — and asks whether a particular chromosome region is shared more often than chance among affected siblings, suggesting a disease-causing variant in that region. SNP, single nucleotide polymorphism, is a single-letter DNA difference; SNP arrays are chips that genotype hundreds of thousands of pre-chosen common SNPs cheaply. EHF stands for ETS homologous factor, a transcription factor expressed in epithelial tissues that regulates expression of CFTR and other epithelial-barrier genes — meaning EHF turns on or off the very gene whose dysfunction causes cystic fibrosis in the first place. APIP stands for APAF1-interacting protein, also known as MtnB, an enzyme in the methionine salvage pathway with a separate body of literature on inflammation. Quantitative trait locus, abbreviated QTL, is a genome region statistically associated with a continuous trait. LOD score, log of the odds, is the test statistic for linkage analysis — a LOD above three is considered significant evidence of linkage, and a LOD above 5.03, the value in this paper, is genome-wide significant. Modifier gene is the term for a gene that affects disease severity in someone who has inherited the primary disease-causing mutation; in CF, modifiers explain why two F508del homozygotes can have wildly different clinical courses. Penetrance, again, is the probability a variant carrier develops disease — for the primary CFTR variants in CF, penetrance is essentially complete; for modifier variants, penetrance is partial.

JORDAN What was unknown when this paper was written?

ALEX By 2011, three things were known and two were not. Known: cystic fibrosis is caused by CFTR mutations; clinical severity, especially of lung disease, varies dramatically among patients with identical CFTR genotypes; and twin and sibling studies — the same family-based studies anchored by NCT00037778 and NCT00676169 that we have discussed in earlier episodes — had estimated heritability of CF lung disease at roughly fifty percent independent of CFTR. Unknown: which specific genes outside CFTR were responsible for that fifty percent. Candidate-gene-era studies had nominated dozens of plausible modifiers — TGFB1, MBL2, IFRD1, others — but none had been replicated reliably across cohorts. The candidate-gene approach was producing a graveyard of irreproducible claims. The field needed a hypothesis-free, genome-wide, replication-anchored study to clear the air. This paper is that study. It is the modern landmark that ended the candidate-gene era for CF and opened the GWAS era.

JORDAN How did they tackle it?

ALEX They combined two complementary genetic methods on two complementary cohorts. The discovery cohort for GWAS was 1,978 F508del homozygous CF patients with adjusted lung-function phenotypes, drawn from the U.S. Gene Modifier Study Group consortium. They restricted to F508del homozygotes for the reason we walked through earlier: removing CFTR-locus variability isolates the modifier signal. They used SNP-array genotyping with imputation against the HapMap reference panel — this was 2011, before the 1000 Genomes era was fully consolidated — and tested each common variant for association with lung function, adjusting for ancestry principal components and study site. They reported findings against the standard genome-wide significance threshold of p less than 5 times 10 to the minus 8. They then did a separate replication GWAS in 557 F508del homozygotes from the family-based Hopkins twin and sibling study, an independent cohort with no overlap with the discovery sample. Finally, they did a linkage analysis on 486 sibling pairs from the family-based study, using affected-sib-pair variance-components methods. Linkage and GWAS test fundamentally different hypotheses — linkage finds rare large-effect variants that segregate within families, GWAS finds common smaller-effect variants in unrelated individuals — so positive findings from both methods at the same locus are independently strong, and findings from one method but not the other still teach us something distinct. ALEX pushes back here on the F508del/F508del restriction: it isolates modifier signal cleanly, but it also means the paper cannot speak to whether the same modifier loci act in patients with non-F508del CFTR genotypes, and modulator-era treatment populations now include many non-F508del patients on Trikafta and other regimens, so the generalizability question is real.

JORDAN What did they find?

ALEX Three key findings. First, GWAS identified a genome-wide-significant association near EHF and APIP on chromosome 11p13, with a discovery p-value of 3.34 times 10 to the minus 8 in the 1,978-patient F508del homozygote sample. The signal replicated in the independent 557-patient family-based cohort at p equal to 0.006 — well below replication-tier significance — and the joint meta-analysis across the discovery and replication cohorts gave p equal to 1.49 times 10 to the minus 9. That is a clean, properly-replicated, genome-wide-significant CF lung-disease modifier locus, the first one of the GWAS era. Second, the linkage analysis identified a quantitative trait locus on chromosome 20q13.2 with a LOD score of 5.03 — well above the three-point-six threshold for genome-wide significant linkage — but no specific common SNP at that locus reached GWAS significance in the same data. The 20q13.2 signal is real but its underlying causal variant is not pinpointed. Third, the EHF/APIP signal restricted to F508del homozygotes is an important caveat: the analysis was deliberately scoped to that subgroup, and whether the same locus modifies severity in patients with other CFTR genotypes is not addressed by this paper. Did they answer the question they posed? Largely yes — they identified one robust, replicated common-variant modifier locus, and one linkage-only signal that defines a region for future fine-mapping. They did not produce a comprehensive list of modifiers, and they did not pin down the causal gene at chr11p13 — both EHF and APIP sit in the credible interval, and the candidate-gene story for either is plausible. They were appropriately careful not to overclaim.

JORDAN How does this connect back to where we have been heading with the CFTR PheWAS?

ALEX One direction this paper suggests, in plain English: the chr11p13 locus has been a positive control for CF modifier biology for fifteen years, replicated in Corvol 2015 and Zhou 2023. A phenome-wide CFTR PheWAS that uses chr11p13 as a known-positive anchor — testing the lead variant against every available registry phenotype, including non-pulmonary outcomes — would do two things at once. It would confirm the pipeline is functioning by re-finding the lung-severity association as a positive control, and it would simultaneously interrogate whether the same locus has detectable effects on CFRD, gastrointestinal complications, mental health, modulator response, transplant outcomes, or mortality — none of which the original Wright paper or the lung-function-restricted follow-ups have evaluated. The formal one-sentence version: a phenome-wide scan of the chr11p13 EHF/APIP lead variant from Wright et al. 2011 — and ideally also the chr20q13.2 linkage region, which has never resolved into a clean GWAS hit — across the registry-wide phenome including non-pulmonary, post-modulator-era, and longitudinal outcomes, would simultaneously serve as a pipeline positive control and as a pleiotropy scan with novel-finding potential at non-lung phenotypes. Frame this as one possible direction this episode suggests.

JORDAN Given that, what does the paper close, what does it leave open, and what would Runyu need to verify?

ALEX Conditional on that proposal, the paper closes the discovery question for the chr11p13 lung-severity modifier locus in F508del homozygotes — the locus is real, replicated, and accepted by the field. It leaves several things open. The causal-gene question at chr11p13 — whether EHF, APIP, both, or a regulatory variant affecting another nearby gene drives the signal — is unresolved by this paper and remains contested even now. The chr20q13.2 linkage signal has never been resolved into a specific genome-wide-significant SNP in any subsequent paper, including Corvol 2015 and Zhou 2023 — a quietly open follow-up question, possibly involving a structural variant or a rare-variant burden invisible to common-variant GWAS. The pleiotropy question — does chr11p13 affect non-pulmonary CF complications? — is entirely unaddressed. And the modulator-era question — does the EHF-as-CFTR-regulator mechanism still matter when CFTR function is pharmacologically restored by Trikafta? — is unaddressed by 2011 design but is the most clinically important transfer question. What we would need to verify before committing: whether the consortium has run any non-lung-function PheWAS-style scan at chr11p13 already — published or in progress; whether the registry has the longitudinal phenotypes needed to test modulator-era persistence of the signal; whether non-F508del patients in the registry are linkable to genotype data and powered enough to address the generalization question; and whether the chr20q13.2 region has any structural-variant or rare-variant follow-up underway. None of this implies a research direction has been chosen.
