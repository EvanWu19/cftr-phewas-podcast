Blurb

Sun, Chiou, Traylor, Benner, and the UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project consortium — a thirteen-pharma-company precompetitive collaboration with senior author Christopher Whelan at Biogen — published in Nature in October 2023 the foundational paper for biobank-scale open-access plasma proteomics. The paper measured 2,941 protein analytes spanning 2,923 unique proteins across 54,219 UK Biobank participants on the antibody-based Olink Explore 3072 proximity extension assay platform, then ran cis and trans pQTL mapping per protein against UKBB's existing imputed genotype data. The headline numbers: 14,287 statistically significant primary pQTLs at a multiple-testing-corrected threshold of P below 1.7×10⁻¹¹, distributed across 3,760 independent genetic regions, with 81 percent of associations previously unreported; 82.6 percent of measured proteins had at least one detected pQTL and 66.9 percent had a cis pQTL within one megabase of the encoding gene; 1,955 cis and 12,332 trans pQTLs in the headline catalog; genomic inflation lambda controlled at 1.02. The paper demonstrates four classes of downstream use: therapeutic-target extension via Mendelian-randomization on PCSK9; long-range epistasis between ABO blood-group genotype and FUT2 secretor status on plasma concentrations of gastrointestinal-tissue-enriched proteins; ancestry-specific pQTL mapping in non-European subgroups; and disease-mechanism disambiguation at COVID-19 susceptibility loci. The proposed direction this episode suggests, conditional on later inspection of CFGP variant calls and modifier-locus list, is a pre-registered cis pQTL look-up analysis on the publicly-released UKB-PPP summary statistics for every CFTR-modifier-gene candidate whose protein product is on the Olink Explore 3072 panel — providing an immediate biological-plausibility check on whether the modifier-locus lead variant is regulating the candidate gene's plasma protein concentration in the general population. The open question is whether the cis pQTL effect direction observed in non-CF UKBB participants — where the CFTR pathway is functional and the proteome is operating in its normal state — generalizes to CF patients, where the airway and pancreas are in chronically dysregulated inflammatory states that may distort systemic plasma protein clearance.

Plain-English glossary

  • ABO — the gene encoding the proteins that determine your ABO blood type; carries large-effect functional polymorphisms that produce the A, B, AB, and O phenotypes.
  • Antibody — an immune-system protein that binds a specific spot on a specific other protein; in laboratory assays, antibodies are used as molecular tags for detecting target proteins of interest.
  • cis pQTL — a protein quantitative trait locus located within roughly one megabase of the gene encoding the affected protein; usually interpreted as a regulatory variant for that specific gene.
  • Coefficient of variation (CV) — a quality-control statistic measuring intra-individual variability across duplicate samples; protein assays with low CV are considered more reliable.
  • Epistasis — a genetic interaction in which the effect of one variant depends on the genotype at another, unlinked variant; here, the joint ABO + FUT2 effect on protein levels exceeds the sum of the independent effects.
  • eQTL (expression quantitative trait locus) — a genetic variant that associates with the messenger-RNA expression level of a gene; the RNA-level analogue of a pQTL.
  • FUT2 — a fucosyltransferase enzyme that determines whether a person expresses the H antigen and downstream ABO antigens on the surface of their gut and other secretory-tissue cells; people with two non-functional FUT2 copies are called non-secretors.
  • Imputed genotype data — genetic variant calls inferred from a reference haplotype panel rather than measured directly; UKBB has imputed genotypes for ~16 million variants per participant.
  • Limit of detection (LOD) — the lowest protein concentration a particular assay can reliably distinguish from background noise; samples below LOD are commonly censored or modeled separately.
  • Mendelian randomization (MR) — a statistical technique that uses genetic variants as natural-experiment instruments to estimate the causal effect of a putative exposure on a downstream outcome.
  • Olink Explore 3072 — a specific commercial proximity-extension-assay platform that measures roughly 2,941 different proteins in parallel from a single blood sample, organized into eight thematic protein panels.
  • PEA (proximity extension assay) — laboratory technique that uses pairs of antibodies tagged with short DNA barcodes; when both antibodies bind the same target protein simultaneously, the barcodes link into a template that gets amplified and counted by a sequencer.
  • Plasma — the cell-free fluid portion of blood; what is left after the red and white cells have been spun out.
  • pQTL (protein quantitative trait locus) — a genetic variant that associates with the blood concentration of a specific protein.
  • Pre-competitive consortium — an arrangement in which competing companies share data and methods at a stage before any of them are competing in product development.
  • Proteome — the full set of proteins a cell, tissue, or body fluid produces; the downstream functional layer between the genome and the phenotype.
  • trans pQTL — a protein quantitative trait locus located more than a megabase away from the encoding gene, often on a different chromosome; usually interpreted as an indirect regulatory effect through some intermediary biology.
  • UKB-PPP (UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project) — a precompetitive consortium of thirteen biopharmaceutical companies that funded the proteomic profiling described in this paper.

Methods to lift

  • Olink Explore 3072 PEA platform — antibody-pair proximity extension assay measuring 2,941 protein analytes spanning 2,923 unique proteins across eight thematic panels (cardiometabolic, cardiometabolic II, inflammation, inflammation II, neurology, neurology II, oncology, oncology II); each panel costs roughly the same per sample whether you use one or all eight.
  • Dilution-stratified protein measurement — proteins are assayed at four expected-concentration dilutions (1:1, 1:10, 1:1000, 1:100,000); coefficients of variation vary across dilution factors and the proportion of samples below the limit of detection is dilution-dependent — both are important QC dimensions to report.
  • Sample-selection three-arm design — a representative random baseline (n=46,595) plus a consortium-selected disease-enriched arm (n=6,376) plus a COVID-19 imaging arm (n=1,268); pQTL discovery uses the European-ancestry randomly-selected baseline (n=34,557) as discovery and the held-out 17,806 as replication.
  • Multiple-testing-corrected pQTL threshold — the headline statistical threshold is P below 1.7×10⁻¹¹, which corrects for the number of proteins tested in addition to the standard genome-wide threshold.
  • Cis-window definition (1 Mb) — the convention used here for distinguishing cis from trans pQTLs is one megabase from the gene encoding the protein; some prior pQTL papers use 250 kb or 500 kb, so the cis-window choice is a reportable methodological decision.
  • Ancestry-stratified pQTL mapping — discovery is European-ancestry-only, with non-European subgroups analyzed separately for ancestry-specific pQTL discovery rather than pooled with European data using PC adjustment alone.
  • Mendelian-randomization with cis pQTLs as instruments — using a cis pQTL as the instrumental variable for circulating protein concentration provides cleaner causal-inference structure for therapeutic-target validation than using disease-associated variants directly.
  • Long-range epistasis detection — testing pairs of unlinked variants for joint effects on a single protein concentration is a tractable analysis when both variants are common (ABO and FUT2 are both common-variant loci); the analysis would be much harder for rare-variant epistasis.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFGP WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage:

A pre-registered cis pQTL look-up analysis on the publicly-released UKB-PPP summary statistics, restricted to the candidate-modifier-gene panel locked from a CFGP-internal discovery scan (SLC26A9, EHF, APIP, CHP2, CEBPB, PRSS1, plus chr5p13 and chr11p13 and chr20q13.2 region candidates and any newly-discovered loci), checking for each candidate gene whether its protein product is on the Olink Explore 3072 panel and, if so, whether the modifier-locus lead variant has a statistically significant cis pQTL effect on that protein at a pre-registered multiple-testing-corrected threshold, with the cis-window definition (one megabase from the encoding gene) and the ancestry-stratification choice (European-ancestry-discovery look-up versus per-ancestry-stratum look-up) pre-registered before the look-up is run.

What to verify first

  • Panel coverage: which of the candidate-modifier-gene proteins (SLC26A9, EHF, APIP, CHP2, CEBPB, PRSS1, etc.) are actually measured on Olink Explore 3072 — many cell-surface transporters and intracellular transcription factors are not on antibody panels at all, and the panel manifest needs to be checked gene-by-gene before any look-up plan is finalized.
  • Whether any small CFGP proteomics sub-cohort exists or could be generated with the same Olink panel — even a few hundred CF-affected plasma samples would let us check the disease-state-shift on the cis pQTL effect direction directly, rather than assuming UKBB-derived effect directions transfer.
  • The published version of the UKB-PPP summary statistics current as of the analysis cut-off date and the file format of the public release — the file format determines how easy or hard a programmatic look-up will be to script.
  • Whether the cis-window definition (1 Mb in UKB-PPP convention) materially affects the look-up calls relative to a tighter window — some CF papers use 250 kb or 500 kb windows for fine-mapping; the choice should be pre-registered.
  • The multiple-testing burden of the look-up: how many candidate-gene-by-cis-window comparisons will the look-up actually run, and what corrected threshold do we need.
  • Whether the consortium-selected enrichment in the original UKB-PPP cohort affects the cis pQTL effect-size estimates — the consortium-selected arm was enriched for cardiometabolic, inflammation, neurology, and oncology cases, which may slightly bias the proteome distribution and therefore pQTL effect magnitudes for proteins related to those disease classes.
  • Whether the cis pQTL effect direction in the general population is biologically interpretable as a positive control for the CFGP modifier-direction call — the modifier signal in CFGP is on a CF-affected disease background and the pQTL signal in UKB-PPP is in the absence of CF disease; the directions might agree without that being meaningful, or disagree without that ruling out the modifier effect.

The paper appeared in Nature in October 2023, titled "Plasma proteomic associations with genetics and health in the UK Biobank." We are reading it because it represents a fundamentally different kind of biobank-scale resource than anything we have covered so far: not genotype-to-disease, but genotype-to-protein-to-disease — a middle layer between DNA variation and clinical outcome that opens up an entirely new class of CFTR PheWAS extension.

JORDAN Stop. Vocabulary pass. I think proteomics is going to be a whole new domain for me.

ALEX It is. Let me lay out the terms we will need. The proteome is the full set of proteins a cell, tissue, or body fluid produces — it is the downstream functional layer of biology, between the genome (the DNA blueprint) and the phenotype (the observable trait). Plasma is the cell-free fluid portion of blood — what is left after you spin down the red and white cells. Plasma proteome means the proteins circulating in the blood, which include hormones, antibodies, clotting factors, inflammatory signals, and proteins leaked from tissues. A proximity extension assay, abbreviated PEA, is a laboratory technique that uses pairs of antibodies — each antibody binds one specific spot on a target protein, and each antibody is tagged with a short DNA barcode — when both antibodies bind the same protein molecule simultaneously, the two DNA tags come into close proximity and can be linked into a single DNA template that gets amplified and counted by a sequencer. The Olink Explore 3072 panel is a specific commercial PEA platform that measures roughly 2,941 different proteins in parallel from a single blood sample. A protein quantitative trait locus, abbreviated pQTL, is a genetic variant that associates with the blood concentration of a specific protein — analogous to how an expression quantitative trait locus, or eQTL, is a variant that associates with the messenger-RNA level of a gene. A cis pQTL is one located within roughly one megabase of the gene encoding the affected protein — usually interpreted as a regulatory variant for that gene. A trans pQTL is one located more than a megabase away, often on a different chromosome — interpreted as an indirect regulatory effect through some intermediary biology. Mendelian randomization, abbreviated MR, is a statistical technique that uses genetic variants associated with a putative causal exposure as natural-experiment instruments to estimate the causal effect of that exposure on a downstream outcome — useful for distinguishing whether a protein actually causes a disease versus merely associating with it. Pre-competitive consortium means that the participating pharmaceutical companies share data and methods at a stage before any of them are actually competing in product development — they each get the resource, the resource is published openly, and only the downstream therapeutic-target validation is competitive. The ABO gene encodes the proteins that determine your ABO blood type. The FUT2 gene encodes a fucosyltransferase that determines whether you express the H antigen and downstream ABO antigens on the surface of your gut and other secretory-tissue cells — people with two non-functional FUT2 copies are called non-secretors. With those terms in hand, we can talk about the paper.

JORDAN What was the problem they were solving — what was unknown before this paper?

ALEX The gap they were filling is the disconnect between human genetics and drug discovery. Population-scale GWAS studies routinely identify variants associated with disease, but turning a variant into a drug target requires knowing which gene the variant regulates and what the regulated protein actually does in the relevant tissue. Plasma protein measurements at population scale would tell you, for any genetic variant linked to disease, whether that variant also moves the concentration of any of three thousand circulating proteins — and if it does, the affected protein becomes a candidate causal mediator of the disease and a candidate drug target. Two related smaller efforts had measured plasma proteomics in tens of thousands of participants — the SomaScan-based studies, the earlier Olink-based studies — but typically without participant-level access linked to deep phenotyping, and at smaller scale. UKB-PPP would scale that up to fifty-four thousand participants in a cohort with full access to UKBB's deeply linked NHS phenotype data and existing UKBB exome and array genotypes — and the data would be publicly released, supporting downstream use across the whole research community. Before this paper, no proteo-genomic resource at this scale existed in the open public domain.

JORDAN How did they actually do it? Mechanically.

ALEX They selected 54,219 UKBB participants from the cohort. The selection had three parts: a randomly-selected baseline sample of 46,595 participants representative of the overall UKBB population; a 6,376-person consortium-selected cohort enriched for specific disease cases of interest to the funding pharma companies; and a 1,268-person COVID-19 imaging cohort sampled at multiple visits. They drew blood plasma at baseline visits, ran the Olink Explore 3072 antibody-based PEA platform on each sample to measure 2,941 protein analytes spanning eight thematic protein panels — cardiometabolic, cardiometabolic two, inflammation, inflammation two, neurology, neurology two, oncology, oncology two — yielding measurements for 2,923 unique proteins per individual. Quality control: protein-level coefficient of variation across duplicate samples ranged from 1.8 percent to 27.2 percent with a median of 6.7 percent, batch and plate effects were below ten percent for all proteins, and 73.2 percent of analytes had less than 30 percent of samples below the limit of detection. For the GWAS step, they used UKBB's existing imputed genotype data — up to sixteen point one million imputed variants per participant — and ran pQTL mapping per protein in the European-ancestry randomly-selected baseline sample of 34,557 participants as discovery, with 17,806 European-ancestry samples held out as replication, and ancestry-stratified analyses run separately in the non-European subgroups. ALEX should push back on a design choice here. The sample-selection strategy combines a representative random baseline with two non-random enrichments — the consortium-selected cases and the COVID-19 imaging participants — and although the team carefully reports the demographic and disease enrichments produced by this combined sampling, the headline pQTL discovery numbers come from the pooled cohort. The split-discovery design partially handles this, but anyone using the pQTL summary statistics for a specific downstream analysis needs to remember that the consortium-selected enrichment is for diseases the funding companies care about, which is not a neutral selection.

JORDAN What did they actually find — what is the headline?

ALEX The numbers worth carrying out of this paper are stark. They identified 14,287 statistically significant primary pQTLs at a stringent multiple-testing-corrected threshold of P below 1.7 times ten to the negative eleven, distributed across 3,760 independent genetic regions. Eighty-one percent of those associations had not been previously reported. Of the 2,922 proteins tested, 2,414 — that is 82.6 percent — had at least one detected pQTL, and 66.9 percent had a cis pQTL within one megabase of the encoding gene. The breakdown was 1,955 cis pQTLs and 12,332 trans pQTLs. Genomic inflation was well-controlled at lambda 1.02. They also did several biological deep-dives. They demonstrated long-range epistatic effects of ABO blood-group genotype combined with FUT2 secretor status on the plasma concentrations of multiple proteins enriched in gastrointestinal tissue — meaning that two unlinked genetic variants jointly determine the level of a circulating protein in a way that neither variant predicts on its own. They showed that pQTLs for the protein PCSK9 — a well-known drug target for LDL-cholesterol lowering — could be used as Mendelian-randomization instruments to extend our understanding of which downstream endpoints PCSK9 modulation might affect, beyond LDL itself. And at the COVID-19-susceptibility loci that had been identified in earlier UKBB GWAS, they used the proteomic layer to disentangle which specific genes and proteins at each locus were perturbed — providing mechanistic clues at loci that GWAS alone had only narrowed down to a region. Did they answer the question they posed? Yes, fully. They produced the first open-access proteo-genomic resource at the fifty-thousand-participant scale, with both the protein measurements and the pQTL summary statistics publicly available, and they demonstrated four classes of downstream use — therapeutic target extension, epistasis discovery, ancestry-specific pQTL mapping, and disease-mechanism disambiguation at GWAS loci.
