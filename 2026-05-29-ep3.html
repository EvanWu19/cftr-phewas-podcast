Blurb

Atteih, Raraigh, Blackman, Cutting, and Collaco use the CF Twin and Sibling Study (n=1,677, enrolled 2000–2013) to test whether low birth weight (LBW <2500g) or small for gestational age (SGA <10th percentile) predict long-term respiratory and nutritional outcomes in cystic fibrosis. After family-clustered, multivariable adjustment, LBW predicts lower BMI z-score at ages 2, 6, and 12 (all P<0.001) and higher FEV1 at age 6 (+2.9% per kg birth weight, P=0.013); the effects attenuate by age 18. SGA predicts BMI z-score only at age 2. A post hoc analysis shows lower height z-scores at all ages for both LBW and SGA, suggesting persistent linear-growth deficit. Predictors of birth weight itself: female sex (-190g), pancreatic insufficiency (-220g), gestational age (+120g/week). One proposed direction: an age-window-stratified CFTR-modifier PheWAS using the Atteih covariate menu as the baseline adjustment layer. Open: whether the covariate template transfers to the modulator era and whether birth weight is captured longitudinally for the full CFFPR-linked WGS cohort.

Plain-English glossary

  • BMI z-score: Standardized body mass index relative to age- and sex-matched population norms; used here instead of percentile due to non-normal distribution.
  • CF Twin and Sibling Study (NCT00037778, Cutting/JHU): U.S. cohort recruiting CF patients with at least one twin or sibling also with CF; supports family-based modifier studies.
  • CFFPR: Cystic Fibrosis Foundation Patient Registry — national U.S. CF registry.
  • CFTR: Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane conductance Regulator gene; defective in CF.
  • F508del: Most common CF-causing CFTR variant (~70% of CF alleles, ~50% of patients are homozygous).
  • Family clustering: Statistical adjustment for correlated observations within families (siblings, twins).
  • FEV1 percent predicted: Forced expiratory volume in 1 second, expressed as a percentage of the age/sex/height-matched population norm.
  • GLI (Global Lung Function Initiative): Reference-equation set for FEV1 prediction across age, sex, height, and ancestry.
  • Gestational age: Pregnancy duration in weeks; preterm = <37 weeks.
  • LBW (low birth weight): Birth weight <2,500 grams; standard WHO cutoff.
  • Meconium ileus (MI): Intestinal obstruction at birth from abnormally thick meconium; ~15% of CF newborns.
  • Modulator therapy: Small-molecule CFTR-protein-rescuing drugs (ivacaftor, lumacaftor, tezacaftor, elexacaftor); excluded from outcome time-points in this paper.
  • Pancreatic insufficiency (PI): Inadequate pancreatic exocrine enzyme production; ~85% of CF patients; requires enzyme replacement.
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa (PA): Chronic airway pathogen of CF; the main driver of progressive lung decline.
  • SGA (small for gestational age): Weight <10th percentile for gestational age using U.S. norms; partly overlaps with LBW but mostly captures different patients.
  • Singleton gestation: Single fetus (not twin or higher-order multiple).
  • Twin-Sibling Study cohort recruitment bias: Requires two living affected siblings; selects for better-than-average CF survival.

Methods to lift

  • Twin-Sibling Study + CFFPR linkage — twin-sibling enrollment with U.S. national registry follow-through; canonical CFF-cohort family-design substrate.
  • GLI reference equations for FEV1 — current standard for population-norm-relative lung function in non-CF-specific contexts; CF-specific Kulich equations are the alternative.
  • CDC BMI norms — population-normalized child/adolescent BMI percentiles, converted to z-scores for regression.
  • Maximum BMI percentile / max FEV1 within fixed age windows — operationalization of longitudinal pediatric outcomes for cross-sectional regression; primary analysis uses max, sensitivity analysis uses mean.
  • Pancreatic status determination hierarchy — genotype first, fecal elastase/fat second, enzyme-use as fallback.
  • Family-clustered linear regression — Stata IC 15.1 implementation; clustering by family to handle sib correlations.
  • Bonferroni correction across age windows — 0.05/4 for BMI, 0.05/3 for FEV1.
  • F508del-homozygous-only sensitivity analysis — drops FEV1 birth-weight effect, suggests CFTR-genotype-correlated mediation.
  • Height z-score post hoc analysis — captures persistent linear-growth deficit independent of BMI catch-up.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage: design an age-window-stratified CFTR-modifier-locus association scan in the CFF WGS cohort against BMI z-score (windows: 2–3, 6–7, 12–13, 18–19 yrs) and FEV1 percent predicted (windows: 6–7, 12–13, 18–19 yrs), adjusting for the empirically-validated Atteih covariate menu — sex, race/ethnicity, twin gestation, gestational age, meconium ileus, age at diagnosis, pancreatic insufficiency — with family-clustered standard errors, per-window Bonferroni or FDR correction, and explicit modulator-era stratification. Test whether known sentinels (SLC26A9, SLC6A14, EHF/APIP, chr20q13.2, FTO, ADAMTS5) show window-specific effects.

What to verify first

  • Whether the CFF WGS cohort overlaps the Twin-Sibling Study and therefore inherits its family-clustering structure and parental-recall birth-weight measurement.
  • Whether birth weight, gestational age, and SGA status are captured for the broader CFFPR-linked WGS cohort or only the twin-sibling subset.
  • Whether the cohort spans the modulator era (post-2012 ivacaftor; post-2019 Trikafta) and whether stratification breaks effect sizes from this pre-modulator cohort.
  • Whether GLI reference equations or CF-specific Kulich equations should be the FEV1 standard for CFF-cohort analysis.
  • Whether height z-score is captured longitudinally with sufficient density to use as an unconfounded linear-growth outcome.
  • Whether age-of-diagnosis is operationally identical to newborn-screening status in the modern cohort; universal screening was achieved in the U.S. only by 2010, so older cohort members have non-screening diagnoses.

Gap-map: closes / leaves open

  • Closes: empirical covariate menu for BMI and FEV1 in CFF-cohort substrate; rule-out of higher SGA/LBW rates in CF full-term singletons vs general population; confirmation of Darrah-2008-style birth-weight × age-6-FEV1 link at larger sample; quantitative effect sizes per kg birth weight on BMI z-score at each age window.
  • Leaves open: any modifier-locus interaction with birth weight; mechanism of birth-weight × lung-function association (placental CFTR expression hypothesis floated but not tested); whether modulator-era patients show the same trajectories; whether catch-up growth interventions in the first two years of life modify long-term BMI trajectory; why F508del-homozygote-only sensitivity analysis drops the FEV1 effect; whether linear-growth deficit (persistent height z-score effect) traces to a distinct gene-environment etiology from BMI deficit.

JORDAN Let's start at zero. What are the terms I need to know.

ALEX I will go slowly. Cystic fibrosis, CF, is the autosomal recessive disease caused by two pathogenic variants in CFTR, the cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator gene. Birth weight is straightforwardly the infant's weight at delivery. Low birth weight, LBW, is the standard cutoff of under two thousand five hundred grams. Gestational age is the duration of pregnancy in weeks. Small for gestational age, SGA, means weight below the tenth percentile for the infant's gestational age, using U.S. reference norms. The two definitions overlap only partly; a baby can be small without being preterm or preterm without being small. Preterm means born before thirty-seven weeks. Twin and sibling study refers to a cohort design that intentionally recruits CF patients who have at least one twin or sibling also with CF, which gives you matched pairs for genetic-modifier studies because the affected siblings share half their non-CFTR genome on average. The CF Foundation Patient Registry, CFFPR, is the national U.S. registry that tracks every CF patient. F508del is the most common CF-causing variant; about half of CF patients are homozygous for it, meaning they carry it on both copies of CFTR. Pancreatic insufficiency, PI, means the pancreas does not produce enough digestive enzymes; about 85 percent of CF patients are PI and require enzyme replacement. FEV1 percent predicted is the standard lung function measure: the volume of air a person can forcibly exhale in one second, expressed as a percentage of the population-norm value for someone of their age, sex, and height. The Global Lung Function Initiative, GLI, is the reference equation set used here. BMI is body mass index, weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared. BMI z-score is the standardized deviation from age-and-sex-matched norms. Meconium ileus, MI, is intestinal obstruction at birth caused by abnormally thick meconium, occurring in about fifteen percent of CF newborns. Pseudomonas aeruginosa, abbreviated PA in this paper, is the chronic airway pathogen that drives CF lung decline. Newborn screening, universal in the U.S. by twenty ten, identifies CF very early via heel-stick blood test.

JORDAN Okay. What is the question.

ALEX The question is whether being born small, by either LBW or SGA, predicts how the CF patient does later in life, specifically nutrition and lung function. Two things motivate this. First, prior literature, including a Darrah paper they reference, suggested CF babies are smaller at birth than non-CF babies and that smaller birth weight tracks with worse lung function later. Second, if birth weight predicts adult outcomes, that creates a clinical handle: you might be able to intervene early. The unknown was specifically what predicts long-term outcomes, because most prior work was single-center, small, or only cross-sectional.

JORDAN Why is this relevant to a modifier-genetics project?

ALEX Because a CFTR PheWAS that uses lung function or BMI as outcomes has to decide what covariates to adjust for. Birth weight, SGA status, pancreatic function, meconium ileus, and gestational age are all candidate covariates. This paper tells you which ones matter at which ages, with concrete effect sizes, in a CFF-cohort substrate. That is exactly the input a modifier study needs to specify its statistical model.

JORDAN Methods.

ALEX The cohort, CF Twin and Sibling Study, P.I. Cutting, enrolled twenty eighty-six patients in one thousand eighteen families between October two thousand and March twenty thirteen, with sixteen hundred seventy-seven having birth-weight data. Outcomes were extracted from chart review, questionnaires, and CFF Patient Registry linkage through December twenty sixteen, under Johns Hopkins IRB number NA-zero-zero-zero-three-five-six-five-nine. Birth weight and gestational age came from charts when available, parental recall otherwise. LBW was the standard under-2500-gram cutoff. SGA used U.S. norms for the tenth percentile by gestational age. The primary lung outcome was maximum FEV1 percent predicted, using the Global Lung Function Initiative reference equations, measured in three age windows: six to seven, twelve to thirteen, eighteen to nineteen years. The primary nutritional outcome was maximum BMI percentile, CDC norms, measured in four age windows: two to three, six to seven, twelve to thirteen, eighteen to nineteen. Patients on a CFTR modulator or post-transplant were excluded from those time-points. Pseudomonas status was a binary indicator of any positive culture in that window. Pancreatic status came from genotype for ninety-four percent, fecal elastase or fat for the rest, or enzyme use as fallback. Statistics were in Stata IC fifteen-point-one. Two-sided P less than 0.05 was the threshold for confounder screening; the Bonferroni-corrected significance thresholds were 0.0125 for BMI tests across four time-points and 0.0167 for FEV1 across three. Linear regressions for continuous outcomes, logistic for binary, all clustered by family to handle relatedness. Pushback on-air: parental recall for birth weight introduces bias, and the Twin-Sibling Study population has higher lung function than the general CF population because of the recruitment criterion of having two living affected siblings, which biases the substrate toward better survival.
