How did this episode land for you?

One-paragraph blurb

Labenski and colleagues from the Hannover Medical School group (Tümmler lab), working within the European CF Twin and Sibling Study, applied a three-stage haplotype-guided hierarchical fine-mapping pipeline to three immunorelevant candidate CF modifier genes — STAT3, IL1B, IFNGR1 — across 101 F508del-homozygous CF families. The intragenic 20-bp microsatellite STAT3Sat in intron 6 correlated with lower STAT3 expression and with the presence of CFTR-mediated residual chloride secretion (p = 0.0031). An IL1B intragenic three-SNP haplotype 2-2-2 segregated with concordant severely-affected pairs (global permutation-corrected p = 0.0417), independently confirming a North American consortium signal in the same 4 kb sub-region. IFNGR1 intron-1 resequencing of contrasting-haplotype patients reduced the candidate variants to two positions — rs9376268 and a novel SNP-P 455 bp upstream — both differentiating concordant from discordant CF sibling pairs at OR 3.25 (raw p = 0.0113). The proposed direction is to PheWAS these signals across the CFFPR phenome paired with modern eQTL cross-reference; the open questions include whether STAT3Sat is even callable from short-read CFF WGS, whether the IFNGR1 novel SNP-P has a stable dbSNP identifier in modern variant annotation, and whether modulator-era inflammation suppression attenuates the modifier effect.

Plain-English glossary

  • Allele — one of the alternative forms of a gene or marker at a given site.
  • Candidate-gene study — pre-selecting specific genes for testing based on biological hypothesis, rather than scanning the whole genome.
  • CF basic defect — the underlying ion-transport problem caused by broken CFTR, measured in patients by sweat chloride, nasal potential difference, or intestinal current measurement on rectal biopsies.
  • Concordant pair / Discordant pair — sibling pairs from the CF Twin-and-Sibling Study with similar (concordant) or very different (discordant) disease severity despite both carrying CF.
  • CON+ / CON− — concordant mildly affected / concordant severely affected patient pairs.
  • Cytokine — a small immune-signaling protein that cells use to communicate.
  • Direct blotting electrophoresis (DBE) — high-resolution polyacrylamide-gel-based genotyping technique used to size microsatellite repeats.
  • eQTL — expression quantitative trait locus; a variant that changes how much of a nearby gene is expressed.
  • F508del homozygote — a CF patient carrying the same single CFTR mutation on both copies of the gene.
  • FAMHAP — statistical software package that supports family-based TDT, case-reference comparison on unrelated individuals, and haplotype-based testing with Monte-Carlo simulation.
  • Haplotype — the set of variants riding together on a single parental copy of a chromosome.
  • Haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping — a three-stage approach: microsatellite interrogation → intragenic SNP haplotype localization → targeted resequencing of contrasting haplotypes.
  • IFNGR1 — the alpha chain of the interferon-γ receptor.
  • IL1B — the gene encoding interleukin-1 beta, a pro-inflammatory cytokine.
  • Intestinal current measurement (ICM) — ex-vivo electrophysiology on rectal suction biopsies to measure CFTR-mediated chloride conductance.
  • Interferon-γ — a cytokine central to anti-bacterial and anti-viral immune responses.
  • MAF — minor allele frequency.
  • Microsatellite — a short DNA repeat unit (e.g., a 20-bp sequence repeated some number of times); highly polymorphic.
  • PCR-RFLP — PCR amplification followed by restriction-fragment-length polymorphism analysis; an older genotyping approach.
  • Residual chloride secretion — measurable chloride transport in CF intestinal tissue despite both CFTR copies being F508del; correlates with milder disease.
  • STAT3 — Signal Transducer and Activator of Transcription 3; a central inflammatory-signaling relay protein.
  • STAT3Sat — a 20-bp microsatellite repeat in intron 6 of STAT3.
  • TDT — transmission-disequilibrium test; family-based test for whether parents transmit one allele to the affected child more often than the other.
  • TNF-α — tumor necrosis factor alpha; another pro-inflammatory cytokine.
  • trans-acting factor — a regulatory protein (e.g., a transcription factor) whose own genetic locus is elsewhere in the genome but which acts on a cis-DNA element to modulate gene expression.

Methods to lift

  • Three-stage haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping — stage 1 informative microsatellite near/within the candidate gene; stage 2 multiple intragenic high-MAF SNPs and two-marker haplotype comparison; stage 3 targeted resequencing of contrasting-phenotype contrasting-haplotype patients.
  • FAMHAP — statistical engine for TDT, case-reference comparison, haplotype tests, with permutation-based multiple-testing correction (P_best, P_corr, P_global).
  • Transcriptome-guided candidate-gene selection — pick genes for genetic testing from genes differentially expressed in CF tissue (Affymetrix GeneChip Human Genome U133 Plus 2.0 Array; data deposited in GEO under GSE15568).
  • Concordant-vs-discordant sibling-pair contrast — design contrast that specifically targets trans-acting modifier mechanisms, because shared alleles within a pair cannot explain discordant phenotype unless paired with a trans-encoded factor.
  • Resequencing of contrasting-haplotype patients on a 7-kb fragment — manual fine-mapping by direct sequencing, which modern automated fine-mapping tools (FINEMAP, SuSiE, PolyFun) essentially imitate at scale.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage:

In the CFF whole-genome sequencing cohort (n≈5,000), perform a focused PheWAS of the STAT3 intron-6 region (including any callable STAT3Sat-tagging variation), the IL1B intragenic three-SNP haplotype rs1143643-rs3917356-rs4848306, and the IFNGR1 intron-1 haplotype rs9376269-rs1327475-rs9376268 (including any locatable SNP-P modern annotation equivalent) against the full CFFPR phenome. Pair with cross-reference to whole-blood and lung-tissue eQTL data from GTEx and CF-specific airway expression datasets; test whether the pulmonary-severity / discordance signals extend to pleiotropy across CFRD, CFLD, MI, infection-acquisition age, growth percentile, sweat chloride, and other CFFPR endpoints. Stratify by F508del-homozygous status and by modulator-era exposure.

What to verify first

  • Whether STAT3Sat — a 20-bp tandem microsatellite repeat — is reliably callable from short-read CFF WGS. Standard short-read pipelines do not typically call long microsatellite repeats accurately; a specialized caller (e.g., ExpansionHunter, GangSTR, HipSTR) may be required or the marker may simply be unavailable.
  • Whether the IFNGR1 SNP-P that Labenski et al. discovered by intron-1 resequencing in 2011 received a stable dbSNP rsID and propagated into modern variant annotation databases; if not, its 455-bp-upstream-of-rs9376268 position would need to be checked manually against the CFF call set.
  • Whether the IL1B SNPs rs1143643, rs3917356, rs4848306 are well-genotyped in the CFF variant set or imputable from the standard CF-cohort imputation reference.
  • Whether the CFF cohort has a sufficient F508del-homozygous subset to recapitulate the original substrate, and whether modulator-era stratification (Trikafta-treated vs pre-modulator) is operationally feasible from CFFPR.
  • Whether the CFFPR phenotype catalog has been mapped to phecodes or another standardized terminology to support PheWAS at scale.
  • Whether the trans-acting transcription-factor mechanism Labenski et al. propose for IFNGR1 intrapair discordance can be tested directly — i.e., whether the candidate transcription factors and their own genetic variation can be identified from cis-element-binding databases and tested for sib-pair discordance in CFF data.
  • Whether North American Levy et al. cohort IL1B genotypes can be cross-referenced for combined trans-Atlantic meta-analysis, given the convergence on the same approximately 4-kb IL1B sub-region.

Gap-map: closes / leaves open

Closes

  • Establishes STAT3Sat length variation as correlating with STAT3 mRNA levels (p = 0.0075) and with CFTR-mediated residual chloride secretion (p = 0.0031) in F508del-homozygous patients — provides a functional bridge from a microsatellite genotype to STAT3 expression to the CF basic defect.
  • Localizes a CF severity association in IL1B to a roughly 4-kb intragenic region, independently consistent with a North American consortium result (Levy et al.) on different markers in the same region.
  • Reduces the IFNGR1 intrapair-discordance signal to two specific positions (rs9376268 + a novel intron-1 SNP 455 bp upstream) within a 7-kb resequenced fragment.

Leaves open

  • Whether any of these signals replicate in a fully independent CF cohort under modern significance standards (corrected p-values are 0.03–0.04 at best — not GWAS-significant).
  • Whether the same modifiers operate in non-F508del-homozygous or non-European-ancestry CF patients.
  • Whether the IL1B and IFNGR1 risk haplotypes have pleiotropic effects on non-pulmonary CF complications (CFRD, CFLD, MI, infection acquisition) — untested in this paper.
  • Whether the modifier effects attenuate under CFTR-modulator therapy (Trikafta era), where baseline CF inflammation is substantially reduced.
  • The specific trans-acting transcription factor proposed to explain IFNGR1 intrapair discordance — an unidentified placeholder in this paper, awaiting functional follow-up.
  • The pathway-level integration of these three immune-relevant signals with each other and with CF airway-mucosal-defense GWAS signals (Polineni 2018 EHF/APIP, HLA, etc.) — none of these papers cross-reference each other directly.

ALEX Welcome to episode three. Today's CFTR-WGS paper is Labenski, Suk, Hedtfeld, Becker, Hinrichs, and Tümmler, from the Hannover Medical School group, published in 2011 in Genes and Immunity. The title is "Initial interrogation, confirmation and fine mapping of modifying genes: STAT3, IL1B and IFNGR1 determine cystic fibrosis disease manifestation."

JORDAN An older, pre-GWAS paper from the European CF Twin and Sibling Study era. What's the angle?

ALEX The angle is that this is a candidate-gene paper, not a GWAS — meaning the authors deliberately chose three immune-related genes ahead of time and tested those, instead of scanning the whole genome. The interesting feature is that they describe a step-by-step approach for taking a candidate gene from suspicion to a specific causal variant — a method they call haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping. The findings themselves are smaller-scale than a modern modifier paper, but the approach is worth understanding because some of what they wrestled with reappears in modern WGS-era analyses.

JORDAN Before we go in, can you define some things? STAT3, IL1B, IFNGR1 — what do those do?

ALEX Sure. STAT3 stands for Signal Transducer and Activator of Transcription 3 — it's a protein that sits in the cytoplasm of cells until a cytokine — a small immune-signaling molecule — binds a receptor on the cell's surface. The signal travels down to STAT3, which then moves to the nucleus and turns specific genes on or off. It is a central relay in inflammation. IL1B encodes interleukin-1 beta, one of the most potent pro-inflammatory cytokines the body makes. IFNGR1 encodes the alpha chain of the receptor for interferon gamma — interferon gamma is another cytokine, central to anti-bacterial and anti-viral immune responses, and IFNGR1 is the receiver on cells that listens for it. Beyond those three, you'll hear: CF basic defect, which means the underlying ion-transport problem caused by broken CFTR, measured in living patients by sweat chloride testing, nasal potential difference, or intestinal current measurement on rectal biopsies. Residual chloride secretion is when an F508del-homozygous patient — who in theory has no working CFTR — actually retains a measurable amount of chloride transport in their gut tissue; that residual function correlates with milder disease.

JORDAN And the study design vocabulary?

ALEX Right. TDT is the transmission-disequilibrium test — a family-based test where you check whether parents transmit one allele to their affected child more often than the other; it's protective against population stratification confounding. A microsatellite is a short DNA repeat unit, like a 20-base-pair sequence repeated some number of times; the number of repeats varies between individuals, making it a highly informative marker for genotyping. A haplotype, as we discussed yesterday, is the set of variants riding together on a single parental copy of a chromosome. Concordant and discordant patient pairs are sibling pairs from the European CF Twin and Sibling Study: concordant means the two siblings have similar disease severity, discordant means they have very different severity despite carrying the same CFTR mutation. CON-plus is concordant mildly affected; CON-minus is concordant severely affected.

ALEX Background and motivation. The European CF Twin and Sibling Study had been a major engine for CF modifier research in the 2000s — Tümmler and colleagues had assembled a panel of 466 twin-and-sibling pairs and had already published the broader candidate-gene scan in 2010. That earlier paper reported microsatellite-marker associations with CF severity for many genes — but most associations were initial hits without follow-up fine-mapping to specific causal variants. The motivating question for this 2011 paper is: take three of those candidate genes that look most promising — STAT3, IL1B, IFNGR1 — and walk them through a structured pipeline to localize, when possible, an actual specific sequence variant doing the modifying.

JORDAN Why those three?

ALEX The authors selected them from transcriptome data — gene expression measurements — comparing F508del-homozygous CF patients with different residual chloride secretion levels against non-CF controls. STAT3 was upregulated in CF patients who lacked residual function. IL1B and IFNGR1 were both upregulated in CF patients overall versus non-CF controls. Picking based on expression is a way to bias toward genes that are actually doing something in CF tissue, rather than just genes the literature has worked on.

ALEX Methods. The study population was 101 CF families, with 85 belonging to the larger twin-and-sibling-study panel. All patients are F508del-homozygous — that is, both copies of the CFTR gene carry the same single deletion. They are stratified into informative subgroups: extreme mildly-affected concordant pairs, extreme severely-affected concordant pairs, discordant pairs where the two siblings differ markedly, and patients sorted by whether they have residual chloride secretion in intestinal current measurement of rectal biopsies. Genotyping was a mix of older techniques: PCR amplification followed by either restriction-fragment-length polymorphism analysis — meaning the PCR product is cut with an enzyme whose cut depends on which allele is present — or direct-blotting electrophoresis for microsatellites. The statistical engine is the FAMHAP software package, which supports family-based TDT, case-reference comparison on unrelated individuals, haplotype-based testing, and Monte Carlo simulation for empirical p-values, with multiple-testing correction by permutation. The three-stage pipeline they call haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping works as follows. Stage one: genotype an informative microsatellite near or within the candidate gene; compare allele distributions between cases and references. Stage two, if stage one is positive: genotype several intragenic SNPs with high minor allele frequency, and look at two-marker and longer-marker haplotypes; the idea is that older, common SNPs define larger ancestral haplotype blocks, while recent functional variants will sit on a specific subset of those blocks. Stage three, if a specific haplotype block is implicated: select patients with contrasting haplotypes and contrasting phenotypes, then sequence the implicated DNA fragment in those patients to identify all sequence differences. This is logically what large-scale fine-mapping pipelines like FINEMAP and SuSiE try to automate today, just done at small scale by hand.

JORDAN Pushback?

ALEX The biggest design concern is that this is a candidate-gene study with a small sample — 101 families. Even after permutation correction for multiple testing of markers within a single gene, the overall study tested three genes with multiple markers each, and the p-values that survive correction are around 0.03 to 0.04 at best. By modern GWAS standards, which use a 5-times-10-to-the-minus-8 genome-wide significance threshold, none of these would clear the bar. The authors themselves are clear that they are interrogating and confirming candidate associations, not establishing them at GWAS-level evidence. The other concern is the candidate-gene-from-expression bias: picking genes that are upregulated in CF and then testing them for genetic association with CF severity creates a circular flavor that the authors acknowledge for STAT3 specifically. Replication in a fully independent cohort is what would convert any of these to settled findings.

ALEX Results. For STAT3, the intragenic microsatellite STAT3Sat — a 20-base-pair tandem repeat in intron 6 of STAT3 — showed two correlated findings. First, rare large alleles at STAT3Sat accumulated in patients who retained CFTR-mediated residual chloride secretion, with a raw p-value of 0.0031 by permutation analysis of intestinal current measurement phenotype. Second, those large alleles correlated with lower STAT3 mRNA expression measured by Affymetrix probe set 208992_s_at — p-value 0.0075 by Mann-Whitney U test on 13 F508del-homozygous patients. The interpretation: longer STAT3Sat alleles in intron 6 reduce STAT3 expression, and lower STAT3 expression is associated with retained residual chloride function. That's consistent with prior work showing that cytokines like TNF-alpha and interferon-gamma, which signal through STAT3, can downregulate CFTR function.

ALEX For IL1B, three intragenic SNPs — rs1143643, rs3917356, rs4848306 — were genotyped after an initial microsatellite signal. The authors compared concordant mildly versus concordant severely affected patient pairs. SNPs rs3917356 and rs4848306 each showed raw p-values around 0.025 to 0.03 for that contrast; rs1143643 showed a TDT signal at raw p equals 0.055. A three-SNP haplotype 2-2-2 was over-represented in concordant severely affected patients and under-transmitted from parents — internally consistent, designating it as a risk haplotype, with global permutation-corrected p equals 0.0417 for the case-reference contrast. The authors also note that a North American consortium paper from Levy and colleagues had independently implicated rs1143634 and rs1143639 — two different IL1B SNPs sitting in the same approximately 4-kilobase intragenic region — as CF severity modifiers, so two independent studies converge on the same IL1B sub-region.

ALEX For IFNGR1, this is where the haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping went all the way through stage three. Nine SNPs were typed across IFNGR1. Concordant versus discordant patient pairs differed significantly at rs1327475, and at the two-marker haplotype rs9376269-rs1327475 with raw p equals 0.0113 — corrected for 10-marker multiple testing, p equals 0.0358. The authors then sequenced a 7-kilobase fragment of IFNGR1 intron 1 in patients with contrasting haplotypes and contrasting phenotypes. The concordant and discordant chromosomes differed at exactly two positions across that 7 kilobases — rs9376268 and a novel SNP located 455 base pairs upstream of it, which they called SNP-P. The haplotype 1-2-2 at rs9376269-rs1327475-rs9376268 is present on 78.6 percent of discordant chromosomes versus 53.1 percent of concordant chromosomes — odds ratio about 3.25. The biological interpretation the authors propose: this is an intron-1 regulatory region, and the discordance-associated haplotype creates a binding site for a transcription factor whose own variation across siblings then introduces the phenotypic discordance — a trans-acting mechanism layered on a cis-marked haplotype.

ALEX Beat six, proposed direction. One specific concrete idea this paper opens up for Runyu: a PheWAS of the STAT3, IL1B, and IFNGR1 modifier signals against the CFFPR phenome, with explicit testing of cis-acting expression effects in airway and intestinal tissues where data are available. Plain English: take the three immune-relevant variants this paper localizes, look up their effects on gene expression in modern eQTL atlases like GTEx, and then ask whether the same variants associate with non-pulmonary CF complications — not just severity in general, but specific endpoints like CFRD, CFLD, MI, infection-acquisition age, and growth percentile. Formal one-sentence version: in the CFF WGS cohort, perform a focused PheWAS of the STAT3Sat-tagged region, the IL1B three-SNP risk haplotype, and the IFNGR1 intron-1 rs9376268-plus-SNP-P haplotype against the full CFFPR phenome, paired with cross-reference to whole-blood and lung-tissue eQTL data from GTEx and from CF-specific airway expression datasets, testing whether the pulmonary-severity signals extend to pleiotropy across other CF complications.

JORDAN Beat seven — what's the gap, conditional on that proposal?

ALEX What this paper closes: it puts three immune-pathway candidate genes onto the CF modifier map with permutation-corrected support for IL1B and IFNGR1 at the haplotype level, and a functional expression mechanism story for STAT3. It also gives Runyu a worked example of the manual fine-mapping logic that modern automated tools attempt to replicate. What it leaves open. First, we'd need to verify which of these specific markers are well-genotyped or imputed in the CFF WGS variant set — STAT3Sat is a microsatellite repeat, not a SNP, and standard short-read WGS pipelines do not call long microsatellite repeats reliably; the CF cohort may not have STAT3Sat genotypes at all without special calling. Second, the IFNGR1 SNP-P that the authors discovered by intron-1 resequencing in 2011 may or may not have been deposited in dbSNP under a stable rsID and may not be present in modern variant annotation databases — we'd need to check whether the CFF call set captures it. Third, the original signals were detected against an F508del-homozygous-only substrate; whether the same haplotypes carry the same effect in compound-heterozygous or non-European-ancestry CF patients is untested, and the CFF cohort spans both. Fourth, this paper predates the modulator era — Trikafta, Kalydeco, Symdeko, Orkambi all postdate it — and a STAT3/IL1B/IFNGR1 modifier effect that works through baseline inflammation may attenuate when CFTR function is pharmacologically rescued; stratifying CFFPR by modulator-era exposure is necessary before generalizing. Fifth, no replication beyond the Levy IL1B convergence is available; a CFFPR-cohort test functions partly as a delayed replication, but only if the same markers can be reliably interrogated. Sixth, the haplotype-guided hierarchical fine mapping logic is sound but assumes pure cis-acting variation — the trans-acting transcription-factor mechanism the authors propose for IFNGR1 intrapair discordance would require independent functional follow-up, not just association statistics.
