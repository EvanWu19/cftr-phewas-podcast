Blurb

Zheutlin and colleagues (Am J Psychiatry, 2019) ran the first cross-site phenome-wide association study of a psychiatric polygenic risk score, using 106,160 patients across Geisinger, Mount Sinai, Partners, and Vanderbilt. A PGC-trained schizophrenia PRS replicated the phecode 295.1 association at OR=1.55 per SD (p=4.48×10⁻¹⁶) — but with top-vs-bottom-decile odds ratios of 3.3-4.6, much attenuated relative to the 7.8–20.3-fold range PGC reported in curated research cohorts. Pleiotropy hit 29 phecodes spanning psychiatric, neurological, and metabolic categories; four sensitivity analyses (adjusting for and excluding schizophrenia diagnosis and antipsychotic prescriptions) separated biological pleiotropy (anxiety, mood, substance use, obesity, urinary, malaise) from clinical mediation (suicidal behaviour, personality, memory loss, synovitis). Proposed direction for CFTR PheWAS: replicate the multi-site EHR-PheWAS template using a CFTR-residual-function tier score instead of a common-variant PRS, with modulator exposure as the analogue of antipsychotic-mediation sensitivity. Open: does CFFPR phenotype-to-phecode mapping exist, and is the CFF WGS n≈5,000 sufficient at the ≥100-cases-per-phecode threshold?

Plain-English glossary

  • AUC / AUROC — area under the receiver operating characteristic curve, a 0.5-to-1.0 metric of how well a score discriminates cases from controls. 0.5 = chance, 1.0 = perfect.
  • Bayesian PRS / PRS-CS — a continuous-shrinkage Bayesian polygenic score method that keeps essentially all SNPs but shrinks each weight using a probabilistic LD model.
  • Bonferroni correction — multiple-testing p-value adjustment: divide alpha by the number of tests.
  • BioVU — Vanderbilt University Medical Center's DNA biobank linked to its EHR.
  • eMERGE — Electronic Medical Records and Genomics Network; NIH-funded multi-site EHR-plus-DNA consortium. PsycheMERGE is its psychiatric arm.
  • EHR — electronic health record.
  • GWAS — genome-wide association study; SNP-by-SNP regression against a phenotype.
  • ICD-9 / ICD-10 — International Classification of Diseases versions 9 and 10; standard billing codes.
  • LD / linkage disequilibrium — tendency of nearby SNPs to be correlated because they ride together on a haplotype block.
  • LD-pruned PRS / PRSice — simple polygenic-score method that keeps one SNP per LD cluster (r²>0.1 within 250 kb) at the lowest p-value, then sums weights.
  • MAF — minor allele frequency; how common the rarer of two alleles is in the cohort.
  • OR — odds ratio.
  • PGC — Psychiatric Genomics Consortium; produces large psychiatric GWAS summary statistics.
  • Phecode — a curated bucket grouping dozens of ICD codes into one biologically meaningful disease category. ~1,860 phecodes in the standard scheme.
  • PheWAS — phenome-wide association study; sweep one exposure (genotype, PRS, lab value) across every phecode.
  • Pleiotropy (biological vs mediated) — biological = the same genetic factor independently affects multiple traits; mediated = the genetic factor causes trait A, and trait A or its treatment causes trait B.
  • Principal components — orthogonal axes capturing the major sources of variation in a genotype matrix; used as covariates to adjust for population structure.
  • PRS — polygenic risk score; per-individual weighted sum of risk alleles using GWAS effect-size weights.
  • SNP — single-nucleotide polymorphism; a single-letter DNA variant.

Methods to lift

  • PRSice — LD-pruned polygenic-score tool that keeps the smallest-p SNP per LD cluster (r²>0.1, 250 kb window) and sums log-OR-weighted dosages.
  • PRS-CS — Bayesian continuous-shrinkage PRS method that infers posterior SNP weights using GWAS summary stats and an external LD reference (1000 Genomes EUR, N=503).
  • PheWAS R package — runs per-phecode logistic regression with covariates and meta-analyses via fixed-effect inverse-variance weighting.
  • metafor R package — fixed-effect inverse-variance meta-analysis of log-odds-ratios across sites.
  • Phecode-as-case rule — ≥2 ICD codes mapping to the phecode = case; 0 = control; 1-code excluded. Phecodes with <100 cases excluded.
  • PLINK 1.9 — genotype QC, score computation.
  • Multi-site meta-analysis with site-as-stratum — fit logistic at each site separately, meta-analyse log-ORs; allows per-site QC pipelines to differ without breaking the analysis.
  • Biological-vs-mediated pleiotropy sensitivity quartet — four parallel PheWAS reruns: (1) add disease-itself covariate, (2) add primary-treatment covariate, (3) exclude disease cases, (4) exclude all primary-treatment patients. Associations that survive all four look biological; those that attenuate or flip look mediated.

Proposed direction (conditional)

Conditional on inspection of CFF WGS variant calls and registry phenotype linkage: replicate the Zheutlin multi-site EHR-PheWAS architecture using the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation Patient Registry as a single-site analogue of the four eMERGE sites, with a CFTR-residual-function score (derived from genotype-mapped CFTR mutation class I–VI, or alternatively from CFTR2-curated functional annotations) substituting for the common-variant PRS. Sweep across every phecode for which the CFFPR yields ≥100 cases, with covariates for age, sex, principal components, and modulator exposure. Apply the four-arm sensitivity-analysis design (add CFTR-modulator covariate; exclude modulator-treated person-time; add severe-CFTR-class covariate; exclude class-I/II homozygous person-time) to separate primary-CFTR-driven pleiotropy from modulator-mediated phenotypic patterns.

What to verify first

  • Whether CFFPR phenotypes can be mapped to phecodes — CFFPR uses bespoke registry fields rather than a direct ICD-9/10 dump; a phecode mapping must exist or be built before any sweep is operational.
  • How many CFFPR phecodes meet the ≥100-cases threshold; saturation may be limiting outside the obvious pulmonary/pancreatic/hepatobiliary set.
  • Whether CFF WGS at n≈5,000 (the smallest Zheutlin site was already 2× that) gives any detectable signal at modulator-stratified strata — power calc before analytic commit.
  • How CFTR mutation class is mapped — published curated tables exist for the common variants, but novel rare CFTR missense without prior characterisation may be unassignable.
  • Ancestry-stratum sample sizes for African-ancestry and Hispanic CF subjects in the WGS cohort.
  • Modulator-era follow-up depth — what fraction of WGS-cohort person-time is pre- vs post-modulator, and is post-modulator depth sufficient for the sensitivity-analysis stripping.
  • Whether CFFPR captures the non-CF psychiatric, neurological, metabolic, and urinary phenotypes Zheutlin found pleiotropic — CFFPR is CF-focused and may not depth-record these.

ALEX Welcome back. Today's first paper is Zheutlin and colleagues, 2019, in the American Journal of Psychiatry, titled "Penetrance and Pleiotropy of Polygenic Risk Scores for Schizophrenia in 106,160 Patients Across Four Health Care Systems." It's the inaugural report from the PsycheMERGE consortium, and it's the largest test to date of a psychiatric polygenic risk score against electronic-health-record phenotypes — which is exactly the design pattern we keep circling back to for a CFTR project.

JORDAN Hold on Alex, before we step in — I want to make sure I'm not pretending. Spell out the acronyms first. What's a polygenic risk score, what's a PheWAS, what's a phecode, what's an EHR, what's a biobank?

ALEX Yes — let's slow down. A polygenic risk score, PRS, is a single number you compute for one person that summarises how many disease-risk alleles they carry across many places in the genome, each weighted by how strongly that allele was associated with the disease in a big previous study. For schizophrenia, that big previous study comes from the Psychiatric Genomics Consortium, PGC, which is an international research collaboration that ran a genome-wide association study, a GWAS, of about a hundred thousand schizophrenia cases versus controls. A GWAS is the experiment that looks at millions of common variants — single-letter DNA changes called SNPs, pronounced "snips" — and asks, for each one, does carrying this letter make the disease more or less likely? The output is a giant table: SNP, allele, effect size, p-value. That table is the "summary statistics," and a PRS recipe takes those weights, looks up your genotype at each SNP, multiplies, and sums.

JORDAN OK so PRS equals "personal-disease-tally based on someone else's GWAS."

ALEX Exactly. Then PheWAS. PRS is the score for one person; phenome-wide association study, PheWAS, asks the opposite question. Take that score and run it against every disease label in someone's medical record, all at once, and see which lines up. The "phenome" is the whole pile of conditions a person has ever been diagnosed with. To make the labels tractable, researchers don't use raw billing codes — they use phecodes. A phecode is a curated bucket that groups dozens of similar ICD-9 and ICD-10 billing codes into one biologically meaningful disease category. ICD codes are the international standard codes hospitals use to bill insurance. ICD-9 was older, ICD-10 is current, and codes look like "295.1, disorganized type schizophrenia." There are about eighteen hundred phecodes in the standard scheme. EHR just means electronic health record — your chart, digital. And a biobank, in this paper's sense, is a hospital programme where patients consent to having their leftover blood sent off for DNA extraction and genotyping, and that DNA gets linked back to their chart. Four big U.S. systems run them: Geisinger, Mount Sinai, Partners, and Vanderbilt's BioVU.

JORDAN Got it. So the whole study is: build a schizophrenia PRS, run it against every phecode in four EHRs, see what associates. Why does this paper matter for a CFTR question?

ALEX Because they're trying to bridge the gap between an idealised research cohort, where every case is interviewed by a trained psychiatrist, and a real hospital system, where the cases are just whoever happened to get an ICD code for schizophrenia. That gap is the same gap we'd face in CF. The Cystic Fibrosis Foundation Patient Registry, the CFFPR, is built from clinical encounters at CF care centres, with phenotypes that are rich but were not collected for a research protocol. So the methodological question — how much penetrance and pleiotropy can a PRS-style scan recover from EHR-style phenotypes — is directly transferable.

JORDAN So what was actually unknown going in?

ALEX Two specific things. First, how well does a schizophrenia PRS, trained on the PGC research cohort, predict schizophrenia diagnoses in a real-world health system where the cases are messier? Earlier PGC papers had reported eye-popping numbers — seven-point-eight to twenty-point-three-fold higher odds of schizophrenia in the top PRS decile versus the bottom. But those numbers came from extreme-sampled research cohorts. The clinical question is what happens when you stop cherry-picking. Second, pleiotropy. Pleiotropy is when one genetic factor affects multiple traits. Schizophrenia genetically correlates with depression, bipolar, anxiety, substance use — but how broadly does the PRS reach when you scan an entire phenome of eighteen hundred conditions? And critically, when associations show up — say, schizophrenia PRS associating with diabetes — is that because the same genes drive both biologically, what they call biological pleiotropy, or is it because schizophrenia leads to antipsychotic prescriptions which cause weight gain which causes diabetes, what they call mediated pleiotropy?

JORDAN That distinction is the whole game. How do they tackle it?

ALEX One hundred six thousand one hundred sixty patients, all of European ancestry — Geisinger forty-four thousand four hundred thirty-six, Mount Sinai ninety-five hundred sixty-nine, Partners eighteen thousand four hundred sixty-one, Vanderbilt thirty-three thousand six hundred ninety-four. Standard quality control at each site, kinship coefficient cutoff zero-point-two to drop related pairs, ten principal components for ancestry. They computed the PRS two ways. The simple way is LD-pruning with a tool called PRSice — LD stands for linkage disequilibrium, the tendency of nearby SNPs to ride together because they sit on the same haplotype block — and what pruning does is keep only one SNP from each tight LD cluster, the one with the smallest p-value, so you don't double-count the same signal. The Bayesian way uses a tool called PRS-CS — continuous shrinkage — which keeps essentially all SNPs but shrinks each weight using a probability model that accounts for the LD structure directly. PRS-CS is generally more accurate. They z-scored both within each site, then ran a phecode PheWAS using the PheWAS R package, with covariates: ten principal components, median age, sex, genotyping platform, batch. Bonferroni correction at p equals three-point-seven times ten to the minus five for the eighteen hundred fifty-nine phecodes that had at least a hundred cases. Then — and this is the methodologically nice move — four follow-up sensitivity PheWASs to dissect biological versus mediated pleiotropy: add a covariate for schizophrenia diagnosis; add a covariate for any antipsychotic prescription; remove all schizophrenia cases; remove all patients with antipsychotic history. If an association survives those, it's probably biological. If it drops, it's probably mediated.

JORDAN That's elegant. Any design choice that bugs you, Alex?

ALEX One. They restricted everything to European ancestry because PRSes trained on European GWASes transfer poorly to other ancestries — which is true and well-documented — but it means we cannot read this paper as evidence about PRS performance in non-European populations. And the CFFPR is roughly nine in ten white, but the African-ancestry and Hispanic strata exist and would be small but real.

JORDAN Fair. What did they find?

ALEX The PRS robustly associated with the schizophrenia phecode 295.1 — odds ratio one-point-five-five per standard deviation increase, ninety-five-percent confidence interval one-point-four to one-point-seven, p equals four-point-five times ten to the minus sixteen. Top decile versus rest: odds ratio one-point-nine for LD-pruned, two-point-three for Bayesian. Top decile versus bottom decile: three-point-three LD-pruned, four-point-six Bayesian. Now compare those to the original PGC numbers of seven-point-eight to twenty-point-three. The EHR-derived effect sizes are about a quarter to a half of what the curated research cohorts reported. Authors are explicit: this is the real-world number. When the cases are whoever has two ICD codes for schizophrenia and the controls are everyone else, the PRS does the right thing, but it does it less dramatically than the research cohort headline suggests.

JORDAN And the pleiotropy?

ALEX Twenty-nine phenotypes hit the Bonferroni threshold in the cross-site meta-analysis. Positively: schizophrenia of course, plus bipolar, depression, substance use, anxiety, personality disorders, suicidal behaviour, neurological disorders, memory loss, viral hepatitis, urinary syndromes, malaise and fatigue. Negatively: obesity, morbid obesity, and synovitis. The four sensitivity analyses then sort these into biological versus mediated. The psychiatric and substance-use and obesity associations mostly survive — those look biological. The personality-disorder, suicidal-behaviour, neurological, and memory-loss associations attenuate or flip — those look mediated through the schizophrenia diagnosis or the antipsychotic prescription itself. The AUC, area under the receiver-operating-curve, ranged zero-point-six-oh to zero-point-seven-one across sites — modest, but in the same ballpark as research-cohort PRSes for breast cancer or inflammatory bowel disease. So they answered both questions: penetrance is attenuated but real, and pleiotropy is broad but partially mediated.

JORDAN OK. Translate this into one specific thing Runyu could try.

ALEX Here's one possible direction. The Zheutlin design is essentially a recipe: take a quantitative genetic score, run it against every phecode, then layer sensitivity analyses to separate genuine pleiotropy from clinical-mediation. For CFTR, the analogous score is not a polygenic risk score — CFTR is a single Mendelian gene — but a CFTR-functional-class score, where each patient's CFTR genotype gets mapped to a residual-function tier using the standard CF mutation classes one through six, or alternatively using CFTR2-curated functional annotations. Plain English: give each CF patient a CFTR-severity number based on their two CFTR alleles, then sweep that number across every phecode in the CFFPR phenome and see what lights up beyond the pulmonary, pancreatic, and hepatobiliary phenotypes everyone already expects, with sensitivity layers stripping out modulator effects, ivacaftor and elexacaftor-tezacaftor-ivacaftor prescriptions, the way Zheutlin stripped out antipsychotic effects. Formal version: in the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation Patient Registry linked to Cystic Fibrosis Genome Project whole-genome sequence data, build a CFTR-residual-function score from genotype-derived mutation class, run a phecode-level PheWAS across the CFFPR phenome, and apply Zheutlin-style sensitivity analyses adjusting for and stratifying by CFTR-modulator exposure to separate primary-genetic pleiotropy from modulator-mediated phenotypic patterns.

JORDAN That's concrete. What does this paper close, and what does it leave wide open for that direction?

ALEX What it closes: the question of whether EHR-derived phenome scans can recover a real genetic signal at all is now answered — yes, attenuated but real. The four-site cross-validation design with site-as-stratum is portable. The biological-versus-mediated sensitivity-analysis design is portable. What it leaves open for CFTR specifically: first, Zheutlin used common-variant PRS, where you sum across millions of SNPs each contributing a tiny effect. CFTR is a single-gene Mendelian disorder where two specific variants drive the phenotype — the maths and the interpretation will be different, and the analogue of "PRS decile" is "CFTR mutation class," which has discrete tiers rather than a continuous distribution. Second, the n equals one hundred six thousand here is hundreds of times what we'd have in CFF — the smallest individual site, Mount Sinai, was already ninety-five hundred, twice the entire CFF WGS cohort. So a stripped-down CFTR PheWAS will be much less powered, and we'd need to verify whether the CFFPR has enough non-pulmonary phecode counts above the hundred-case threshold the paper used. Third, Zheutlin restricted to European ancestry. We'd need to check what to do for the African-ancestry and Hispanic strata in CFF given small sub-cohort sizes. Fourth — and this is the gating question — we'd need to verify that CFFPR phenotypes can be mapped to phecodes at all, since CF clinical phenotypes use bespoke registry fields, not direct ICD-9/10 dumps. That mapping has to exist or be built before any of this is operational. Fifth, the modulator-era stratification — we'd want to check what fraction of the CFF WGS cohort has post-modulator follow-up, since the sensitivity analyses depend on having both modulator-exposed and modulator-naive person-time to compare.
