Blurb

The first All of Us Research Program genomic-data release paper describes 245,388 clinical-grade whole-genome sequences released through the cloud-based Researcher Workbench under a "data passport" access model. AoU is the most ancestrally diverse large-scale WGS cohort to date — 77% of participants from communities historically under-represented in biomedical research and 51.1% non-European ancestry — and it identified more than one billion variants, including 275 million previously unreported. For the CFTR PheWAS proposal, this paper functions as a methods source (ancestry classifier, joint calling, cross-biobank replication via PGRM) and as the obvious replication cohort for any CFF-derived modifier finding.

Methods to lift

  • gnomAD-aligned ancestry classifier — random-forest-style classifier trained on a 16-PC PCA space derived from a 3,202-sample HGDP + 1000 Genomes reference panel and 151,159 autosomal SNPs; project new samples into the trained PC space and assign categorical ancestry labels (AFR/AMR/EAS/EUR/SAS/MID). Same labels as gnomAD enables direct allele-frequency cross-tabulation.
  • Joint-call QC framework from gnomAD v3.1 — flag samples on eight outlier metrics; filter on AAscore-style logistic regression quality.
  • Validation against Genome in a Bottle reference samples using Krusche 2019 best practices — SNV sensitivity >98.7%, precision >99.9%; indel sensitivity >97%, precision >99.6%.
  • PCA-on-unrelated-then-project pattern — compute PCA on the maximal independent set, project relatives into the same space rather than letting kinship warp the PCs.
  • Maximal independent set construction for unrelated-individual analyses (231,442 of 245,388 retained at 2nd-degree-or-closer cutoff).
  • PGRM (Bastarache 2023) as a cross-biobank replication benchmark — 4,947 published variant–phenotype associations mapped to phecodes, evaluated as ancestry-stratified powered replication rates.
  • Genomic Variant Store (GVS) — cloud-native variant storage where the VCF is rendered on demand from a queryable schema rather than being the primary store. Likely overkill for the CFF cohort but worth citing as the biobank-scale joint-calling benchmark.
  • Illumina Nirvana for functional consequence annotation on canonical ENSEMBL transcripts.

Gap-map note

AoU at 245,388 WGS is roughly 50× the size of the CFF WGS cohort (~5K) and 51% non-European versus the CFF cohort's predominantly European composition; this argues for an explicit two-stage proposal design — discover modifier signals in the CFF cohort using rich registry-derived longitudinal phenotypes (FEV1 trajectories, exacerbation counts, CFRD onset) where CFF has unique strengths, then replicate index variants in AoU using EHR-derived CF and CF-adjacent phecodes where AoU's scale and diversity dominate. This framing turns the saturation and power risks into a coherent narrative rather than two separate weaknesses.
