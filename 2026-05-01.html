Blurb

Two contemporary UK Biobank exome papers (Backman 2021, n=454,787 with REGENIE; Wang 2021, n=281,104 with collapsing analyses) frame the methods envelope for what large-scale rare-variant PheWAS work looks like, and what it won't transfer to a 5K cohort. We then revisit Blackman 2013, the foundational CF Gene Modifier Consortium GWAS that established SLC26A9 plus four type-2-diabetes candidate loci as CFRD modifiers — the direct ancestor to Aksit 2020 (covered yesterday). The take-home: Blackman demonstrates direct evidence of pleiotropy at an established CF modifier locus (SLC26A9 increases CFRD risk but decreases T2D risk in the general population), which is exactly the kind of cross-system effect the registry-anchored CFTR PheWAS is positioned to scan for. The single sentence to drop into the concept note's limitations paragraph: we are powered for higher-effect rare-variant gene-burden signals at N≈5K, not for the modest 1.2–1.4 per-allele hazard ratios Blackman detected at common modifier SNPs; common-variant follow-up will go through external biobank portals.

Gap-map after today

  • Gap 1 (phenome-wide scans against CF modifier loci): Hardened. Blackman 2013 provides direct evidence of pleiotropy at SLC26A9 — opposite-direction effects on CFRD vs. T2D in the general population — and the closely linked GI-vs-lung dissociation (P=0.5 for CF lung function vs P=9.8×10⁻¹⁰ for CFRD onset). The registry-anchored PheWAS is positioned to systematize what Blackman demonstrated for one phenotype pair.
  • Gap 4 (non-pulmonary comorbidities): Reinforced. CFRD is the non-pulmonary anchor in the modifier literature; everything else (mental health, bone, reproductive) remains untested at modifier loci.
  • Gap 5 (sex/ancestry stratification): Confirmed open and inherited from the field — three biobank papers and one CF GWAS, all four built on ≥92% European samples. Plan ancestry-stratified secondary analyses; lookups against AZ-PheWAS portal (281K) and All of Us are mandatory for any CFF-cohort hit.
  • Methods envelope sharper: lift collapsing-analysis design from Wang 2021; pLOF-plus-deleterious-missense burden composition from Backman 2021 (REGENIE); proportional-hazards age-of-onset modeling from Blackman 2013 for any CFRD-like time-dependent phenotype.

JORDAN Per-participant, what does that look like?

JORDAN 214 pLOFs per person sounds like a lot until you think about how much of the genome that has to spread across.

ALEX Right. The point is most genes still see only a handful of carriers per gene per cohort, even at 454,787. They make this explicit later — they project that even at five million exomes, only 8,376 genes will have at least 500 heterozygous pLOF carriers. So the mantra here is: rare-variant gene tests scale much faster than your common-variant catalogue, but per-gene power saturates slowly.

JORDAN Okay, so what did they actually run?

ALEX REGENIE. They tested 3,994 traits — 3,702 binary with at least 100 cases, and 292 quantitative across anthropometry, biochemistry, hematology. They restricted primary discovery to the 430,998 European participants, about 95 percent of the cohort. Single-variant tests plus gene-level burden tests on rare pLOF and deleterious missense, with MAF thresholds up to one percent.

JORDAN REGENIE — remind the listener.

ALEX REGENIE is the whole-genome regression approach Mbatchou and Marchini published in 2021. Step one fits a leave-one-chromosome-out ridge regression on common variants to capture polygenic structure and relatedness, step two does fast Firth approximation for the binary outcomes. The big advantage over SAIGE here is computational, particularly for the case where you have thousands of phenotypes against half a million samples. They report no substantial inflation from population structure or unmodelled relatedness.

JORDAN Total tests?

ALEX Roughly 2.3 billion. Bonferroni threshold of P less than or equal to 2.18 times ten to the minus eleven. At that threshold they found 8,865 significant associations, involving 564 genes, 492 traits, and 2,283 unique gene-trait pairs.

JORDAN That is the number to remember. Five hundred and sixty-four genes.

ALEX And 91 percent of those rare-variant signals remained significant after conditioning on common-variant GWAS sentinels nearby. So they are largely independent of the GWAS layer. That is the value proposition of exome sequencing in one number.

JORDAN Replication?

ALEX DiscovEHR, the Geisinger cohort. n equals 133,370 European-ancestry exomes. When they restricted to the 212 associations that had at least 80 percent power for replication, the rate jumped to 81 percent, 172 of 212. That is the number Runyu should hold onto.

JORDAN Eighty-one percent power-conditioned replication. Fine. But — wait, does this scale to 5K samples?

ALEX It does not. That is the answer. Most of these signals were enabled by the half-million sample size. Their rare-variant burden tests with MAF up to one percent worked because they had thousands of carriers per gene at the most permissive thresholds. At 5,000 samples — our CFTR PheWAS sample size — even a one percent MAF variant is expected to produce only fifty heterozygous carriers, give or take.

JORDAN So Backman 2021 is essentially a power demonstration we cannot match.
